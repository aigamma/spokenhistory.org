This is a joint Civil Rights History Project interview with Lucius Holloway Sr. and his wife, Emma Kate Holloway, conducted by Hasan Kwame Jeffries on March 9, 2013, in Albany, Georgia, on the campus of Albany State University. Both were born in Terrell County, Georgia, where the seat is Dawson: Lucius in 1932 to sharecropper parents, and Emma Kate into a farming family for whom she picked cotton as her father's best hand. Lucius enlisted in the military in June 1952 to help support his large family, and after his discharge in June 1955 he met Emma Kate, who was still in high school. They married in June 1957, with Lucius agreeing to pay for her schooling, and raised four children.

The heart of the interview is the cost their family paid for Lucius's voter-registration work with the SCLC and the local NAACP. Emma Kate trained as a beautician at Albany Tech and built a business, but their customers fell away after white people warned the community not to associate with the Holloways. She recounts the reprisals at home: a gas line cut in the winter of about 1959 with a newborn in her arms, her husband fired from the post office on a pretext, and a night the family expected to die, when a Black cook warned that white men planned to kill Lucius. He went to the church meeting anyway with his wife and four children, and the family survived only because a friend swapped cars so a decoy drew off the armed men waiting by the creek. A week or two later a man came to the door to kill Lucius but found he could not do it and left town.

Lucius recounts being made first vice president of the Terrell County NAACP in 1955, a post he still held at the time of the interview, and how lawsuits he helped file opened nearly every local office to Black candidates. He served four years on the City Council and more than seventeen years on the Board of County Commissioners, and tells of a newspaper article naming him the last man standing among four local organizers, two of whom were killed. Both close with a message to younger generations: Lucius asks them to trust God and stay focused, and Emma Kate urges them to respect their elders and themselves, get an education, and stay in school.
