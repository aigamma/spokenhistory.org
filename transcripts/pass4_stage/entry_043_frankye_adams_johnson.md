**Net-new ground-truth corpus candidates:**

- Stokely Carmichael: SNCC chairman 1966-67, foundational SNCC field organizer who came to Greenwood MS during Freedom Summer 1964 and later coined "Black Power" — Adams-Johnson is a canonical first-person witness to his Greenwood organizing presence; the corpus likely already has SNCC entry but a standalone Carmichael entry would unify the many cross-references.
- Colia Liddell Lafayette Clark: Jackson NAACP youth advisor 1961-62, Tougaloo student leader, North Jackson Youth Council co-founder, married Bernard Lafayette (SNCC Freedom Rider and Selma project director). Adams-Johnson references her as one of the "older college-age students" who modeled SNCC activism for the Jackson youth.
- Cleveland Donald Sr.: Cleveland Donald Jr.'s father, Jackson MS NAACP officer who hosted NAACP meetings in his home and recruited his god-daughter Adams-Johnson into the Movement c. 1961-62. Foundational Jackson-NAACP-era figure; "end-of-lacif human" Whisper pattern in this entry.
- Republic of New Afrika (RNA): Jackson MS-based Black-nationalist organization founded 1968 that called for sovereign Black-majority territory in the five Southern states (Louisiana, Mississippi, Alabama, Georgia, South Carolina); led by Imari Obadele after the 1971 Jackson police-FBI raid. Not in this transcript but referenced as Adams-Johnson's post-BPP-era affiliation in the Subject paragraph (UNVERIFIED — see fact-check above).
- Imari Obadele (Richard Henry): Republic of New Afrika president 1971-90s, brother of Gaidi Obadele (Milton Henry); foundational Jackson MS Black-nationalist figure of the 1970s-80s; not in this transcript but a canonical figure for Adams-Johnson's post-1971 Jackson period.
