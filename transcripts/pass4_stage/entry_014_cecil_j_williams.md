**Net-new ground-truth corpus candidates:**

- Joe Mosnier: canonical interviewer for the Civil Rights History Project oral history corpus (60+ Mosnier-led entries housed in the joint Library of Congress + Smithsonian NMAAHC archive); historian with the Southern Oral History Program at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill; project-essential figure who deserves a canonical entry to ground the corpus-pervasive Whisper failure "Joe Manier".
- Rev. Joseph A. DeLaine (1898-1974): canonical Clarendon County SC AME minister + Briggs v. Elliott petition organizer; driven from his home by white violence after the case; arsoned out of state, charged in absentia with assault for defending himself; foundational pre-Brown figure already in Pass 3 candidates but not yet added to corpus.
- Briggs v. Elliott (1949-54): canonical SC case that was the first of the five consolidated cases that became Brown v. Board of Education (1954); the predecessor case from Clarendon County SC; warrants its own canonical-events entry distinct from Brown.
- Levi Pearson: canonical Clarendon County SC farmer; the original (1947) Briggs v. Elliott petition was filed in his name (*Pearson v. County Board*); pre-1949 petition that became the basis for Briggs. Foundational pre-Brown figure not in any canonical corpus entry.
- Pearson family + Briggs family + DeLaine family — the three Clarendon County SC families at the core of the Briggs v. Elliott petition. All three families suffered loss of livelihood, arson, and forced relocation as retaliation. Foundational pre-Brown corpus context.
- *Pittsburgh Courier* (1907-1966): canonical Black national newspaper; one of the most influential African-American newspapers of the civil rights era; consistently garbled by Whisper as "Pittsburgh Correa" and similar. Corpus-essential publication.
- *Baltimore Afro-American* (founded 1892): canonical Black national newspaper; one of the most influential African-American newspapers of the civil rights era; consistently garbled by Whisper as "outflow American" and similar. Corpus-essential publication.
- Felton Hall / Felton Gymnasium (SC State College / SC State University): canonical largest assembly space on the SC State campus in the 1950s; venue for Thurgood Marshall's 1955 post-Brown rally; named for SC State President Reginald T. Felton (1908-11).
- *Jet* magazine: canonical Black-press weekly news magazine (1951-2014, Johnson Publishing Company Chicago); Williams was the magazine's Orangeburg correspondent from age 15; foundational Black-press publication that documented the civil rights movement from a Black-community perspective. Not currently in civil_rights_facts.json.
- *Edwards v. South Carolina* (1963): canonical US Supreme Court decision (372 US 229) protecting peaceful protest against unconstitutional municipal trespass laws; argued by Matthew J. Perry Jr.; foundational First Amendment civil rights case. Already referenced in Pass 3 candidates but warrants its own corpus entry.
- Strom Thurmond (1902-2003): canonical SC Governor 1947-51, US Senator 1954-2003; segregationist whose 24-hour-18-minute filibuster against the Civil Rights Act of 1957 is the longest in US Senate history; ran for President as a Dixiecrat in 1948; foundational opposition figure to the civil rights movement. Williams's mention of writing him about out-of-state tuition is canonical: Thurmond's policy explicitly refused such applications. Not in civil_rights_facts.json despite massive corpus-wide relevance.
