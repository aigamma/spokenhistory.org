**Net-new ground-truth corpus candidates:**

- Milton Shapp (1912-1994): Pennsylvania Governor 1971-1979, the first Pennsylvania governor of Jewish descent and the first PA governor elected to two consecutive terms under the 1968 PA constitutional amendment. Administered PA Act 195 (Public Employee Relations Act, 1970, signed by predecessor Shafer) which gave PA hospital workers collective-bargaining rights five years before the federal NLRA amendments of 1974/75. Tillow's canonical PA-1199-era governor reference; worth adding to corpus as a Northern-state-labor-rights-adjacent figure to balance the Southern-civil-rights-focused canonical set.
- *Encyclopedia Africana* (Kwame Nkrumah / W.E.B. Du Bois project, 1961-): The Pan-African scholarly encyclopedia commissioned by Nkrumah's Ghanaian government and edited by Du Bois from his move to Accra in October 1961 until his death August 27, 1963. The project was partially completed by the Du Bois Centre in Accra after his death and produced its first volume in 1997. Canonical Pan-African intellectual project of the early 1960s. Worth catalog entry as a foundational document/project.
- PA Act 195 / Public Employee Relations Act of 1970 (Pennsylvania): Signed July 23, 1970 by Gov. Raymond P. Shafer, extended collective bargaining rights to PA public employees including hospital workers, pre-dating the 1974 Health Care Amendments to the federal NLRA by four years. Canonical Northern-state-labor-rights legislation; Tillow's 1199 PA organizing won under its provisions. Worth catalog entry.
- *Shelby County v. Holder*, 570 U.S. 529 (2013): U.S. Supreme Court 5-4 decision delivered June 25, 2013 (less than two months before Tillow's Aug 14, 2013 interview), invalidating Section 4(b) of the Voting Rights Act of 1965 — the coverage formula determining which jurisdictions required federal preclearance under Section 5 before changing voting laws. Tillow's "the voting rights decision is just heartbreaking" comment refers to this. Canonical 2010s civil-rights-rollback decision. Worth catalog entry since multiple 2013-cohort interviewees will reference it.
- Shira A. Scheindlin (b. 1946): Southern District of New York federal judge whose Aug 12, 2013 ruling in *Floyd v. City of New York* found NYPD's stop-and-frisk policy unconstitutional under the 4th and 14th Amendments. Pass 1 #74.50 / Pass 2 #74.P2.55 logged. Worth corpus entry. Tillow's interview took place Aug 14, 2013, two days after the ruling — her reference is to a then-fresh canonical-jurisprudence development.
- CLUW (Coalition of Labor Union Women, 1974): Founded March 23-24, 1974 in Detroit's Cobo Hall with 3,200 union women in attendance; Olga Madar (UAW VP) was founding president. Pass 1 #74.47 / Pass 2 #74.P2.53 logged. Tillow was Pittsburgh chapter president. Worth corpus entry as a canonical labor-feminism bridge organization.
- *Floyd v. City of New York* (NYPD stop-and-frisk, 2013): The U.S. District Court (SDNY) ruling by Judge Shira A. Scheindlin on Aug 12, 2013, holding that the NYPD's stop-and-frisk practice constituted indirect racial profiling in violation of the 4th and 14th Amendments. The opinion required substantial NYPD reforms including an outside monitor. Cross-references all stop-and-frisk-era 2013 oral history references. Worth catalog entry.
