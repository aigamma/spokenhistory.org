**Net-new ground-truth corpus candidates:**

- Witherspoon v. Illinois, 391 U.S. 510 (1968): U.S. Supreme Court decision holding that prospective jurors categorically opposed to the death penalty may not be excluded for cause from capital-case juries; the decision provided the legal-strategic foundation for capital-defense attorneys (including Millard Fuller, Millard Farmer, Donald Hollowell, and LDF/ACLU attorneys) to challenge the de-facto all-white death-penalty juries that produced racially-disparate capital sentencing in the Deep South 1968-72. Cross-cutting canonical case in 1960s-70s civil-rights-adjacent capital-defense legal history.
- Donald Hollowell (1917-2004): Atlanta Black civil rights attorney; lead counsel in University of Georgia desegregation case (Holmes v. Danner, 1961); EEOC Southeast Regional Director 1966-1985; mentor and law partner to Vernon Jordan. Foundational Atlanta-Black-civil-rights-bar figure. Cross-referenced in #78 (death-penalty defense work in Georgia) and recurring across Atlanta civil-rights legal corpus.
- Millard Farmer (b. 1934): Atlanta-based capital defense attorney; founder of the Team Defense Project (1976+); pioneering Southern capital-defense litigator in the post-Furman / pre-Gregg legal landscape. Cross-referenced in #78 alongside Millard Fuller's death-penalty work; recurring across post-1972 Southern capital-defense corpus.
- Joseph Levin Jr. (b. 1943): Co-founder of the Southern Poverty Law Center (1971) alongside Morris Dees; Montgomery AL civil rights attorney; legal-strategic counterpart to Dees in SPLC's founding decade. Should be added alongside the existing SPLC / Morris Dees corpus-candidacy cluster from Pass 3.
- Faith Fuller (b. ~1963): Daughter of Millard and Linda Fuller; director of *Briars in the Cotton Patch* (2003, PBS); the canonical first-generation Koinonia-Habitat documentarian. Foundational Koinonia documentary filmmaker.
- Huntingdon College (Montgomery, AL): Methodist liberal arts college founded 1854 as Tuscaloosa Female College; relocated to Montgomery 1909; canonical 1950s-60s Methodist intellectual training ground for Alabama civil rights activists including Bob Zellner (1958-59 sociology class), Linda Caldwell Fuller (1961-66 student body), and others. Cross-corpus relevance to #30 (Dorothy Zellner) and Alabama-civil-rights-college cluster. Catalog candidate distinct from already-established Tougaloo-cluster HBCU pattern.
- The 1971 Americus Boycott: Sumter County GA Black-community boycott of white-owned downtown businesses to force the integration of the Americus police force and county sheriff's department; canonical Linda + Millard Fuller participation; led to the hiring of the first Black Americus police officers and the first Black woman officer. Foundational post-1965-VRA Southern small-town civil-rights-action narrative.
