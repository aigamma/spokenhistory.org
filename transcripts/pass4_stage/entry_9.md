**Net-new ground-truth corpus candidates:**

- Harry T. Moore (1905-1951) and Harriette Moore (1902-1952): NAACP organizers in Mims, Florida; murdered by KKK bombing on Christmas night 1951 (Harry T. died that night; Harriette died nine days later from her injuries). Often referred to as the first martyrs of the modern Civil Rights Movement era. Foundational pre-Brown v. Board martyrs missing from corpus. HIGH PRIORITY — Moore was the foundational pre-Movement NAACP martyr and his absence from the corpus is a notable gap.
- Irene Morgan (Kirkaldy) (1917-2007): Baltimore woman who refused to give up her Greyhound bus seat in Virginia 1944; her case, *Morgan v. Virginia* (1946), was the SCOTUS ruling that struck down segregation in interstate bus travel — the legal precursor to *Boynton v. Virginia* (1960) and the 1961 Freedom Rides. Pre-Movement figure foundational to the Freedom-Rides legal chain. HIGH PRIORITY.
- Sgt. E.L. Cowling (Eli L. Cowling): Alabama Public Service Commission investigator riding undercover on the Greyhound bus that was firebombed outside Anniston May 14, 1961. Drew his pistol in the bus doorway to keep the mob from boarding while passengers escaped. Foundational Freedom-Rides eyewitness figure. (Reinforces Pass 3 ground-truth-corpus candidate.)
- Frank M. Johnson Jr. (1918-1999): US District Judge, Middle District of Alabama 1955-1979 (later Eleventh Circuit Court of Appeals 1979-1999). Issued numerous canonical Civil Rights Era rulings: *Browder v. Gayle* (1956) panel majority, Selma march injunction (1965), Alabama prison reform, mental-health-system reform. One of the canonical Southern federal judges who advanced desegregation jurisprudence. HIGH PRIORITY — foundational jurist missing from corpus.
- Earl Warren (1891-1974): Chief Justice of the US Supreme Court 1953-1969. Authored *Brown v. Board of Education* (1954) unanimous opinion; presided over *Bond v. Floyd*, *Loving v. Virginia*, many Civil-Rights-Era rulings. Foundational jurist; predict cross-corpus recurrence.
- Joe Mosnier: Southern Oral History Program (UNC Chapel Hill) interviewer who conducted ~30+ of the Library of Congress Civil Rights History Project oral histories (2010-2011). Subject of multiple Whisper-failure renderings ("Joe Manier" being one). Worth adding as a non-Movement-figure metadata-corpus entry to support interviewer-name disambiguation. (Not a Movement figure; instead a Movement-archive figure.)
- Carl J. Murphy (1889-1967): *Baltimore Afro-American* publisher 1922-1967 (already in Pass 3 candidate list — reinforce). HIGH PRIORITY.
- Bruce Boynton (1937-2020): Plaintiff in *Boynton v. Virginia* (1960) — arrested in 1958 Trailways Richmond VA bus station for refusing to leave the whites-only restaurant section. The SCOTUS ruling established the legal basis for the 1961 Freedom Rides. Foundational Movement figure who has been understudied. (Reinforces Pass 3 candidate.) HIGH PRIORITY.
