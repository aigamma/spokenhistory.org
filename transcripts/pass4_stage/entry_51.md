- **St. Augustine Foot Soldiers Monument (2011)**: Bronze monument in St. Augustine FL honoring the youth foot soldiers of the 1964 St. Augustine Movement; the canonical three honorees on the monument plaque are Audrey Nell Hamilton, JoeAnn Anderson Ulmer, and a third figure (Pass 4 adversarial-flagged as "Elmer", canonical full name TBD); subjects also appear in transcript #4 in this corpus. - **St. Cyprian's Episcopal Church (Lincolnville, St. Augustine FL)**: Founded ~1900 as the African-American Episcopal congregation in Lincolnville; Duncan's great-grandmother Abby Finley was a co-founder; the building has remained the same congregation site for over 150 years per Duncan's testimony. Foundational pre-Movement Black religious institution in St. Augustine.
- **Lincolnville (St. Augustine FL)**: Historic African-American neighborhood of St. Augustine, FL; the 1963-65 St. Augustine Movement campaign center; the SCLC headquarters and Dr. King's St. Augustine staging-ground locations were all within Lincolnville; KKK night marches through Lincolnville were a defining event of the 1963-65 campaign. Note: Central Avenue (Lincolnville's main street) was renamed Martin Luther King Jr. Avenue post-Movement.
- **Excelsior Elementary School (St. Augustine FL)**: Canonical Lincolnville segregation-era Black elementary school; closed during forced integration in the mid-1960s. Recurring reference in Duncan's transcript.
- **Solomon Calhoun Community Center (St. Augustine FL)**: Canonical Lincolnville community center; possible canonical referent for Duncan's "Gallimor Center" Whisper-rendering (adversarial-flagged).
- **Flagler College (St. Augustine FL)**: Private liberal-arts college founded 1968 in the former Ponce de Leon Hotel; the college's president was a member of the 40th ACCORD's St. Augustine civil rights museum planning committee per Duncan's testimony.
- **Shirley E. Barnes (1939-)**: Possible canonical referent for Duncan's Madagascar-ambassador-cousin claim; Black US Ambassador to Madagascar 1998-2001 under Clinton; alternative candidate to the adversarial-flagged "Iolanda Allen" reading (P2.51). - **Fairchild Industries / Fairchild Hiller**: Defense contractor predecessor at the St. Augustine site before Northrop Grumman acquisition; canonical referent for Duncan's "Fertile Hill" Whisper-rendering (P2.36, adversarial-flagged); the Fairchild Republic Aircraft Division had operations in St. Augustine before Northrop Grumman's acquisition.
