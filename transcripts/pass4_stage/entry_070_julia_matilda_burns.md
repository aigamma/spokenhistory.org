**Net-new ground-truth corpus candidates:**

- Mileston Cooperative Association (Holmes County, Mississippi): The Black-landowner farming cooperative grown out of the 1940 FSA Project MS-21 land-purchase program at Mileston, Holmes County. Funded the Holmes County Health Research Program (1969-78) that produced *The Extended Family in Black Societies* (Mouton 1978). Burns served as its secretary in the 1970s. Functionally distinct from the Mileston Community Center (the physical Abe-Osheroff-built building) — the Cooperative is the legal/economic entity, the Community Center is the building. Worth adding as a separate corpus entry from Turnbow's Mileston Fourteen.
- Tchula Consolidated School integration (1965): The first integration of the all-white Tchula Consolidated School in Holmes County, Mississippi, in the fall of 1965, by approximately 16 Black first-graders including Christopher Epps and Burns's son Reginald. The school was set on fire twice by segregationists, then fully abandoned by white families who formed the Cruger-Tchula Academy. Canonical example of one of the most extreme white-flight resistance patterns in post-*Brown* Mississippi public-school integration history. Worth corpus entry as a distinct event from generic Mississippi public-school-integration overview.
- William Dean (Holmes County's first Black school superintendent, late 1980s): Holmes County, Mississippi's first Black school superintendent, appointed in the late 1980s. Marks the canonical milestone of the Mississippi Black-Voting-Rights-Act-driven officeholding wave reaching the local-school-administrative apex. Worth lightweight corpus entry as a Mississippi-Delta-Black-officeholding-pipeline figure.
- Holmes County Health Research Program (1969-78): The University-of-Illinois–led community-health-research program conducted in Holmes County, Mississippi, 1969-78, that documented hypertension rates in 6-year-olds, FSA-land-cooperative economic resilience, and Mississippi Delta Black population shift patterns. Produced *The Extended Family in Black Societies* (Shimkin, Shimkin, and Frate, eds.; Mouton 1978). The canonical academic-Movement collaboration grounding the Mileston Cooperative's post-Voting-Rights-Act economic-development phase. Worth corpus entry as a distinct program from generic civil-rights-era community-health-research overviews.
- The Cruger-Tchula Academy (Holmes County MS, 1965-present): The segregation academy formed by white families who pulled their children out of Tchula Consolidated School in 1965 after the school was integrated and twice burned. Located in Cruger, Mississippi. Worth corpus entry as one of the most canonical Mississippi segregation academies, distinct from the broader Mississippi segregation-academy network.
