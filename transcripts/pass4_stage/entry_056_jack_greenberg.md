**Net-new ground-truth corpus candidates:**

- Jack Greenberg (1924-2016): NAACP LDF Director-Counsel 1961-84 (succeeded Thurgood Marshall); co-counsel with Robert L. Carter in the Topeka KS *Brown v. Board* trial 1951; argued *Swann v. Charlotte-Mecklenburg* (1971); author of *Race Relations and American Law* (1959), the first major civil-rights-law treatise since 1919; Columbia Law professor 1984-2017; instrumental in developing the LDF Southern-Black-attorney $10,000-startup-grant program that recruited Julius Chambers, Mel Watt, Marian Wright Edelman, and others into Southern civil rights litigation. (Pass 3 already noted; Pass 4 confirms.)
- Roy Wilkins (1901-1981): NAACP Executive Secretary 1955-77; the canonical national-NAACP head during the 1957 LDF tax-exemption spinoff; the foundational LDF-vs-parent-NAACP institutional-tension figure. Greenberg's first-person account ("tension between us and Roy Wilkins") is canonical.
- Marian Wynn Perry (1907-1969): The first woman NAACP LDF attorney (mid-1940s); resigned in early 1949 to take a position with New York State, creating the vacancy Marshall filled by hiring Greenberg in March 1949. Married name change to "Adams" documented in NAACP records.
- *Alexander v. Holmes County Board of Education* (1969): Canonical SCOTUS unanimous per curiam decision mandating immediate school desegregation, ending the "all deliberate speed" deferral pattern that *Brown II* (1955) had established. Greenberg's first-person account of how LDF used the ancient common-law writ of certiorari to bypass Nixon's HEW-Finch-Stennis blockade and force the case to SCOTUS is canonical first-person source material.
- *Coke v. City of Atlanta* (184 F. Supp. 579, N.D. Ga. 1960): Canonical Atlanta airport desegregation case; plaintiff surname was Coke; foundational pre-Civil-Rights-Act-of-1964 federal-court airport-desegregation precedent. Greenberg argued the case for LDF.
- Edmond Cahn (1906-1964): NYU School of Law professor of legal philosophy; author of *The Sense of Injustice* (1949) and the foundational academic critique of *Brown* footnote 11's social-science basis; the canonical anti-Brown-reasoning intellectual partner-piece to Herbert Wechsler's "Toward Neutral Principles of Constitutional Law" (1959 Harvard Law Review). Both Cahn and Wechsler defended *Brown*'s outcome while criticizing its reasoning.
- Marshal Ferdinand Foch (1851-1929): French Marshal who commanded Allied forces at the Second Battle of the Marne (July 1918); the canonical attribution-of-record for the "My left flank is in retreat, my right flank is in retreat, my center collapses, I attack" quote (though some historians attribute it to Pétain or to the First Battle of the Marne). Greenberg's invocation of Foch as the inspiration for LDF's ancient-common-law-writ-of-certiorari strategy is canonical first-person source material.
