**Ground-truth corpus candidates (figures to add to civil_rights_facts.json):**

- Kathleen Neal Cleaver: Subject of this transcript. Communications Secretary of the Black Panther Party 1967-71; daughter of Tuskegee/State Dept Point Four Program officer Ernest Cooper Neal; married Eldridge Cleaver June 1968; Algeria + Cuba exile 1969-75; later Yale JD 1989; Emory Law professor. Canonical BPP-women's-leadership figure and a foundational first-person witness across BPP, SNCC, COINTELPRO, and post-1968 Black radical history.
- Sammy Younge Jr.: Tuskegee Institute student + Navy veteran murdered Jan 3 1966 by Marvin Segrest. First college student killed in the Civil Rights Movement. Trigger event for SNCC's 1966 anti-Vietnam-War statement (which then triggered Julian Bond's denied Georgia state legislature seating Jan 1966 → *Bond v. Floyd* 1966 SCOTUS). Foundational figure missing from current corpus.
- George Ware: Tuskegee Institute Advancement League (TIAL) leader 1965; quit Hoffmann-La Roche chemistry job after Sammy Younge murder to rejoin SNCC; took over SNCC's campus program in Atlanta January 1967. Kathleen Cleaver's direct SNCC supervisor. Canonical SNCC campus organizing figure.
- Maxwell C. Stanford Jr. (Muhammad Ahmad): Founder of Revolutionary Action Movement (RAM) Philadelphia 1962; pre-BPP Black-nationalist underground organizer; later African Studies professor at Temple University. Canonical RAM founding ideologue.
- Pete O'Neal: Kansas City BPP chairman; left US 1970 for Tanzania after federal weapons charge; founded United African Alliance Community Center in Arusha (still operating in 2026). Canonical BPP-in-exile figure.
- Assata Shakur: NY-area BPP / Black Liberation Army member; convicted 1977 of NJ State Trooper Foerster's 1973 killing; escaped 1979; granted political asylum in Cuba 1984; still living in Havana. International symbol of US political-prisoner debate.
- Mumia Abu-Jamal: Philadelphia BPP teenager / MOVE activist; convicted 1982 of 1981 Officer Daniel Faulkner killing; currently serving life sentence (commuted from death row 2011); international cause célèbre via his prison journalism.
- Dr. Charles G. Gomillion: Tuskegee Civic Association (TCA) leader; sociologist at Tuskegee Institute; plaintiff in *Gomillion v. Lightfoot* (1960) SCOTUS gerrymandering case that struck down Tuskegee's racial-gerrymander city-boundary redrawing. Canonical pre-Voting-Rights-Act voter-suppression-legal-challenge figure.
- Ernest Cooper Neal: Tuskegee Institute faculty; US State Department Point Four Program officer in India, Philippines, Sierra Leone, Liberia 1953-65. Kathleen Cleaver's father; canonical Black foreign-service-officer pioneer.
- Gen. Vo Nguyen Giap: Vietnamese commander, Tet Offensive 1968; the "we won every battle" quotation Cleaver cites is canonical anti-war movement reference. Worth catalog entry given recurring corpus references.
- Marvin Segrest: White Tuskegee gas-station attendant who murdered Sammy Younge Jr. Jan 3 1966; acquitted by all-white Macon County AL jury. Canonical Movement-era assailant whose acquittal trigger.
- Joudon Major Ford: Early NYC Black Panther Party leader; previously in SNCC. Worth canonical entry as early NYC BPP figure.
- Chico Neblett: Boston BPP deputy chairman; younger brother of Chuck Neblett of SNCC Freedom Singers. Canonical mid-period Black Panther regional leader.
- Carey McWilliams: Writer + Oberlin College faculty 1960s; canonical *Nation* magazine editor; influential mentor for Cleaver during her Oberlin year.
