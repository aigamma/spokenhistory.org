**Ground-truth corpus candidates (figures to add to civil_rights_facts.json):**

- Cecil J. Williams (b. 1937): canonical SC civil rights photographer; *Jet* magazine's Orangeburg correspondent from age 15; documented the Briggs petitioners (1949-52), the Elloree teachers' 1956 mass resignation, Harvey Gantt's 1963 Clemson entrance, the 1968 Orangeburg Massacre. Authored *Freedom & Justice* (1995), *Out of the Box in Dixie* (2007), *Orangeburg 1968* (2008+ photo-history). The in-community Black photographer whose archive is the canonical visual record of the SC movement. Foundational for Smithsonian-grade corpus (the project is co-sponsored by NMAAHC + LoC).
- Rev. Joseph A. DeLaine (1898-1974): canonical Clarendon County SC AME minister + Briggs v. Elliott petition organizer; driven from his home by white violence after the case; fired-on by Klan + arson 1955; arsoned out of state, charged in absentia with assault for defending himself. Foundational pre-Brown figure. Not in current corpus.
- Briggs v. Elliott (1952): canonical SC case that was the first of the five cases consolidated into Brown v. Board of Education (1954); the predecessor case that established the school-segregation challenge that Brown decided. Already mentioned in the Brown v. Board entry but warrants its own canonical-events entry.
- Constance Baker Motley (1921-2005): canonical NAACP Legal Defense Fund attorney (1945-65); first Black woman in NY State Senate (1964-66); first Black woman federal judge (SDNY 1966-2005); argued 10 SCOTUS cases winning 9; argued landmark school-desegregation cases including James Meredith v. Univ of MS. Not in current corpus despite foundational role.
- Matthew J. Perry Jr. (1921-2011): canonical SC civil rights attorney; first Black federal judge in SC (US Military Court of Appeals 1976; SC US District Court 1979); argued Briggs v. Elliott; argued *Edwards v. South Carolina* (1963, SCOTUS, protecting peaceful protest); foundational SC Movement legal figure. Not in current corpus; cross-corpus entry #87 also features him.
- Harvey Gantt (b. 1943): canonical first Black student at Clemson University (1963); SC State student transferring after Williams's transcript-era expulsion environment; later Charlotte NC mayor (1983-87); 1990 and 1996 US Senate candidate (against Jesse Helms). Not in current corpus.
- Cleveland Sellers (b. 1944): canonical SNCC program secretary 1965-67; the only person convicted in connection with the 1968 Orangeburg Massacre (incitement to riot, 7 months served); pardoned 1993; later president of Voorhees College 2008-17; author of *The River of No Return* (1973). Cross-corpus #22 features him in his own interview. Not in current corpus despite SNCC executive-team role. Canonical figure who suffers the catalog-pervasive "Slave Sellers" Whisper failure.
- Voorhees College: canonical HBCU in Denmark, SC; founded 1897 by Elizabeth Evelyn Wright (Booker T. Washington protégé); Cleveland Sellers served as 7th president 2008-17.
- Orangeburg Massacre (Feb 8 1968): already in the corpus (canonical entry); no addition needed but Williams's transcript is the canonical in-community visual record source.
- South Carolina State College / University: canonical HBCU in Orangeburg SC (founded 1896); site of the 1968 Orangeburg Massacre.
- Claflin University: canonical HBCU in Orangeburg SC (founded 1869, oldest HBCU in SC); the institution that gave Williams the scholarship after BC Turner rescinded the SC State one.
- *The Orangeburg Massacre* (1970 book by Jack Bass + Jack Nelson): canonical investigative-journalism account of the Feb 8 1968 events; required cross-reference for any post-1970 Orangeburg-Massacre coverage.
- John Stroman: canonical SC State student leader who organized the Feb 1968 All-Star Bowling Alley protests; charged with riot/incitement; deserved-but-unindicted historical record. Speaker references him as the bowling-alley protest leader.
