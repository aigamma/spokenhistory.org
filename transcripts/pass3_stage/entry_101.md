**Ground-truth corpus candidates (figures to add to civil_rights_facts.json):**

- Reginald "Reggie" Robinson (b. 1939): Foundational SNCC field secretary 1961-67. Joined SNCC at the June 1961 Louisville coordinating committee meeting (where Ella Baker recognized him by his family resemblance). Among the earliest McComb MS organizers under Bob Moses; co-founder of the Cambridge MD SNCC project with Bill Hansen. First-person witness to the Herbert Lee killing (Sept 1961) and the Burglund High walkout aftermath (Oct 1961). Foundational figure for both McComb and Cambridge Movements; not in civil_rights_facts.json. Cross-corpus #101.
- Herbert Lee (d. September 25, 1961): NAACP voter-registration organizer murdered by Mississippi State Representative E.H. Hurst at a Liberty MS cotton gin in front of multiple witnesses. The canonical first staff-adjacent martyr of SNCC's Mississippi project. Robinson's first-person account of the killing and its strategic aftermath (Amzie Moore's advisory to stay in MS, not retreat to Atlanta) is canonical. Not in civil_rights_facts.json — foundational MS martyr.
- Louis Allen (d. January 31, 1964): Black logger and witness to Herbert Lee's killing who testified to the FBI; subsequently murdered in Liberty MS the night before he was to leave for Milwaukee. The second canonical MS voter-registration-witness martyr. Robinson's "Fred Allen" identification with high confidence. Not in civil_rights_facts.json.
- E.H. (Eugene Hubert) Hurst: Mississippi State Representative who killed Herbert Lee in front of witnesses Sept 25, 1961 and was acquitted that same day in coroner's inquest. Canonical perpetrator-not-prosecuted figure. Not in civil_rights_facts.json.
- Gloria Richardson (Dandridge) (1922-2021): Foundational Cambridge MD movement leader 1962-64; canonical advocate for boycotting the 1963 public-accommodations referendum. Already cross-corpus #49. Foundational non-Southern Black-movement figure; consider promoting to civil_rights_facts.json.
- Bob Mants (1943-2011): SNCC Lowndes County AL field secretary 1965-67; foundational Lowndes County Freedom Organization (LCFO) organizer (the original "Black Panther" emblem org); founder of the Bessemer-Carver Foundation. Not in civil_rights_facts.json; foundational LCFO figure.
- Maj. Gen. George M. Gelston (1907-1981): Maryland National Guard general dispatched to Cambridge MD 1963-64 during the canonical Cambridge crisis; the canonical example of restrained National Guard response that maintained order without escalating violence. Important figure for the Cambridge Movement.
