- *NAACP v. Claiborne Hardware Co.* (458 U.S. 886, 1982): Unanimous SCOTUS decision establishing that a politically-motivated boycott (the 1966-70 Port Gibson MS NAACP boycott of white merchants) is protected by the First Amendment, even though it causes economic damage to the boycotted businesses. Decided 8-0 (Justice Marshall recused; opinion by Justice Stevens). Foundational First Amendment + civil-rights-as-protected-political-action case. NOT in `civil_rights_facts.json` despite being one of the few SCOTUS cases that arose directly from a SNCC/NAACP local movement and shielded the boycott tactic from civil-litigation reprisal.
- Rudy Shields (~1932-c. 1980): Canonical SW Mississippi NAACP/Movement field organizer; came to Port Gibson with Charles Evers 1965-66; later lived in Yazoo MS where he died. Foundational SW MS NAACP voter-registration figure not in any standard reference work despite ~15 years of full-time field work across Port Gibson, Yazoo, Natchez, and Hattiesburg. The Yazoo-host-family's daughter is reportedly writing a Shields biography (per Guster's transcript).

Foundational post-Medgar MS NAACP figure not in `civil_rights_facts.json`.
