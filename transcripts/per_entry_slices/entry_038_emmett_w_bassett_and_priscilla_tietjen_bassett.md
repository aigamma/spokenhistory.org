### 38. Emmett W. Bassett and Priscilla Tietjen Bassett

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Emmett W. Bassett and Priscilla Tietjen Bassett_interview_20250704_201849/`
**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier (Southern Oral History Program at UNC Chapel Hill); videographer John Bishop — 21 July 2011, at the Bassetts' home in Grahamsville, New York
**Subject**: Dr. Emmett W. Bassett (1921–2018) and Priscilla Tietjen Bassett — interracial couple married in 1950; Emmett: Tuskegee Institute (work-study under George Washington Carver), WWII US Army Quartermaster Corps at Camp Lee (Petersburg VA), University of Massachusetts Amherst master's, Ohio State PhD (likely first African-American PhD in dairy science / dairy microbiology in the US), Columbia University faculty (biosynthesis of patulin and aromatic amino acids); Priscilla: Plainfield NJ Republican-family upbringing, Smith College (history major), Life magazine pre-college work, Progressive Party activist, New York Public Library children's librarian, UAW shop steward at General Motors Columbus OH; both: long history of civil rights and progressive activism through Columbus OH (Brown II era, Emmett Till relief fund), the In Friendship founding cohort with Bayard Rustin and Stanley Levison (1955–56), the Prayer Pilgrimage 1957, March on Washington 1963 bus recruitment, the Council on Interracial Books for Children (1960s onward), and the Ocean Hill–Brownsville community-control struggle (1968).
**Cross-references**: Bassett↔Stanley Levison (the King financial adviser whom Priscilla recruited the Bassetts through In Friendship); Bassett↔Bayard Rustin (March on Washington co-organizer; Tom Kahn relationship — see Thelwell #34.26 and #34.27); Bassett↔Robert F. Williams and Mae Mallory (the canonical Monroe NC armed-self-defense advocacy, cited by Priscilla as the relevant precedent for choosing private school for the Bassett children); Bassett↔March on Washington 1963 (Priscilla and Emmett recruited buses from Manhattan).
