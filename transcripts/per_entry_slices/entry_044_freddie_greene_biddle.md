### 44. Freddie Greene (Biddle)

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Freddie Greene_interview_20250704_204156/`  
*Layer 5 removed 3 low-impact phantom rendering rows (whisper renderings not present in raw).*
**Interviewer / Date**: Emily Crosby (Southern Oral History Program at UNC Chapel Hill); videographer John Bishop — 10 December 2015, at the Library of Congress (interview was relocated from a field site to the LOC building for this session)
**Subject**: Freddie Greene Biddle — Greenwood MS-born (February 15, 1945); daughter of a Black house-painter / NAACP father (active in NAACP in early 1960s) and a Tougaloo-high-school-and-college-educated mother (teacher); high school senior at the time of the foundational 1962-63 SNCC voter-registration drive in Greenwood; helped distribute the surplus food brought to Greenwood by Dick Gregory (via plane) and Ivanhoe Donaldson (via truck) when Leflore County terminated its commodity-food distribution to retaliate against the voter-registration push; her brother Dwight had applied unsuccessfully to be the second Black student at Ole Miss in 1962 (the slot eventually went to Cleve McDowell); her brother George went down to Parchman in 1963 with Hollis Watkins and MacArthur Cotton for trespass; Dillard University (New Orleans, 1963-65; nursing-major then politics-major); Tougaloo / SNCC connection through Casey Hayden contact via Tulane; SNCC summer 1964 in McComb MS at the Freedom House on the night it was firebombed (her brother George cast the lone dissenting vote against bringing the rest of the Freedom Summer volunteers down to McComb the night before the bombing); MFDP delegate from McComb to the Atlantic City Democratic National Convention August 1964; Atlanta SNCC office finance staff from 1965, working under Ruby Doris Smith Robinson and James Forman; left SNCC in June 1968 after King's assassination and the financial collapse.
**Cross-references**: Greene↔Stokely Carmichael (Carmichael was a regular guest at the Greene family Greenwood home during his 1962-64 Mississippi visits); Greene↔James Forman / Ruby Doris Smith Robinson (Atlanta SNCC office mentors); Greene↔Dick Gregory / Ivanhoe Donaldson (the 1962-63 Leflore County food-relief operation); Greene↔Sam Block / Willie Peacock / Bob Moses (the foundational SNCC Greenwood field secretaries); Greene↔Amzie Moore (the Cleveland MS NAACP / Mississippi Free Press distribution partner); Greene↔Pete Seeger / Peter Paul and Mary / SNCC Freedom Singers (the Greenwood freedom-singing festival on the McGee family farm); Greene↔Joyce Ladner / Dorie Ann Ladner (the Tougaloo / Greenwood network — both Ladners visited Greene at Dillard in NOLA late-night).

#### Notes for Pass 2

- **Full read**: This transcript was fully read end-to-end.
- **Greenwood MS NAACP / SNCC canonical first-person**: Greene's transcript is one of the most-detailed first-person sources in the corpus on the foundational 1962-63 Leflore County voter-registration drive, the simultaneous commodity-food cutoff in retaliation, and the Dick Gregory / Ivanhoe Donaldson food-airlift / truck-relief response. Pair with Simpson #41 (parallel Greenwood-area first-person from a younger SNCC field worker).
- **McComb Freedom House firebombing canonical first-person**: Greene's account of being in the McComb Freedom House the night it was firebombed July 1964 — and of her brother George casting the lone "no" vote the night before against bringing the rest of the Freedom Summer volunteers down to McComb — is one of the most-direct first-person sources in the corpus on the McComb-specific bombing campaign of Freedom Summer.
- **Heffners canonical first-person**: Greene's account of the Dennis Sweeney–led visit to Red and Malva Heffner (the white McComb couple who had known Sweeney's minister-friend in the military and were subsequently shunned out of town for hosting SNCC volunteers) is the most-direct first-person source in the corpus on this canonical "race-traitor"-expulsion narrative — Florence Mars and Joseph Crespino both write about the Heffners; Greene's first-person account fills in the SNCC-side context.
- **Atlanta SNCC office finance staff canonical first-person**: Greene's account of working under Ruby Doris Smith Robinson and James Forman in the Atlanta SNCC office, learning finance and bookkeeping from a Black woman who "could outwork and outdrive anybody," is one of the most-detailed first-person sources in the corpus on the Robinson-Forman SNCC organizational culture 1965-68.
- **MFDP Atlantic City 1964 canonical first-person**: Greene was an MFDP delegate from McComb. Pair with Adams-Johnson #43 (Jackson MFDP delegate) and Simpson #41 (Greenwood MFDP delegate) for the most complete corpus picture of the foundational Atlantic City challenge.
