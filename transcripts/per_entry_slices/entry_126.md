### 126. William G. Anderson

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/William G. Anderson_interview_20250705_023404/`
**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier (SOHP UNC Chapel Hill) — Tuesday July 26, 2011, Detroit MI
**Cameraman**: John Bishop
**Subject**: Dr. William G. Anderson DO (b. ~1927 Americus GA), canonical founder + president of the Albany Movement Nov 1961-62; canonical Americus GA Movement childhood-friend of Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. (via his wife who knew King from Atlanta) + Ralph Abernathy (Anderson's classmate at Alabama State College Montgomery in the late 1940s, where Abernathy was the canonical godfather of Anderson's eldest child). Family background: father Morehouse-college-attendee + insurance agent for North Carolina Mutual Life in Albany GA; mother high-school-teacher who took 20 years to finish her BA degree; canonical 14-year-old 1941 attempted-Army-enlistment after Pearl Harbor blocked by his mother; canonical 15-year-old 1942 high-school-graduation → Fort Valley State College where he played football + drove for canonical Horace Mann Bond (the canonical first Black Morehouse College President + father of Julian Bond — Anderson was 16-year-old chauffeur for canonical Horace Mann Bond's Georgia + Alabama + Mississippi educational research trips). Canonical 1944 Navy enlistment (Anderson refused the Stewart's Branch + chose the canonical "last all-Black Seamen company" experiment) → canonical 1945 hospital corpsman training in the Philippines → canonical 1946 post-war discharge. Post-Navy: Atlanta + Alabama State Montgomery → canonical 1949-52 mortician studies at Atlanta College of Mortuary Science (paid his way through college as a Montgomery mortician) → 1952 Des Moines University College of Osteopathic Medicine (the only school that accepted him, after every MD school turned him down due to canonical Fort Valley State's non-accreditation) → canonical 1955-56 Flint MI Osteopathic Hospital intern (first Black osteopathic intern outside an HBCU-owned hospital in US history) → canonical 1957 return to Albany GA after his wife's canonical promise-to-go-back-South lecture. Canonical 1957-onward Albany GA $3-office-visit private practice (saw 100+ patients per day; admitted some at the canonical Bainbridge GA 50-bed Black-owned hospital 50 miles south; never lost a patient on the road carrying a pistol-permit through canonical Newton GA Baker County). Canonical Nov 1961 organizer of the Albany Movement (after watching SNCC's Charles Sherrod + Cordell Reagon + Charles Jones canonical pre-Nov 1961 voter-registration arrests through his office window in Harlem, the Black business district of Albany); canonical Dec 1961 visit by MLK + Abernathy + Wyatt T. Walker + Andy Young at canonical Shiloh Baptist Church + canonical Mount Zion Baptist Church (the canonical 4-times-spoke same-night MLK appearance) where Anderson "tricked" King into committing to Albany ongoing; canonical Dec 1961 MLK + Abernathy + Anderson arrest at canonical Lee County Lee County jail farm; canonical 1961-62 Albany Movement period of mass-arrests + canonical sit-ins at the Trailways bus station + canonical Albany Police Chief Laurie Pritchett "in his office, hand-over-heart, 'is this how you make people like you'" moment that became the basis of their eventual long-term friendship.
