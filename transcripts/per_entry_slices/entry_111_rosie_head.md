### 111. Rosie Head

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Rosie Head_interview_20250705_013259/`
**Interviewer / Date**: Dr. John Dittmer — Wednesday March 13, 2013, Tchula MS
**Cameraman**: John Bishop
**Subject**: Mrs. Rosie Head (b. ~1942-1945 Lexington/Greenwood MS, raised on Greenwood-area plantation until age ~17, then moved to Tchula MS Holmes County after her father refused to allow her mother to pick up his paycheck from the white plantation owner who demanded the wives "pick up the paycheck" as a sexual-coercion lever). Canonical Holmes County Mileston Community Center director under Henry + Sue Lorenzi + later under federal CDGM Head Start funding. Family background: father a carpenter + sharecropper who worked half-day, half-of-cotton, never-cleared-anything for two decades in Greenwood; mother cooked at the plantation owner's house and was the canonical "Miss Corrine"-required-respect target of the white-plantation gender-power-coercion system. As a 17-year-old got involved in Greenwood SNCC voter-registration meetings 1964 (with Bob Moses + Stokely Carmichael + Sam Block + Lawrence Guyot + Willie Wazir Peacock + Hollis Watkins); brought the canonical Greenwood-style SNCC meetings to Holmes County via the canonical John D. Ball (Holmes County's first SNCC organizer). Carried forward Holmes County Movement substantial work: ran the Mileston Community Center where Whisper-name-rendered-"milestone-14" canonical voter applicants (the original 14 Mileston applicants headed by Hartman Turnbow) met; ran the makeshift kindergarten 1964-65 before federal CDGM funding became available; took over the Mileston Head Start center from 1966 forward; the canonical Greyhound-bus-children-to-Washington-DC tactic was the substrate. Worked at Saints Junior College Lexington under Dr. Arenia Mallory (cross-corpus #107 Clark). Canonical 1967 election poll worker — manager + receiving + returning manager — for the Robert Clark #107 Mississippi state legislative victory. Was registered as a voter via the canonical Federal Registrar process post-1965-VRA after multiple prior in-person attempts at the Holmes County courthouse had been blocked by dog-guarded jail-closet-style intimidation (where Head was held in a 4-hour-long closet with a guard dog placed at the door to test whether she would try to escape).

**Cross-references**: Robert G.
