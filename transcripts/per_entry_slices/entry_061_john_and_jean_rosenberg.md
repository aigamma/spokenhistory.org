- **Foot Campbell Yazoo County 38-pistol canonical first-person**: Rosenberg's account of being summoned into Yazoo County MS Circuit Clerk "Foot" Campbell's office, listening to Campbell's lynching reminiscences, and then having Campbell pull a 38 pistol out of his drawer and shoot it into the floor in front of Rosenberg's foot (with the FBI in the next room photographing voting records) — "the only time anything like that ever happened to me" — is canonical first-person material on the foundational pre-Voting-Rights-Act registrar-intimidation tactic.
- **Dallas County 1966 recount canonical first-person**: Rosenberg's account of the foundational 1966 Selma Sheriff Jim Clark recount case — including Jean and Dorothy Shelton Landsberg in the Sheriff's office watching ballot boxes for a week alongside Jim Clark's deputies who tried to romance them and gave them "never" buttons — is canonical first-person material on the foundational first-post-Voting-Rights-Act Black-voter-registration election outcome in the Deep South.
- **Holocaust-refugee-as-civil-rights-attorney canonical first-person**: John Rosenberg's framing that his commitment to civil rights came from "having been born in Germany and seeing and thinking about the Holocaust history of ours and the terrible genocide... and seeing really in having been in the service with people who were Black and thinking that this caste system is wrong in this country, being very proud of this country" — is canonical first-person material on the foundational Jewish-refugee-to-civil-rights-attorney trajectory.
