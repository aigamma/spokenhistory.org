### 19. Charles "Chuck" Siler

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Charles Siler_interview_20250704_184115/`
**Interviewer / Date**: David Klein — 10 May (year unstated; likely 2013), African American Museum at Fair Park, Dallas, TX. Cameraman John Bishop.
**Subject**: Chuck Siler — born early 1940s, Baton Rouge, LA; McKinley High; Southern University. Foot soldier of the **December 1961 Baton Rouge / Southern University demonstrations** (his class led the boycott, jailed 12–13 December 1961, spent Christmas in East Baton Rouge Parish Jail). Hitchhiked to school with junior-high friend Hubert Gerold "**H. Rap Brown**". Drafted to Vietnam 1968; became Army information-office cartoonist and writer. Long career as editorial cartoonist (Louisiana Weekly, Black Commentator) and curator at the Louisiana State Museum (first Black male curator there). Studied photography and worked briefly in Hollywood as a TV/film writer.
