### 21. Clarence Magee

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Clarence Magee_interview_20250704_185204/`
**Interviewer / Date**: Emilye Crosby — 1 December 2015, Hattiesburg, MS (USM McCain Library, Center for Oral History and Cultural Heritage). With John Bishop and Guha Shankar.
**Subject**: Clarence McGee (b. 1932, Marion County, MS) — sharecropper's son; Alcorn A&M graduate; teacher at Lillie Burney School, Royal Street School, Rowan High School, and Blair High School in Hattiesburg. **Local NAACP officer in Hattiesburg during the Freedom Summer era (1964) and beyond**; subpoenaed alongside his wife Carey Bradley and T.F. Boyne in 1962 to Aberdeen, MS to verify that Clyde Kennard was still alive at Parchman Farm. Direct first-person witness to the **murder of Vernon Dahmer** (10 January 1966 firebombing of the Dahmer home by the White Knights of the KKK) — McGee classmates of Dahmer's sons, and stood watch over the Dahmer ruins in the nights afterward. Later led the Hattiesburg NAACP through the long road to the 1998 conviction of KKK Imperial Wizard Sam Bowers for the Dahmer murder.
