### 41. Euvester Simpson

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Euvester Simpson_interview_20250704_202900/`
**Interviewer / Date**: John Dittmer (DePauw University; author of *Local People: The Struggle for Civil Rights in Mississippi* 1994 — the foundational Mississippi-movement monograph in the corpus); videographer John Bishop — 11 March 2013, in Jackson, Mississippi
**Subject**: Euvester Simpson — Mississippi Delta-born (Itta Bena, Leflore County), youngest of seven; sent to Racine WI for high school at age 14; returned to MS January 1963; SNCC voter-registration field worker in Greenwood from spring 1963 (at age 17); one of the six women arrested at the Winona MS bus station on June 9, 1963 returning from the SCLC Citizenship School in Charleston SC (with Fannie Lou Hamer, Annelle Ponder, June Johnson, Rosemary Freeman, and Lawrence Guyot, who was arrested separately); beaten by police-deputized trustees in the Winona jail; in jail when news arrived of Medgar Evers' assassination (June 12, 1963); Tougaloo College 1964-65; later worked at Lawyers Committee for Civil Rights Under Law, Friends of Children in Mississippi (CDGM successor Head Start), Voice of Calvary health clinic in Mendenhall, Delta State University, Kellogg Foundation Mid-South Delta Initiative community-coach.
**Cross-references**: Simpson↔Fannie Lou Hamer (Winona jail beating June 1963 canonical first-person); Simpson↔Annelle Ponder (the SCLC Citizenship School supervisor beaten in Winona); Simpson↔June Johnson (14-year-old fellow Winona beating victim, lifelong friend); Simpson↔Lawrence Guyot (the SNCC MFDP chairman beaten separately at the same jail); Simpson↔Septima Clark / Myles Horton / Guy and Candie Carawan (Citizenship School / Highlander staff who trained Simpson June 1963); Simpson↔Worth Long (SNCC oral historian / folklorist); Simpson↔Dr. Ernst Borinski (Tougaloo German-Jewish-refugee sociology professor); Simpson↔Bernice Johnson Reagon ("walk into the storm" lyric Simpson cites); Simpson↔Chaney/Schwerner/Goodman (Simpson was at the Oxford OH Freedom Summer orientation when the news broke); Simpson↔Lillie Ayers / Jake Ayers (Mississippi HBCU equity-suit family Simpson worked with via Kellogg).
