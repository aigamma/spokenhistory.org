### 27. Dion Diamond

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Dion Diamond_interview_20250704_192332/`
**Interviewer / Date**: David Klein — Sunday 13 December 2015, Washington DC. Cameramen John Bishop, Guha Shankar.
**Subject**: Dion Diamond (b. ~1941, Petersburg, VA) — Howard University 1959+, founding member of NAG (Nonviolent Action Group); led the **Glen Echo amusement park desegregation campaign** summer 1960 (where George Lincoln Rockwell's American Nazi Party counter-protested); participated in the May 1961 **Freedom Rides** continuation from Montgomery to Jackson (the "Bobby Kennedy / Mississippi Governor deal" that arrested all Freedom Riders rather than allowing them to be beaten); served on the Parchman Farm death row alongside James Farmer. SNCC field secretary in McComb and Holly Springs MS, then in Cambridge MD (with Gloria Richardson). Arrested in Baton Rouge on Southern University campus December 1961 — charged with "criminal anarchy" (attempting to overthrow the state of Louisiana). Served the longest solitary confinement of his civil rights career in East Baton Rouge Parish jail. University of Wisconsin and Harvard Graduate School of Education. Career in federal Labor Department, anti-poverty / economic development consulting.
