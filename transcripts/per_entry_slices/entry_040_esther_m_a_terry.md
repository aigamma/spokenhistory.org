**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier (Southern Oral History Program at UNC Chapel Hill); videographer John Bishop — 6 July 2011, at Bennett College in Greensboro, NC (Dr. Terry was serving as Provost at the time of the interview)
**Subject**: Esther M. A. Terry (née Alexander) — Provost of Bennett College at the time of the interview (returned 2009); raised in Wise, NC (Warren County, on the VA border) as the youngest of 12 siblings on a family-owned farm; Bennett College class of 1961 (English and Theatre major under Dr. Hobart Jarrett); participant in the February 1960 Greensboro lunch counter sit-ins (the "Bennett Belles" cohort that planned and conducted the boycott in parallel with the more-famous A&T Four); UNC-Chapel Hill MA in English 1962 (one of the early African American graduate students at UNC; assigned a single room after a white roommate-matching error); Saint Augustine's College faculty 1962–67; UMass Amherst PhD in English (under Sidney Kaplan); founding faculty member of the UMass W.E.B. Du Bois Department of Afro-American Studies (one of the first two BA-through-PhD Black Studies departments in the US); department chair / director 1974+; spouse of fellow UMass scholar Eugene Terry.
**Cross-references**: Terry↔Thelwell #34 (Michael "Mike" Thelwell was Terry's foundational UMass colleague in the W.E.B. Du Bois Department of Afro-American Studies; both arrived in the late 1960s as part of Sterling Brown's pipeline of Black graduate students sent to Sidney Kaplan at UMass; both became foundational department members); Terry↔Dr. Willa B. Player (the Bennett president who quietly supported the Bennett sit-in participants and personally drove to the Greensboro jail to vouch for her arrested students); Terry↔Bennett Belles cohort (Roslyn Smith, Gwen Mackel, Shirley Dismuke, Dolores Finger, Gloria Brown, Linda Brown — the network of Bennett women who planned the February 1960 sit-ins in parallel with the A&T Four); Terry↔Julian Bond / MFDP / Atlantic City 1964 (Thelwell's organizing on the Mississippi Freedom Democratic Party challenge that her UMass cohort referenced).

- **Full read**: This transcript was fully read end-to-end. The transcript file contains a notable Whisper artifact: the entire opening sequence (interviewer introduction + first ~4000 words covering family-origins narrative) repeats verbatim mid-transcript before "Let's take this a little quick pause." Player counseled waiting until after Christmas vacation to maximize student-body presence. Pair with any other Bennett alumna transcript and with the A&T Four memoirs.
- **Dr. Willa B. Player canonical first-person**: Terry's account of Player's quiet but firm support — the personal drive to the Greensboro jail to vouch for arrested students; the institutional independence Player asserted as a private-college president — is one of the most-direct first-person sources on Player in the corpus. Pass 2 should add Player to the ground-truth corpus as a canonical Black-women's-college president figure.
- **Sidney Kaplan / UMass Black Studies pipeline canonical first-person**: Terry's account of the Sterling Brown → Sidney Kaplan → UMass-Amherst graduate-pipeline that produced Thelwell, Bernard Bell, Eugene Terry, and Esther Terry as the founding faculty cohort of the W.E.B. - **The "Bennett Belles get insufficient credit" thesis**: Terry's 50th-anniversary 2010 retrospective conversation with her Bennett classmates (Mackel, Dismuke, Finger, Smith, Brown) is the key first-person source for the historiographical correction that the Bennett women planned the sit-ins and recruited the A&T men, not the reverse.
