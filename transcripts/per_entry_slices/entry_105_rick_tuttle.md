**Interviewer / Date**: David Cline (Virginia Tech / SOHP UNC Chapel Hill) — April 11, 2013, Culver City CA
**Cameraman**: John Bishop
**Subject**: Rick Tuttle (b. New Haven CT to a Connecticut Tuttle family dating to New Haven 1638 + Arkansas-side mother — paternal great-great-grandfather Roger Tuttle, Connecticut regiment, wounded at Fort Wagner (the 54th Massachusetts assault canonical site) + died on the Petersburg front in the Civil War). Educated through Plattsburgh NY high school + Wesleyan University (Middletown CT, class of 1962). Active in Wesleyan's Alpha Chi Rho fraternity-national struggle to drop their Christian-only / hidden whites-only membership clauses. At Wesleyan he was deeply influenced by religion-instructor John Maguire's spring 1961 decision to join William Sloane Coffin Jr. on the canonical Freedom Riders (Coffin recruited Maguire and other Wesleyan faculty including Department Chair David Swift after the Anniston AL bus burning) — Tuttle and his roommate had themselves planned to join but missed the bus. Moved to UCLA fall 1962 for graduate work in history under Progressive-Era historian George Mowry; recruited into the LA-area student-civil-rights network by NSA chapter activist Steve McNichols (former Houston Freedom Rider) + Bob Singleton / Helen Singleton (Mississippi Freedom Riders, Parchman 1961) + Bob Farrell (later LA City Councilman). Read journalist John Poppy's canonical *Look* magazine piece on SNCC Mississippi Delta voter-registration (Sam Block + Charles McLaurin + Willie Peacock) and decided to go down. Spring 1963: introduced to Sam Block + Charles McLaurin at the URC (UCLA Religious Conference Center); freshly-elected Congressman George E. Brown Jr. (LA-area Democrat) wrote Tuttle a letter on letterhead establishing him as a voter-registration "observer" so that he would have federal-implication protection from non-official threats. Arrived Greenwood MS June 1963; was sharing a bed with Lawrence Guyot the morning Guyot left to bail out Fannie Lou Hamer + June Johnson + Annelle Ponder from the canonical Winona MS bus-station-arrest jail-beating crisis (June 9, 1963 — three days before Medgar Evers' assassination); witnessed the immediate aftermath of Evers' assassination from inside the Greenwood SNCC office (run by Willie Wazir Peacock during Bob Moses' absence); placed the canonical phone call to Governor Ross Barnett that threatened "major demonstrations in the Delta" if the location of the Winona prisoners was not disclosed — Barnett caved within hours, the prisoners were located at the jail, and Andrew Young flew in (actually drove in from a Birmingham conference) with bail money to retrieve them. Slipped out of Greenwood under a blanket to a Memphis flight (escaping a credible Byron De La Beckwith threat to "go kill the white boy"), reached Atlanta SNCC office, then assigned to Savannah GA where the canonical Hosea Williams / Benjamin Van Clark Chatham County Crusade for Voters night-marches were underway. Arrested as "John Doe, white" with 45+ Savannah marchers including Hosea Williams; charged on multiple peace bonds (Victor Mulling judge); held in solitary then in the white cellblock where Black trustees (including Filipino-American Welterweight boxer Felian Popeye) physically intervened to protect him from the white prisoners.
