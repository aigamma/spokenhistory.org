- **Peekskill Riots Aug-Sept 1949 canonical first-person**: Seeger's first-person account of the canonical Aug 27 + Sept 4, 1949 anti-Robeson concert violence in Peekskill NY — and his canonical 1999 50th-anniversary press-conference revelations that the local Peekskill teenagers were openly told by police to collect stones to throw at the "commies" — is canonical first-person testimony on a pivotal pre-McCarthy-era proto-fascist mob-violence moment that Seeger argues "may have saved America from full-fledged fascism" because the resulting national outrage backfired on the J. - **Highlander 25th Anniversary 1957 canonical first-person**: Seeger's first-person account of being invited by Myles Horton to perform at the canonical Highlander 25th Anniversary 1957 (after Zilphia Horton's death from drinking poison cleaning fluid) — and the canonical photograph with MLK + Abernathy + Rosa Parks + Anne Braden + Seeger that became the canonical "Martin Luther King Communist Training School" right-wing-propaganda billboard — is canonical first-person testimony on a key Movement-iconography artifact.
- ***We Shall Overcome* canonical lyric-and-rhythm provenance**: Seeger's first-person account of the canonical 1946 Zilphia Horton → Seeger → 1960 Guy Carawan + Frank Hamilton 12/8-time-Highlander adaptation → April 1960 SNCC adoption in Raleigh NC chain is canonical first-person testimony on the most-iconic Movement song's adaptation provenance from striking 1945 NC tobacco-workers to canonical national anthem.
- **MLK "Beyond Vietnam" canonical first-person**: Seeger's first-person account of the canonical April 15, 1967 Spring Mobilization at the UN — where MLK delivered the speech ("I have to face the fact that my country is the greatest purveyor of violence in the world") just the day after Riverside Church April 4, 1967 — and Seeger's observation that "I'm sure that LBJ just lost his temper then... he just got boiling mad and finally he slams his fist down and picks up the phone, 'Whoever do what you want'" is canonical first-person testimony on the LBJ-vs-MLK breaking point.
- **Carnegie Hall 1963 canonical first-person**: Seeger's first-person account of the canonical Carnegie Hall concert 1963 where he invited Beacon NY Black and white neighbors to come down and demonstrate the new 12/8-time *We Shall Overcome* — funded by John H. Hammond Jr.'s $100,000 stage-union cash payment (Columbia Records) — is canonical first-person testimony on the cross-class Movement music infrastructure of the early 1960s.
- **Beacon NY Spirit of Beacon Day canonical first-person**: Seeger's first-person account of how the canonical Spirit of Beacon Day — founded after a Beacon HS race riot, started by a widow with 24 churches' participation, evolved into a town-wide annual celebration of multicultural community — is canonical first-person testimony on a working post-civil-rights-era model of community reconciliation that Seeger argues is "the same thing that produced the Civil Rights Movement."
- **Seeger died Jan 27, 2014**: This July 22, 2011 interview captures Seeger at age 92, two and a half years before his death. The Toshi Seeger references throughout are particularly poignant — Toshi died July 9, 2013, six months before Pete.
