**Subject**: Willie "Bill" Leventhal (b. 1946 New York City; raised North Hollywood + East LA + Westchester + South Bay California). Canonical UCLA SCOPE 1965 student volunteer recruited directly by Dr. King at UCLA April 1965; canonical Bibb County Macon GA SCOPE deployment under William P. Randall; canonical Americus GA arrest with John Lewis + 5 other UCLA SCOPE volunteers; canonical eyewitness to the July 28 1965 Americus GA murder of Andrew Whatley; canonical *Scope of Freedom* book editor and SCOPE oral-history archivist; author of the canonical scope-counties-equals-2008-Obama-counties cross-reference (per his conversation with Andrew Young). Family background: father Dr. Bernard Leventhal (chiropractor, WWII vet, Saginaw MI to New York to LA migration 1948); mother (special-education teacher + later USC special-ed faculty); maternal grandfather Morris Segal (assistant superintendent of New York City schools, organized post-WWII GI Bill vocational education); paternal grandparents immigrants who ran shoe stores; lived in Ramona Gardens federal housing project East LA 1948; mother worked Ed Roybal's first city-council campaign. Education: Grant High School North Hollywood (5 pros on his HS baseball team) → UCLA freshman 1965 sociology → MA UCLA sociology. Key formative experiences: age-5 (1951) Jackie Robinson movie (the eternal Leventhal hero); age-10 work-out with Dodgers at Coliseum with Pete Reiser; Westchester Jewish-Catholic anti-Semitism encounters ("kike" word); June 1963 Medgar Evers assassination; SCOPE recruitment April 1965 from UCLA visit by Dr. King; arrested 3 times in Americus including 1 LIFE magazine front-page incident; later college coordinator for the late-1965 SCOPE expansion; later George McGovern 1972 campaign worker; later substitute teacher Merchiston Elementary School East LA (where he grew up); later host of a Civil Rights Concert at the White House recognized as LA Weekly Person of the Year. Canonical post-SCOPE archival work: edited *Scope of Freedom* (the canonical primary source on SCOPE 1965); spent years successfully petitioning UCLA to install a Dr. King memorial plaque commemorating the canonical April 1965 visit (UCLA repeatedly refused for 6 years before finally complying under pressure from NAACP + UCLA President Mark Yudof). Canonical 2009-10 RBI involvement: connected Andrew Young to the MLB Civil Rights Game series. Canonical lifelong Jewish-baseball cultural framework: from Hank Greenberg → Al Rosen → Sandy Koufax → Norm Sherry → Mike Vogel-class amateurs.
