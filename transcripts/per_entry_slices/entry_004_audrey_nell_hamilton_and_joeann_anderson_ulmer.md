**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier (Southern Oral History Program / UNC) — Tuesday 13 September 2011, St. Augustine, FL (Hilton Bayfront)
**Cameraman**: John Bishop. Also in the room: Dr. Robert B. Hayling, Mrs. June Conway, Mr. Purcell Conway.
**Subject**: Audrey Nell Edwards Hamilton and JoeAnn Anderson Ulmer — two of the "St. Augustine Four" teenagers (with Samuel White and Willie Carl Singleton) who sat in at McCrory's lunch counter in July 1963 and refused to sign the parents' demonstration-renouncement form; sentenced to a year+ at the Florida Industrial School for Girls (Ocala) and the Florida School for Boys (Marianna). Their imprisonment under court order — three of the four parents refused to sign — preceded Dr. King's arrival in St. Augustine by ~3 months; Hayling later described their case as the moment that "set the stage" for the 1964 St. Augustine campaign.
