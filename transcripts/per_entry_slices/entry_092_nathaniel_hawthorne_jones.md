### 92. Nathaniel Hawthorne Jones

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Nathaniel Hawthorne Jones_interview_20250705_000925/`

**Interviewer / Date**: Emilye Crosby — December 3, 2015, Mr. Jones's home in Claiborne County MS
**Cameraman**: John Bishop and Guha Shankar
**Subject**: Nathaniel "Nate" Hawthorne Jones (b. 1914, Claiborne County MS — age 101 at interview, the oldest interview subject in the corpus). Note: This is *not* the same person as the recurring Nathaniel R. Jones (NAACP General Counsel referenced in Roxborough #90). Lived all his life on the same land where he was born (his parents migrated from Lorman MS). Family had a 126-acre farm purchased ~1944 from sharecropping savings. Got 6 months of formal schooling per year until age 9, then sharecropping work claimed the rest. NAACP charter member in Claiborne County 1952 under Rev. Martin of Egypt MS (one of the canonical early underground MS NAACP chapters established before the 1955 wave). US Navy 1944-46 (steward's mate → cook; served Hawaii, Majuro Marshall Islands, Saipan, Eniwetok; the 29-Black-stewards hunger strike to be served at the officers' mess succeeded in 3 days). Registered to vote post-Navy in the canonical 1962 Bob Moses Mississippi Constitution literacy-test trio (with Mr. Griffin + Mr. Ellis Collins; the only three who tried; only Jones + Collins passed). Active in Claiborne County NAACP / Charles Evers + Rudy Shields voter-registration. Drove school bus when desegregation happened. Co-defendant in *NAACP v. Claiborne Hardware* (1982 SCOTUS, cross-corpus with Guster #77). Wife was elected to Claiborne County Circuit Court 1971.

#### Notes for Pass 2

- **101-year-old first-person canonical witness**: Jones is the **oldest interview subject in the entire corpus** (b. 1914, interview Dec 3, 2015 at age 101). His first-person testimony spans the canonical 1920s-30s MS sharecropping economy → 1940s WWII Navy → 1952 Claiborne County NAACP charter → 1962 Bob Moses literacy class → 1965-66 Charles Evers / Rudy Shields voter registration → 1971 Black political office (his wife was elected to the Claiborne County Circuit Court) → 1982 *NAACP v. Claiborne Hardware* (Jones was a co-defendant) → Pass 1 audit. This is an unusually long historical-witness span and the entry is canonical-first-person-testimony-rich on virtually every era of MS Movement history.
- **1944-46 Navy steward's mate hunger strike canonical first-person**: Jones's first-person account of the canonical 1944-46 USS steward's mate Black-sailors 3-day hunger strike to be served at the officers' mess (rather than separately) is canonical first-person testimony on a rarely-documented WWII Navy Black resistance episode.
- **Pittsburgh Courier subscription canonical first-person**: Jones's account of buying *Pittsburgh Courier* subscriptions before the Navy + receiving the *Courier* by mail during his 1944-46 service is canonical first-person testimony on the rural-deep-South Black national-paper distribution that the canonical Roxborough #90 and Mary Jones #85 also reference.
- **1962 Bob Moses literacy class canonical first-person**: Jones's account of the canonical 1962 Bob Moses Mississippi Constitution literacy class at the Port Gibson Masonic Temple (where R.L.T. Smith was running for Congress) — and the resulting three-Black-man voter-registration trio (Jones, Griffin, Ellis Collins) where only Jones and Collins passed — is canonical first-person testimony from one of the *three* names attached to the canonical 1962 Claiborne County voter-registration sting.
- **Cross-corpus Guster #77 canonical confirmation**: Multiple cross-corpus confirmations of Guster #77's testimony on Port Gibson Movement leadership (Rudy Shields, George Henry, Rev. Dawson, *NAACP v. Claiborne Hardware*, A-Mart cooperative).
