### 42. Evans Derrell Hopkins

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Evans Derrell Hopkins_interview_20250704_203259/`
**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier (Southern Oral History Program at UNC Chapel Hill); videographer John Bishop — at Hopkins's home in Richmond, Virginia
**Subject**: Evans Derrell Hopkins — Danville VA-born (June 23, 1954); witnessed at age 9 the 1963 Danville mass demonstrations against the segregated A&P, bus lines, department stores, and city library; attended George Washington High School (Danville's previously-white high school) 1969–72 as one of the early Black integration cohort; led the GWHS Black-student walkout 1971–72; joined the Black Panther Party in Winston-Salem NC at age 17 (1971); moved to Oakland summer 1972 to work on *The Black Panther* newspaper under editor-in-chief David Du Bois; covered the 1973 Bobby Seale-for-Mayor campaign as the newspaper's writer and sound man; left BPP in 1974; subsequent armed-robbery conviction (1974) and a second life-sentence conviction (Danville all-white-jury robbery without physical injury); later author of memoir *Life After Life: A Story of Rage and Redemption*.
**Cross-references**: Hopkins↔David Du Bois (W.E.B. Du Bois's stepson; Hopkins's BPP newspaper mentor); Hopkins↔Bobby Seale and Elaine Brown (1973 Oakland mayoral / City Council campaign Hopkins covered); Hopkins↔Big Man Howard #35 and Aaron Dixon #1 / Elmer Dixon #37 (BPP newspaper-cadre cohort 1972–74); Hopkins↔Emory Douglas (same as Howard #35.16); Hopkins↔Maggie Walker (Richmond VA turn-of-century Black banker / Independent Order of St. Luke founder Hopkins is writing about).
