### 37. Elmer Dixon

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Elmer Dixon_interview_20250704_201309/`
**Interviewer / Date**: David Klein (Virginia Tech) — 28 February 2013, in Seattle, WA
**Subject**: Elmer Dixon — younger brother of Aaron Dixon (#1) and co-founder of the Seattle chapter of the Black Panther Party (the first BPP chapter outside California, organized April 1968 after the brothers' return from the Black Student Union conference at SF State and Bobby Hutton's funeral); BSU organizer at Garfield High School; later founder of the Carolyn Downs Family Medical Center (the Panther free clinic that still operates today as a Seattle FQHC) and the People's Wall mural at 20th and Spruce; current interculturalist / diversity consultant (working with SIETAR Europa and Fortune-500 clients) — the "from revolutionary to interculturalist" framework he describes is the central organizing arc of his self-account.
**Cross-references**: Dixon↔Aaron Dixon #1 (Aaron is Elmer's older brother and Seattle BPP defense captain; the two transcripts are paired); Dixon↔Big Man Howard #35 (Bobby Hutton funeral and the April 1968 Oakland BPP-cohort-galvanization event); Dixon↔Larry Gossett (UW BSU co-organizer; later King County councilmember); Dixon↔Fred Hampton (the Chicago BPP chairman whose "rainbow coalition" formulation Elmer cites as canonical pre-Jesse-Jackson origin); Dixon↔Carolyn Downs (the young Seattle Panther whose namesake clinic continues today).
