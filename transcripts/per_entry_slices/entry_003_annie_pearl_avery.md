### 3. Annie Pearl Avery

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Annie Pearl Avery_interview_20250704_172457/`
**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier (Southern Oral History Program at UNC Chapel Hill) — Tuesday 31 May 2011, Selma, AL
**Cameraman**: John Bishop
**Subject**: Annie Pearl Avery — born Birmingham, AL ~1944. Lived with her great-aunt in Pittsburgh during her early childhood (Emmett Till era). SNCC field organizer from 1961; arrested in Marietta GA, Birmingham, Albany GA, Danville VA (90 days in solitary for contempt), Mississippi (Clarksdale), Natchez. Project director in Hale County, AL on voter registration. Arrested on Bloody Sunday — the only marcher arrested that day (her marker that the police's intent was brutalization, not arrest). Marched on March 7, 9, and the full Selma-to-Montgomery final march.

- **"A.G. Gaston" failure**: rendered as "age of gas", "ageless gas", "agentless gas". Same word, multiple Whisper renderings within a single transcript. Pass 2 should catch every variant.
- **Place-name failures**: "Anniston" → "Anderson"; "Hale County" → "Hill County"; "Poplarville" → "popular via"; "Marietta" → "Marriott". Whisper struggles with Southern place names — Pass 2 should cross-reference every capitalized location against a list of Alabama / Mississippi / Georgia civil rights locations.
- **SNCC-internal name index**: this transcript names ~25 SNCC members. Most are correctly spelled by Whisper; the failures cluster around unusual phonetics (Charles Sherrod → Charles Charade; Prathia Hall → Pratya Hall; Bob Mants → Bob Mance; Bernice Reagon → Bernie's Reagan; Wyatt Walker → Why T-Walker / Waiachi Walker). Pass 2 should build a SNCC-member alias dictionary to catch all of these in one regex sweep.
