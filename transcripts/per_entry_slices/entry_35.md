### 35. Elbert "Big Man" Howard

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Elbert "Big Man" Howard_interview_20250704_200707/`
**Interviewer / Date**: David Klein (Virginia Tech) with the Southern Oral History Program at UNC Chapel Hill — 30 June 2016, in Santa Rosa, California. Videographer John Bishop (Media Generation / UCLA); also present Guha Shankar (Library of Congress).
**Subject**: Elbert "Big Man" Howard — Chattanooga TN-born (1938) Air Force veteran (Europe 1956–60); Merritt College student in Oakland alongside Bobby Seale and Huey Newton; one of the six original founders of the Black Panther Party (October 1966); founding editor of *The Black Panther* newspaper; later BPP Deputy Minister of Information under Eldridge Cleaver; international ambassador to Japan (with Roberta Alexander) and to Scandinavian / European BPP support committees; one of the BPP-leadership representatives who entered Attica Correctional Facility during the September 1971 prisoner rebellion (with Bobby Seale and the prisoners' negotiating committee); after his post-BPP retail career at Kmart and Service Merchandise, founded the Police Accountability Clinic and Help Line (PACH) in Sonoma County, CA after the 2013 police killing of 13-year-old Andy Lopez.
**Cross-references**: Howard↔Aaron Dixon #1 and Elmer Dixon #37 (BPP founding-cohort cross-references on Oakland-Seattle organizational coupling, especially the Bobby Hutton April 1968 funeral and the simultaneous-cross-country COINTELPRO 1968 targeting); Howard↔Sam Napier (the murdered East-Coast BPP distribution manager, a key locus of the East/West Coast 1971 BPP split); Howard↔Emory Douglas (BPP Minister of Culture; designer of *The Black Panther* newspaper graphics).
