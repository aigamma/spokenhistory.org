### 81. Maria Varela

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Maria Varela_interview_20250704_234108/`
**Interviewer / Date**: David Cline (Whisper-rendered "David Kline") — June 29, 2016, Pasadena, CA
**Cameraman**: John Bishop and Guha Shankar (Whisper-rendered "Guhashankar")
**Subject**: Maria Varela (b. Jan 1, 1940, Newell PA) — Mexican-American (father from Mazapil, Zacatecas; grandfather mining engineer who chose Colorado to avoid anti-Mexican sentiment); attended Catholic Alverno College Milwaukee 1957-61 under the Young Christian Students movement (which used the canonical "observe-judge-act" Catholic worker-priest pedagogy from the Belgian + French Resistance period). Joined YCS national staff Chicago 1961-63. Attended Port Huron Conference June 1962 (typed the official minutes; helped resolve "authenticity" language). Recruited by Bob Moses + Frank Smith + Bernard Lafayette to Selma fall 1963 to do adult literacy support for Father Maurice Ouellet at St. Elizabeth Mission. Spent winter 1965 in New Orleans learning photography from Matt Herron (canonical SNCC commercial photographer). Became one of nine SNCC photographers. Designed canonical SNCC literacy + economic-cooperative film strips (Batesville Panola County Okra Co-op booklet/film strip + United Farm Workers / Cesar Chavez film strip) — the canonical Black-people-doing-things visual materials for Movement training. After SNCC, moved to New Mexico 1968 to work with Reies López Tijerina + Rodolfo "Corky" Gonzales on the Chicano land-grant movement; was at the 1968 Poor People's Campaign Resurrection City; helped found the Chicano Press Association.

#### Notes for Pass 2

- **Father Maurice Ouellet + Selma Catholic literacy canonical first-person**: Varela's first-person account of being sent to Selma fall 1963 by Bob Moses + Frank Smith + Bernard Lafayette to support Father Maurice Ouellet's adult literacy efforts at St. Elizabeth Mission — and the canonical Mrs. Kathy who could read but didn't believe she could — is canonical first-person testimony on the under-documented Catholic side of the Selma Movement. Father Ouellet was eventually pulled from Selma by his Montgomery bishop because of his Movement support.
- **YCS observe-judge-act → SNCC literacy canonical first-person**: Varela's account of how the Young Christian Students movement's "observe-judge-act" Catholic Action pedagogy (rooted in the Belgian + French worker-priest WWII resistance) became the foundation for her SNCC literacy work — and the canonical "let's reach out and ask the community what they want to learn about" approach — is canonical first-person testimony on the under-documented Catholic intellectual contribution to SNCC.
- **Batesville Panola County Okra Co-op booklets canonical first-person**: Varela's account of designing the canonical SNCC literacy booklets showing Black farmers in Batesville Panola County doing things — and the SNCC organizer who told her to never distribute them at the start of a meeting because attendees would just read the books instead — is canonical first-person testimony on a key Movement educational-materials project. Cross-corpus with Henderson #68 same set (Panola County Black farmer base).
- **SNCC photographers + *This Light of Ours* canonical first-person**: Varela's account of being one of nine SNCC photographers — the only Civil Rights organization with its own photo department, distinct from SCLC's "celebrity photographer" approach — is canonical first-person testimony on the SNCC documentation strategy. *This Light of Ours* (2011 Maurice Berger curator) is the canonical exhibit + catalog.
- **United Farm Workers SNCC-UFW canonical first-person**: Varela's account of SNCC's Ruby Doris Smith Robinson sending 2-3 Plymouth Valiants + CB radios to the UFW (the first Black civil rights organization to materially support UFW) — via Marshall Ganz + George Ballis — is canonical first-person testimony on the under-documented SNCC-UFW solidarity that predates the canonical 1969-72 UFW grape boycott.
- **Reies López Tijerina + Chicano movement canonical first-person**: Varela's account of joining Reies López Tijerina + Corky Gonzales in 1968 New Mexico land-grant movement work — and her eventual 30-year residence in northern New Mexico — is canonical first-person testimony on the post-SNCC Chicano movement organizing.
- **"Black Power did not throw whites out" canonical first-person**: Varela's account that she was sent the canonical post-Carmichael-elected May 1966 letter from Stokely Carmichael + Ruby Doris Smith Robinson telling her she was still SNCC and welcome — and that the "Black Power threw whites out" narrative is *historically inaccurate* — is canonical first-person testimony on a key Movement-history myth correction. She cites the canonical post-decision SNCC staff list of 75 people that included two Chicanos + five white people.
