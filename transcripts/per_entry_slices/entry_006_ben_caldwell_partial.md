**Interviewer / Date**: David Klein — Thursday 11 April 2013, Los Angeles, CA
**Cameraman**: John Bishop
**Subject**: Ben Caldwell — born Deming, New Mexico, 1945; grandfather was a baseball-team-running barnstormer of Black/Latino/Asian/Indigenous players across the Southwest; father was a Marquette-educated railroad foreman. Drafted into the Army 1966, served in Vietnam (Tet Offensive); MFA from UCLA in the cohort known as the **L.A. Rebellion** (alongside Charles Burnett, Haile Gerima, Julie Dash, Larry Clark, Jamaa Fanaka, et al.). Taught at Howard 1980–83; collaborated with Bernice Johnson Reagon at the Smithsonian and Alan Lomax on blues documentation. Founder of KAOS Network in Leimert Park, LA — community media/tech space; hub of Project Blowed (long-running hip-hop open mic). Filmmaker: *Medea* (1973), *I & I* (1979), *Babylon Is Falling* (1980), *I-Phresh* (1989).
