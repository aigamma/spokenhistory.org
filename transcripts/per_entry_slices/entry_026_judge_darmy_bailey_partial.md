**Subject**: Judge D'Army Bailey (1941–2015) — Memphis, TN native; **expelled from Southern University Baton Rouge January 1962** for his civil rights activities; finished undergraduate at Clark University in Worcester, MA (one of the few northern schools that took in expelled Southern Black students); Boston University and **Yale Law School**. Berkeley, CA city council member 1971–73 (with two colleagues; dubbed "the radical takeover" of Berkeley government by the media). Memphis state court judge. **Co-founder of the National Civil Rights Museum at the Lorraine Motel** in Memphis. Author of *The Education of a Black Radical: A Southern Civil Rights Activist's Journey, 1959–1964* (2009).
