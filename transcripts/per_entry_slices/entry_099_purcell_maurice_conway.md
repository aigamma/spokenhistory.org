**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier, Southern Oral History Program at UNC Chapel Hill — Tuesday September 13, 2011, St. Augustine FL (Bayfront Hilton hotel; also present: Dr. Robert B. Hayling, with Conway's wife June joining later)
**Cameraman**: John Bishop
**Subject**: Purcell Maurice Conway (b. 1948 St. Augustine FL), teenage foot soldier in the 1963-64 St. Augustine civil rights campaign. Raised in Lincolnville (the historically Black neighborhood of St. Augustine) by his great-great-grandmother Mrs. Jourdinson (Seminole and Spanish ancestry); his parents George Conway (grocery deliveryman) and Julia Conway (worked at Flagler Hospital, training nurses on pediatrics) and three brothers lived on Scott Street in West Augustine. As a 10-year-old paper boy he discovered the Whites-only Castillo de San Marcos fort customer base; was pushed out by competing white paper boys. As a 14-15 year old he joined the local Florida Memorial College–based youth wing of the Hayling/SCLC desegregation campaign, embraced nonviolence as tactic while admiring Malcolm X / Stokely Carmichael's more direct line. Participated in the St. Augustine Beach wade-in (April-June 1964) where Andrew "Al" Lingo–led group was attacked by a white mob; the second wade-in where state troopers physically beat the segregationist mob into submission to clear the water for the demonstrators is canonical first-person testimony on Florida-state-trooper intervention in the St. Augustine campaign. Continued in the movement until graduation; attended Daytona Beach Junior College (brick masonry program; testified about racial discrimination in certification testing); moved to New York with his wife June after marriage. Worked NYC Post Office five years, then NYPD; named plaintiff in the successful federal lawsuit against the NYC Police Department's discriminatory sergeant + lieutenant promotional exams (the lawsuit produced 300+ minority sergeant promotions and established the legal criteria still used nationally); retired as NYPD lieutenant; later returned to Florida and lives in Palm Coast, currently advocating to reopen the Solomon Calhoun Community Center (formerly the "Little Links" park) in Lincolnville.

**Cross-references**: Dr. Robert B. Hayling (St. Augustine dentist + SCLC field general, also in this corpus); Andrew Young (referenced obliquely); Hosea Williams (SCLC; recurring "Hosea/Ozea/Jose" Whisper error); Martin Luther King Jr.; Ralph Abernathy; Malcolm X; Stokely Carmichael; ACCORD (Accord Civil Rights Movement of Florida) Freedom Trail organizers (Gwendolyn Duncan, Barbara Vickers); James White + Samuel White (high-school classmates); Gail Blattenberg (Princeton NJ college student tutoring program participant); Mrs. Anne Williams (referenced via the Williams/St. Augustine cluster of foot-soldier interviews).
