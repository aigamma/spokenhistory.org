### 30. Dorothy "Dottie" Foreman Zellner (PARTIAL)

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Dorothy Zellner_interview_20250704_194231/`
**Interviewer / Date**: Emilye Crosby — 8 December 2015, Baltimore, MD (at Betty Garman Robinson's home — recorded same day as Robinson #7). With John Bishop and Guha Shankar.
**Subject**: Dorothy "Dottie" Miller Foreman Zellner (b. 14 January 1938, NYC) — daughter of Lithuanian-Jewish father (dentist) and Ukrainian-Jewish mother; grew up on the political far left in McCarthy-era NYC; high school in NYC during the Rosenberg trial. Queens College 1955–59 — editor of the student newspaper. Joined CORE in summer 1960 at the Miami workshop; arrested at the New Orleans Woolworths Canal Street sit-ins (one of the first NOLA sit-ins). Researcher at the **Southern Regional Council** in Atlanta (1961+), where she wrote the SRC's foundational report on the first 18 months of the sit-in movement. Joined SNCC in fall 1961 as Jim Forman's typist; became a key Atlanta-office staffer 1961–67. Spent 17 years in New Orleans / Louisiana (1962–79?). Co-editor of *Hands on the Freedom Plow* (2010) — the SNCC women's anthology. Former wife of Bob Zellner (the first white SNCC field secretary).

**Partial-read flag**: File is ~133 KB. Pass 1 covered the first ~68 KB. The post-1967 years (the Forman psychiatric breakdown, the post-SNCC New Orleans years, the *Hands on the Freedom Plow* book project) are in the unread portion.
