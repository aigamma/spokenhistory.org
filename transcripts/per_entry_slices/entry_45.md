### 45. Freeman A. Hrabowski III

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Freeman A. Hrabowski_interview_20250704_204521/`
**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier (Southern Oral History Program at UNC Chapel Hill); videographer John Bishop — 14 July 2011, at the University of Maryland, Baltimore County (UMBC) campus
**Subject**: Dr. Freeman A. Hrabowski III — Birmingham AL-born (August 1950); precocious child of older educator parents (mother taught math/English in Birmingham city schools, fired earlier in the late 1940s for organizing teachers to demand equalized salaries; father worked steel mill plus moonlight reading/writing for white supervisors and tutoring GED); raised in the middle-class Titusville neighborhood and attended Ullman High School (where the principal George C. Bell was a mathematician, the high school counselor was Reverend John W. Rice — Condoleezza Rice's father, and the alumni network included Alma Vivian Johnson Powell, Colin Powell's wife); foundational participant in the May 2-7, 1963 Birmingham Children's Crusade at age 12 (spat on by Bull Connor; arrested; spent five days in juvenile detention as the leader of a child-prisoner group); Hampton Institute undergraduate 1966-70; University of Illinois mathematics PhD 1975; UMBC faculty 1987+, vice provost, executive vice president, then president 1992-2022 (one of the longest-serving university presidents in the US); co-founder with philanthropist Robert Meyerhoff of the UMBC Meyerhoff Scholarship Program 1989 — the foundational US Black-and-minority-students-in-STEM pipeline that has produced hundreds of PhDs and MD-PhDs over three decades and inspired numerous national replication programs.
**Cross-references**: Hrabowski↔Birmingham Children's Crusade canonical first-person (May 1963 — paired with the September 15, 1963 16th Street Baptist Church bombing through his classmate Cynthia Wesley); Hrabowski↔Reverend John W. Rice (Hrabowski's high school counselor; Condoleezza Rice's father; canonical Birmingham Presbyterian minister); Hrabowski↔Alma Vivian Johnson Powell (Colin Powell's wife; George C. Bell's niece; canonical Birmingham middle-class Black-community connection); Hrabowski↔Reverend John T. Porter (Hrabowski's pastor at Sixth Avenue Baptist Church — the sister-church of the bombed 16th Street Baptist); Hrabowski↔Richard Arrington Jr. (first Black mayor of Birmingham 1979-99; biochemist PhD; canonical Birmingham progress symbol); Hrabowski↔Robert Meyerhoff (UMBC Meyerhoff Scholarship Program co-founder); Hrabowski↔Condoleezza Rice / Connie Rice (Birmingham middle-class Black childhood-friends network).

- **September 15, 1963 16th Street Baptist Church bombing first-person**: Hrabowski's "bye, Franklin" Friday-before-Sunday encounter with Cynthia Wesley, plus his attendance at the funeral with the tie his school principal exchanged with him, is one of the most-personal first-person sources in the corpus on this foundational movement-trauma event. The "hand and ring" identification detail is canonical.

Pair with any Rice / Powell biographical materials.
