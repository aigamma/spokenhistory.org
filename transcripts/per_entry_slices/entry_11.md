### 11. Candie Carawan and Guy Hughes Carawan

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Candie Carawan and Guy Hughes Carawan_interview_20250704_180614/`
**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier (Southern Oral History Program / UNC), with researcher Heidi Hoskins — Monday 19 September 2011, Highlander Research and Education Center, New Market, TN (the post-1971 Highlander location).
**Cameraman**: John Bishop.
**Subjects**: **Guy Carawan** (1927–2015) — folksong revivalist; the man who introduced **"We Shall Overcome"** (adapted from a Charles Tindley hymn and a Highlander union-song variant) to the SNCC founding meeting at Shaw University in April 1960. Highlander music director from 1959 onward. **Candie Carawan** (b. 1939) — Pomona College student who spent spring 1960 at Fisk on the Carleton exchange, participated in the Nashville sit-ins, met Guy at the Highlander April 1960 conference and married him in 1961. Together they documented and amplified the music of the civil rights movement (Folkways recordings, *Sing for Freedom* workshops 1960–65, the *We Shall Overcome Fund* royalties initiative). The transcript includes seven sung performances of canonical movement songs.
