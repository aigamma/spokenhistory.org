### 120. Timothy Jenkins

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Timothy Jenkins_interview_20250705_020732/`

**Subject**: Timothy Jenkins (b. December 30, 1938, Philadelphia PA; d. June 8, 2022 Washington DC), canonical Howard University NAG / NSA Vice-President 1960-61 + canonical 1961 Capahosic Virginia Voter Education Project Kennedy-administration-negotiation co-architect + canonical Nashville first-Freedom-School organizer Aug 1961 + Yale Law School JD + canonical National Conference of Black Lawyers founder + canonical 1965 Voting Rights Act 3-judge-panel amendment lobbyist + canonical Medgar Evers-funeral-aftermath "Stop in the name of Medgar" rooftop intervention preventing the assassination of canonical DOJ Civil Rights Division attorney John Doar by Black-veteran-snipers. Family background: father (Richmond VA native) ran a canonical mixed-clientele Philadelphia barbershop; mother (lifelong Philadelphia housewife + church volunteer); maternal grandfather William Albert Jones (canonical Philadelphia Black Republican + Remember-John-Brown-organization founder + Philadelphia school board member); paternal grandfather Rev. O.G. Jenkins (canonical founder of the Courtland Virginia Normal and Industrial Institute Rosenwald-school + canonical Hood Memorial Church / AME Zion in Richmond — canonical Booker-T-Washington follower + canonical post-Reconstruction Black Republican-Republicanism). Educated through Thaddeus Stevens School of Practice + Central High School Philadelphia → Howard University (full tuition scholarship; entered 1956). At Howard: canonical Little Forum participant (monthly student-faculty book-discussion club in Cook Hall); canonical Sterling Brown / E. Franklin Frazier / Rayford Logan / Harold Lewis / Mordecai Wyatt Johnson mentorship; canonical Howard student body president; canonical Liberation-of-Namibia + canonical anti-Vietnam War SDS engagement; canonical United States National Student Association (USNSA / NSA) International Student Relations Seminar Harvard participant. Spring 1961 elected USNSA National Affairs Vice President (after CIA-affiliated NSA establishment maneuvered to deny him the International Affairs Vice Presidency he had originally sought due to his Civil Rights ties; he discovered post-fact that the canonical NSA international apparatus was CIA-funded). Canonical role as USNSA-VP-as-SNCC-Executive-Committee-member + the canonical Capahosic Virginia post-Freedom Rides Voter Education Project Kennedy-administration-negotiation along with Charles Jones + Charles Sherrod + Chuck McDew (the canonical "Three Charleses + Jenkins" SNCC delegation) + canonical Burke Marshall + Robert F. Kennedy assistants. Organized the canonical August 1961 Nashville first-Freedom-School / SNCC-leadership-seminar at Fisk University (under New World Foundation + Taconic Foundation funding) — bringing canonical Stokely Carmichael + Courtland Cox + Jean Wheeler + others through a crash course on Black history + politics + economics + sociology. Yale Law School 1961-64 + canonical fundraising tours of New England colleges for SNCC + the Freedom Singers + the canonical William Sloane Coffin Jr. Yale-Chaplain-CT-Women's-League-for-Peace-and-Freedom check-delivery to Myrlie Evers after Medgar's assassination (the first out-of-state check Mrs. Evers received). Post-Yale: canonical National Conference of Black Lawyers founder + canonical draft-resistance attorney for SNCC against General Hershey's targeted-prosecution of SNCC activists + canonical 3-judge-panel amendment to the Voting Rights Act of 1965 (against Kennedy/Justice-Department wishes, securing the 3-judge requirement that made voting-rights cases winnable in the 5th Circuit). Canonical Medgar Evers-funeral-aftermath "Stop in the name of Medgar" rooftop intervention preventing the assassination of John Doar by armed Black-Hattiesburg-veteran-snipers. Co-founder of canonical Teaching for Change educational nonprofit + canonical onevotesncc.org / sncc-legacy-project.org civil-rights-memory work.
