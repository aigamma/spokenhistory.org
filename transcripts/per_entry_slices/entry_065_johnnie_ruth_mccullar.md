**Interviewer / Date**: Dr. Hasan Kwame Jeffries (Ohio State University) and Southern Oral History Program at UNC Chapel Hill — Saturday, 9 March 2013, Albany State University, Albany, GA
**Cameraman**: Petna Dadelica (Whisper-rendered "Petna Dalico" — minor uncertainty in spelling but matches David Cline's video-tech crew)
**Subject**: Johnnie Ruth Brawner McCullar — born February 4, 1940, in Terrell County, GA (Sasser community); daughter of Clifford Brawner and Johnnie Lou Brawner. Attended one-room Pinney Grove school, then Dunbar (Sasser, GA), then Carver High School in Dawson. Attended Albany State College 1960–62 (left due to hepatitis from the dorm food + family-care obligations). Became secretary of the Terrell County Movement after returning home; participated in mass meetings at Mount Olive Baptist Church (Sasser, Terrell Co. GA — famously burned to the ground August 1962, one of the canonical Terrell County church burnings that drew federal Justice Department attention). Worked under Terrell County movement president Lucius Holloway alongside Carolyn Daniels (who hosted SNCC field workers including Charles Sherrod, Prathia Hall, Ralph Allen). Mother (Johnnie Lou Brawner) was fired from her Sasser white-school cook job after registering to vote. Later became NAACP deputy registrar in Terrell County (1979–~1996). Brother of Clifford Brawner (transcript #23 — corpus cross-reference!).

- Ralph Allen → SNCC SW Georgia field worker.

- Mount Olive Baptist Church (Sasser, Terrell County) burning, August 1962 — the canonical Terrell County church burning that triggered federal Justice Department investigation.

#### Notes for Pass 2

- **Mount Olive Baptist Church (Sasser, Terrell County) — canonical first-person**: McCullar's first-person account of the August 1962 burning of Mount Olive Baptist Church — "It was like a death that someone you love had died... I said to think that people would be so mean that they were burned for church... It didn't stop anything. It made us more determined" — is canonical first-person testimony on one of the three most-documented Terrell County church burnings of the SW Georgia Movement, along with Mount Mary and Shady Grove. Pair with Charles Sherrod transcript #18 testimony.
- **Terrell County Movement secretary canonical first-person**: McCullar's account of being secretary of the Terrell County Movement under president Lucius Holloway — including her notebook of mass-meeting notes that the Justice Department took back to Washington and never returned — is canonical first-person evidence on Movement record-keeping practices and how federal civil rights investigations gathered intelligence.
- **Mother's firing for voter registration — canonical first-person**: McCullar's account of her mother (Johnnie Lou Brawner) being fired from her Sasser white-school cook job specifically because she became a registered voter — and the second white employer in Albany who refused the first employer's request to fire her in turn — is canonical first-person testimony on the economic-retaliation pattern that the 1965 Voting Rights Act and its enforcement provisions were designed to address.
- **Sister-of-Clifford-Brawner sibling perspective (cross-ref transcript #23)**: This interview is a sibling-perspective companion piece to the Clifford Brawner interview (#23) on the same family's Terrell County Movement involvement.
- **Carolyn Daniels hosting SNCC workers — canonical first-person**: McCullar's testimony that Carolyn Daniels' Dawson, GA home was the primary lodging for SNCC field workers (Sherrod, Hall, Allen) is canonical first-person corroboration of the SW Georgia SNCC organizing infrastructure.
