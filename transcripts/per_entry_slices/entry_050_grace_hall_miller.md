### 50. Grace Hall Miller

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Grace Miller_interview_20250704_210244/`
**Interviewer / Date**: Will Griffin (UNC Chapel Hill SOHP research associate); videographer John Bishop — 9 March 2013, on the campus of Albany State University in Albany, Georgia
**Subject**: Grace Hall Miller — Baker County GA-born (1932), youngest of six in a sharecropping farm family; married Joseph Miller Sr. secretly in 1946 at age 14; widow with five girls plus one unborn son when her husband was shot in the back by his white plantation neighbor George Williamson on March 8, 1965 over a cattle-ownership dispute (Joseph Miller died March 15, 1965; the killer was never tried — three separate attempts, all all-white Baker County juries); subsequent Baker County voter-registration / school-integration leader; her home became the SNCC organizing base for the Baker County movement (Charles Sherrod's SNCC chapter); 32-year Baker County Board of Education member; mother of Shirley Miller Sherrod (the Obama-era 2010 USDA Georgia State Director who was subject to Andrew Breitbart's selectively-edited video controversy; married to Charles Sherrod — same person as transcript #18 in this corpus).
