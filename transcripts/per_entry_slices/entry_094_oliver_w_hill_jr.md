#### Notes for Pass 2

- **Oliver Hill Sr. canonical lineage**: Hill Jr.'s first-person account of his father Oliver W. Hill Sr.'s trajectory from Roanoke VA Pentecost-family foster home → Dunbar HS Washington DC → Howard Law School 1929-33 (under Mordecai Wyatt Johnson + Charles Hamilton Houston, classmate of Thurgood Marshall) → Roanoke 1933 (Depression-era waiter to earn living) → NAACP Legal Defense Fund cooperating attorney → WWII Army (drafted in his 40s) → 1948 Richmond City Council (first Black elected in Deep South since Reconstruction) → *Davis v. Prince Edward County* (Farmville VA 1951) → *Brown v. Board* 1954 — is canonical first-person testimony on the canonical father-of-the-Movement biography that the speaker shaped (cross-corpus with Becton #71 + Williams #72).
- **Barbara Johns April 23, 1951 Farmville VA canonical first-person (second-hand)**: Hill Jr.'s account of how his father took the canonical Davis case despite the LDF having "all the cases they needed" — because Barbara Johns's persistence and the parents' commitment to fight desegregation per se (not just separate-but-equal) — is canonical first-person testimony on a key NAACP-LDF strategic-pivot moment.
- **Richmond Massive Resistance + 1961 desegregation canonical first-person**: Hill Jr.'s first-person account of being in the first group to desegregate Richmond junior high schools 1961-62 (after Pupil Placement Board distance-by-yardstick distinctions had blocked most Black students) — and the canonical surrealist "school deserted, marching out two-by-two from the principal's office" first-day experience — is canonical first-person testimony on Massive Resistance's institutional aftermath.
- **Judge Merhige + Powell recusal canonical first-person**: Hill Jr.'s account of Judge Robert R. Merhige Jr.'s canonical Richmond busing order including the surrounding counties (which would have produced canonical real integration) — and the canonical 4-4 SCOTUS split that blocked it (Lewis Powell recused due to his Richmond school board past, Rehnquist did not despite participating without hearing the original arguments) — is canonical first-person testimony on a key counter-factual moment in school-desegregation history. "Had Powell not recused himself or had Rehnquist recused himself, this might be a different world now."
- **Howard 1967-71 + Black Power transition canonical first-person**: Hill Jr.'s first-person account of attending Howard University 1967-71 during the canonical late-60s Black Power transition — and his own intellectual pivot from "kind of persuaded by [Black Power argument]... mostly it was kind of just rebellion" to Eastern philosophy + meditation + perception psychology — is canonical first-person testimony on the post-MLK-assassination intellectual trajectories of next-generation civil-rights-family children.
