### 98. Phil Hutchings

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Phil Hutchings_interview_20250705_003414/`
**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier, Southern Oral History Program at UNC Chapel Hill — Thursday September 1, 2011, Oakland CA (offices of the Black Alliance for Just Immigration)
**Cameraman**: John Bishop
**Subject**: Phil Hutchings (b. 1942 Cleveland OH), Howard University–era SNCC organizer and SDS-ERAP Newark project lead. Cleveland-born to a postal-clerk father (subject to the Hatch Act; political activity channeled through the St. James Forum church group) and a social-worker / law-graduate mother who served as chief referee of the Cleveland Civil Service Commission under first-Black-mayor Carl Stokes. Howard University 1960–64, where he joined the Nonviolent Action Group (NAG) and Project Awareness (bringing Malcolm X-vs-Bayard-Rustin debate to campus; bringing Herbert Aptheker as the first-ever "known communist" speaker). Arrested April 1961 at the White Rice Inn Chinese restaurant in Baltimore (Maryland desegregation campaign with CORE / Julius Hobson). Summer 1964 fieldwork in MFDP 2nd Congressional District (Ruleville with Fannie Lou Hamer; with Ivanhoe Donaldson and Stokely Carmichael) collecting duplicate voter-registration records for the upcoming Atlantic City challenge. Tear-gassed at Cambridge MD demonstration against George Wallace alongside H. Rap Brown (1964). November–December 1964 worked Robert Kennedy's NY Senate campaign as research / Speakers' Bureau staff, then joined SDS-ERAP's Newark project at Tom Hayden's invitation December 1964; led the Newark community-organizing effort in the Clinton Hill / Central Ward through the 1967 Newark Rebellion; member-elect of the local Episcopal Diocesan Nathan Wright Black Power Conference organizing group. Final SNCC chairman 1968.

**Cross-references**: Stokely Carmichael (Carmichael/Ture #56 cross-corpus); Courtland Cox (#24 in this corpus); Ed Brown (older brother of H. Rap Brown); H. Rap Brown (Hubert Gerold Brown); Fannie Lou Hamer (Hamer cross-corpus); Ivanhoe Donaldson (SNCC cross-corpus); Bob Moses (referenced in MFDP context); Carl Stokes (first Black mayor of Cleveland, 1967); Tom Hayden (SDS founder, ERAP Newark co-lead); Al Haber (SDS founder); Richard Flacks (SDS theorist); Bayard Rustin (canonical, cross-corpus mentor figure); Herbert Aptheker (CPUSA historian); Tom Kahn (League for Industrial Democracy, Shachtmanite); Max Shachtman (Trotskyist tendency-leader); Nathan Wright (Newark Episcopal organizer / 1967 Black Power Conference); Wazir / Willy Peacock (Mississippi SNCC cross-corpus); Hollis Watkins (Mississippi cross-corpus); Reggie Robinson (DC NAG cross-corpus); George Wallace (canonical segregationist 1964 candidate).

- **Status**: Pass 1 PARTIAL — Whisper transcript is approximately 145 KB; Read tool capped at 73 KB (50%). - **Cross-corpus pattern: "Joe Manier" → Joe Mosnier**: This is the canonical recurring SOHP-interviewer Whisper error across all SOHP-conducted interviews in the corpus. Should be added to the audit pipeline as a global rule.
- **Cross-corpus pattern: "Hammer" → Hamer**: Recurring across multiple Mississippi-related transcripts. Should be added to the audit pipeline as a global rule.
- **Hutchings as bridge figure**: This transcript is one of the few in the corpus where the SNCC-to-SDS bridge is articulated by a participant who was simultaneously active in both organizations. The detailed account of how SDS-ERAP modeled itself on SNCC's organizing approach, but pivoted away from "interracial movement of the poor" toward Black-led community organizing in response to the lack of white grassroots participation, is canonical Movement-historiography first-person testimony.
- **Newark 1965-67 organizing**: The Hutchings/Hayden conversation during the 1965 Watts rebellion, leading to a deliberate two-year organizing buildup ahead of the (predicted) Newark uprising of July 1967, is a canonical articulation of how SDS-ERAP organizers operated in anticipation of urban uprisings. The detailed "polarization" / "black-power" strategy applied to Newark's Italian-American power structure is canonical Movement-organizing first-person testimony.
- **Hutchings, Howard NAG, and the 1960 sit-in suppression**: The detail that Howard's NAACP youth chapter was *dissolved* by the parent NAACP organization specifically for picketing the Dimes' Store / Woolworth / Kresge segregation in Cleveland in spring 1960 is a canonical pattern of conservative NAACP-leadership tension with the emerging sit-in generation. This dovetails with the canonical Greensboro / sit-in-movement / Highlander / SCLC tension narratives.
- **MFDP fieldwork detail**: The pre-Atlantic-City voter-registration record-duplication operation in MS 2nd Congressional District (Ruleville with Hamer + Stokely + Ivanhoe Donaldson) is canonical Atlantic-City-challenge preparation logistics that may not be well-documented elsewhere.
