### 43. Frankye Adams-Johnson

**Interviewer / Date**: Emily Crosby (Southern Oral History Program at UNC Chapel Hill); videographer John Bishop — 6 December 2015, in Jackson, Mississippi
**Subject**: Frankye Adams-Johnson — Pocahontas MS-born sharecropper's daughter; moved to Jackson at age 12; joined the North Jackson Youth Council of the NAACP through her god-brother Cleveland Donald (later the third Black student to enroll at Ole Miss after Meredith and Cleve McDowell); participated in the May 28, 1963 Jackson high school protest march that landed at the Jackson Fairgrounds in mass-arrest holding pens ("concentration camp" comparison echoed by Medgar Evers); witnessed Medgar Evers' assassination summer; struck across the back by a Jackson police officer's club at the Evers funeral march; March on Washington August 1963; Tougaloo College 1964 pre-freshman summer (Freedom Summer); moved to New York 1967; co-founded the White Plains BPP chapter 1968; transferred to Brownsville Brooklyn as officer-of-the-day; one of the few BPP women who lived underground (four years, including giving birth to her youngest daughter underground); current Jackson MS resident.
**Cross-references**: Adams-Johnson↔Medgar Evers ("the closest I came to having a father"); Adams-Johnson↔Cleveland Donald Jr. (third Black student at Ole Miss); Adams-Johnson↔Anne Moody and John Salter (the May 28, 1963 Jackson Woolworth's lunch-counter sit-in protagonists; Adams-Johnson's march two days later was in response); Adams-Johnson↔Pearlena Lewis (Tougaloo Nine; Jackson Woolworth's sit-in); Adams-Johnson↔Jesse Harris (the SNCC Jackson trainer after Evers's assassination); Adams-Johnson↔Annie Devine / Fannie Lou Hamer / Victoria Gray (the three MFDP women who became her role models); Adams-Johnson↔Phillip Gibbs and James Earl Green (Jackson State killings May 1970); Adams-Johnson↔Benjamin Brown (1967 Mississippi National Guard killing near Jackson State).

#### Notes for Pass 2

- **Full read**: This transcript was fully read end-to-end.
- **Jackson MS Fairgrounds mass-arrest canonical first-person**: Adams-Johnson's account of the May 28, 1963 Jackson high school protest march that ended with hundreds of students rounded up and held in livestock pens at the Mississippi State Fairgrounds — and her use of Medgar Evers' "concentration camp" framing for those conditions — is one of the most-direct first-person sources on this foundational Jackson MS movement-trauma event.
- **Medgar Evers as foundational-mentor first-person**: Adams-Johnson's framing of Evers as "the closest I came to having a father" is one of the most-direct personal-first-person sources on Evers as mentor figure (rather than martyr figure) in the corpus.
- **The under-documented Jackson State killings (Gibbs/Green, May 14-15, 1970) canonical first-person**: Adams-Johnson's reference corresponds to the parallel-but-largely-forgotten companion event to Kent State (May 4, 1970). Pass 2 should add Phillip L. Gibbs and James Earl Green to the ground-truth corpus.

Pass 2 should add Brown to the ground-truth corpus.
- **BPP woman living underground canonical first-person**: Adams-Johnson's account of four years of underground life — including giving birth to her youngest daughter underground in a white-women's safe-house network — is one of the rarest first-person source-types in the corpus.
