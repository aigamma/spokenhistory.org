### 53. Gwendolyn Zoharah Simmons (born Gwen Robinson)

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Gwendolyn Zoharah Simmons_interview_20250704_211220/`
**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier (SOHP at UNC Chapel Hill); videographer John Bishop — 14 September 2011, on the campus of the University of Florida in Gainesville
**Subject**: Dr. Gwendolyn Zoharah Simmons (born Gwen Robinson) — Memphis-born; first in family to attend college (Spelman; full scholarship); recruited into SNCC at Spelman by Howard Zinn / Staughton Lynd / Vincent Harding 1962-63; Mississippi Freedom Summer 1964 Laurel MS SNCC Project Director at age 19 (after Lester McKinney's pre-existing arrest forced her promotion); stayed 16 months in Laurel; foundational Atlanta Project SNCC member 1965-66 — co-drafter of the Black Power position paper later credited solely to Stokely Carmichael; expelled from SNCC 1967 with the Atlanta Project on a $3,000-check money-flow dispute; American Friends Service Committee Project on Government Surveillance and Civil Liberties 1970s associate director (FOIA-driven Cointelpro documentation of BPP / AIM / Brown Berets / Crusade for Justice); subsequent Sufism (mystical Islam) under M.R. Bawa Muhaiyaddeen 1971-86; longtime UF Religion Department faculty.
**Cross-references**: Simmons↔Mrs. Eberhart Spinks ("I've been waiting on you all my life" canonical 1964 Laurel-host opening); Simmons↔Stokely Carmichael / Atlanta Project (canonical Black Power position-paper drafting; same era as Patton #52); Simmons↔James Forman / Ruby Doris Smith Robinson (SNCC Atlanta office leadership pair); Simmons↔Bob Zellner (#30) and the 2010s SNCC-reunion reconciliation about the 1966 white-organizers-out-of-SNCC decision; Simmons↔Fred Hampton (canonical Chicago BPP assassinated December 4, 1969; Simmons interviewed his bodyguard for AFSC Cointelpro project); Simmons↔Pol Pot / Phnom Penh (canonical AFSC delegation to Cambodia 1979 — Simmons saw the mountain of skulls and bones); Simmons↔Vincent Harding's "Veterans of Hope Project" (the Iliff School of Theology civil-rights-veterans oral history project).
