Canonical Johns Island SC + Wadmalaw Island SC + Sea-Islands-Movement leader; canonical 1969 Charleston Hospital Workers Strike on-the-ground organizer who set up the back-channel between trustees and Local 1199 with white VP William Huffitz; canonical Korean War wounded combat infantry vet (lost-most-of-his-foot to a booby-trap-trip-flare Dec 26 1951, treated 4-months Japan-hospital but returned to Korea front-line; canonical first-sergeant "you-come-up-here-I-blow-your-brains-out" racism incident). Canonical Esau Jenkins lifelong protégé / Highlander Folk School 1959 trainee (under Septima Clark + Myles Horton + I. DeQuincey Newman + Esau Jenkins) — present at the canonical SC-state-police raid of Highlander Sept 1959 that resulted in the Highlander-property seizure. Canonical break from Esau on nonviolence after Korean War trauma + alignment with Stokely Carmichael / Malcolm X circle and Otis Robinson "I AM SOMEBODY" Black Muslim Charleston (Robinson is canonical pre-Jesse-Jackson source of "I AM SOMEBODY" pre-1968). Canonical 1968 Stokely Carmichael Johns Island visit that catalyzed the Saunders-led District-9-school-board challenge ("we want 3 Blacks on board or we hold our own elections"); resulted in 1 Black appointee + Esau Jenkins appointed as deliberate-divide-and-conquer (officials knew Esau and Saunders were already in conflict). Family + early career: parents on Johns Island sent him to NYC; grandfather farmer + canonical self-taught land-surveyor with marked-tree boundary system; grandfather died 1943 when Saunders was 7-8; canonical John's Island 75-80% Black community with "stay away from white men" elder counsel; canonical Mr. Julius Walsh mattress factory foreman 20 years — boss permitted Saunders's movement work + installed personal telephone at Saunders's sewing machine. Canonical Concerned Citizens of Charleston founder; canonical Low Country News Letter founder (after taking over Dr. Howard Levy's Columbia press when Levy went to prison Vietnam-refusal court-martial); canonical Sea Island Sickle Cell Anemia Program founder 1970 (still running 16% of trades; budget cut 75% under recent administration); canonical 1980 SC state senate run (NAACP + I. DeQuincey Newman opposed him because they wanted Newman to be the first Black SC state senator). Canonical Charleston sanitation department strike second-wave organizer post-hospital-strike. Canonical 1969 Gullah-recognized-as-language legislative win.

- **Smithsonian-grade significance**: William Saunders (1935-2017) is one of the canonical-most-important Charleston SC Sea-Islands civil-rights organizers — a generation lower than Esau Jenkins but more militant + the canonical 1969 Charleston Hospital Strike on-the-ground organizer. His first-person account of the canonical 1969 strike — particularly the canonical back-channel with MUSC white VP William Huffitz that resolved 8-of-10 demands when official negotiations had failed — is canonical first-person testimony on labor-organizing methodology that has been little-documented elsewhere.
- **Canonical "I AM SOMEBODY" pre-Jesse-Jackson origin first-person**: Saunders's first-person account that "I AM SOMEBODY" originated with Otis Robinson (Charleston Black Muslim minister) at the canonical April 4 1968 200-person hospital-workers meeting — not with Jesse Jackson — is canonical first-person testimony at odds with the standard Jesse-Jackson attribution. This is canonical first-person testimony on a famous-phrase-attribution-revision.
- **Canonical Highlander Sept 1959 raid first-person**: Saunders's first-person account of being present at the canonical 1959 SC-state-police raid of Highlander Folk School that resulted in the property seizure + Septima Clark arrest + the breaking-open of cars-trunks-with-keys-they-forced-Saunders-to-surrender is canonical first-person testimony on a canonical movement-training-center attack.
- **Canonical Korean-War-racism first-person**: Saunders's first-person account of his white first-sergeant pointing a .45 at him and saying "if you come up here I blow your brains out" + the canonical post-Korea segregated-train-coach incident + the canonical Columbia SC Greyhound bus-station "boy, what's wrong with you, you don't belong in here" — collectively constitute canonical first-person testimony on the Korean War-era integration-on-paper-only racism that Saunders described as "I have never been an American in America, I have always been a second-class citizen."
- **Canonical 1969 Strike-resolution mechanism first-person**: Saunders's first-person account of how the Strike was resolved — Hugh Lane Sr. contacted a Nixon-cabinet member who cut MUSC's $75M HEW grant + the Saunders-Huffitz back-channel that resolved 8-of-10 demands in a Moe's Corner secret meeting + the Governor McNair fake-telegram-betrayal incident that led to ~200 arrests but exposed McNair's bad faith — is canonical first-person testimony on the actual institutional mechanism that ended the Strike (Saunders's argument: "you would find nowhere where anybody in Charleston got credit for doing anything good with that strike — it was SCLC and the union — but we did the stage, the prop").

DeQuincey Newman + Robert Ford actively opposing him because they wanted Newman (who would become first Black SC state senator 1983-86) — is canonical first-person testimony on intra-Black-leadership conflict over who-gets-to-be-first in post-Voting-Rights-Act state legislatures.
- **Canonical lifelong-anti-integration-but-pro-desegregation framing first-person**: Saunders's first-person account of his 1965-onward opposition to school-busing integration ("America needed integration, not Black people") + his preference for the original Brown ruling's neighborhood-school principle over the eventual Swann busing remedy — is canonical first-person testimony on a Black-Power-era political position that runs orthogonally to standard civil-rights-movement narratives.
