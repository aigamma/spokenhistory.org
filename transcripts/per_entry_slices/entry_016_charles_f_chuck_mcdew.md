### 16. Charles F. ("Chuck") McDew

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Charles F. McDew_interview_20250704_182513/`  
*Layer 5 removed 2 low-impact phantom rendering rows (whisper renderings not present in raw).*
**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier (Southern Oral History Program / UNC) — Saturday 4 June 2011, Albany, GA (Albany State University, during the 53rd Reunion of the Southwest Georgia Civil Rights Movement). Cameraman John Bishop.
**Subject**: Charles "Chuck" McDew (1938–2018) — born Massillon, OH; football star raised in a steelworker-union town with strong immigrant culture; sent by his father to attend HBCU South Carolina State College in 1959 to experience Black professional culture. Arrested in Sumter, SC on Thanksgiving 1959 (after striking back at the cop who hit him) — three arrests in two days converted him to the cause. Led the Orangeburg, SC student movement (800+ arrests). **Second chairman of SNCC** (succeeding Marion Barry, October 1960; preceding John Lewis, 1963). Witness and architect of SNCC's earliest strategic decisions — the choice of voter registration in the Black Belt counties precisely because that's where the long-tenure Southern senators (Stennis, Eastland, Byrd, Talmadge) drew their power.
