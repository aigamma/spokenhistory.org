### 10. Calvin "Cal" Luper

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Calvin Luper_interview_20250704_180332/`

**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier (Southern Oral History Program / UNC) — Tuesday 24 May 2011, Oklahoma City, OK (the Luper household). Cameraman: John Bishop.
**Subject**: Calvin Luper — son of **Clara Luper**, the Oklahoma City NAACP Youth Council advisor who organized the **first organized sit-in of the modern civil rights movement** at the Katz Drug Store on Main & Broadway, OKC, on **19 August 1958** (a year and a half before Greensboro, six months after Wichita). Calvin was one of the original child participants (he was eight on the day of the first Katz sit-in). The interview covers his childhood inside the OKC Youth Council, the family discipline framework that kept the teenage participants both nonviolent and academically focused, and his lifelong co-hosting of *The Clara Luper Show* on Oklahoma City radio.
