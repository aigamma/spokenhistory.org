### 98. Phil Hutchings

**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier, Southern Oral History Program at UNC Chapel Hill — Thursday September 1, 2011, Oakland CA (offices of the Black Alliance for Just Immigration)
**Cameraman**: John Bishop
**Subject**: Phil Hutchings (b. 1942 Cleveland OH), Howard University–era SNCC organizer and SDS-ERAP Newark project lead. Cleveland-born to a postal-clerk father (subject to the Hatch Act; political activity channeled through the St. James Forum church group) and a social-worker / law-graduate mother who served as chief referee of the Cleveland Civil Service Commission under first-Black-mayor Carl Stokes. Howard University 1960–64, where he joined the Nonviolent Action Group (NAG) and Project Awareness (bringing Malcolm X-vs-Bayard-Rustin debate to campus; bringing Herbert Aptheker as the first-ever "known communist" speaker). Arrested April 1961 at the White Rice Inn Chinese restaurant in Baltimore (Maryland desegregation campaign with CORE / Julius Hobson). Summer 1964 fieldwork in MFDP 2nd Congressional District (Ruleville with Fannie Lou Hamer; with Ivanhoe Donaldson and Stokely Carmichael) collecting duplicate voter-registration records for the upcoming Atlantic City challenge. Tear-gassed at Cambridge MD demonstration against George Wallace alongside H. Rap Brown (1964). November–December 1964 worked Robert Kennedy's NY Senate campaign as research / Speakers' Bureau staff, then joined SDS-ERAP's Newark project at Tom Hayden's invitation December 1964; led the Newark community-organizing effort in the Clinton Hill / Central Ward through the 1967 Newark Rebellion; member-elect of the local Episcopal Diocesan Nathan Wright Black Power Conference organizing group. Final SNCC chairman 1968.
