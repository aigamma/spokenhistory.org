### 86. Mateo Camarillo

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Mateo Camarillo_interview_20250704_235443/`

**Subject**: Mateo Camarillo (b. 1941, Tijuana, Mexico — same year US WWII entry; mother Rufina born in Campo CA, two uncles died in WWII; father from Nuevo León, Mexico) — immigrated to San Diego 1950. Hoover HS, SDSU graduate 1962 (track scholarship); Viva Kennedy Club 1960 founder. Army 1965-67 (Germany — Boy Scouts of America Transatlantic Council Scout Master). Welfare Department social worker; founded San Diego Trabajadores de la Raza (national Latino social workers org). Executive Director of Chicano Federation (San Diego County multicultural umbrella) 1972-76; sued San Diego County for employment discrimination (longest-running US civil rights case at the time, expanded to include African Americans, Asian Americans, Filipinos). McDonald's franchisee from 1976 (third application accepted after Camarillo brought elected officials to the meeting); founded Quetzal Bilingual Communications + half-dozen Spanish-language radio stations 1980s. Senior National Council of La Raza board member (helped negotiate the 1986 Immigration Reform and Control Act with Reagan + Sen. Orrin Hatch + Sen. Ted Kennedy).
