### 20. Clarence B. Jones (PARTIAL)

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Clarence B. Jones_interview_20250704_184745/`
**Interviewer / Date**: David Klein — 15 April 2013, Palo Alto, CA. Cameraman John Bishop.
**Subject**: Clarence B. Jones (b. 1931, Philadelphia) — son of two domestic servants of the J.B. Lippincott publishing family; raised by Irish Catholic nuns in a Cornwells Heights, PA boarding school for "colored boys and orphans" because his parents could not house him while serving. Columbia College ('53), drafted into the Army in 1953, court-martialed and given an undesirable discharge as a security risk; ACLU attorney Stanley Faulkner won him an honorable discharge on appeal. Boston University Law School. **Personal attorney and speechwriter to Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.** from 1960 onward; drafted significant portions of King's "I Have a Dream" speech at the 1963 March on Washington and the Letter from Birmingham Jail's drafting context. Brother-in-law (through his first wife Ann Norton) to the apartheid South African regime's banker (Daniel Krenge De Iongh, treasurer to Hendrik Verwoerd).
