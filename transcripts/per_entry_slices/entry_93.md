### 93. Norma Mtume

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Norma Mtume_interview_20250705_001313/`

**Cameraman**: John Bishop and Guha Shankar
**Subject**: Norma Mtume (b. 1949 San Diego CA, *née* Norma Stoker; later Norma Armour during BPP years; now Norma Mtume after marriage to Omawale Mtume) — canonical LA BPP cadre member; coordinator of the canonical Bunchy Carter People's Free Medical Clinic (32nd and Central, LA, 1970-72); then director of the canonical George Jackson Free Clinic (Berkeley, 1972-74); then Minister of Finance for the BPP National HQ (Oakland, 1974-77 under Elaine Brown). Cal State LA student 1967-69 (Black Student Union). After exiting BPP in 1977 (following a high-profile Huey Newton-ordered raid + her arrest + 12-13 months in California Institution for Women Frontera CA), built a healthcare nonprofit career: at Los Angeles County Alcohol and Drug Program 1990 → SHIELDS for Families (founded under Drew Medical School auspices 1990-91 → independent 501(c)(3)) → built to ~350 employees + ~$25M budget by 2014 retirement. Now community faculty at Charles R. Drew University of Medicine and Science.

#### Notes for Pass 2

- **LA Bunchy Carter People's Free Medical Clinic canonical first-person**: Mtume's first-person account of becoming the canonical Bunchy Carter People's Free Medical Clinic coordinator at age 19-20 in January 1970 (after the canonical Dec 8, 1969 LA police raids that injured her husband Albert Armour) — and self-teaching the medical operations of a free clinic via Marie F. Branch + Merck Manual + UCLA pre-med volunteers — is canonical first-person testimony on the BPP 10-Point-Program Point-4 ("decent health care") implementation in the immediate post-Hampton-and-Clark-murder period.
- **George Jackson Free Clinic Berkeley canonical first-person**: Mtume's first-person account of running the canonical Berkeley George Jackson Free Clinic 1972-74 — collaborating with the Berkeley Free Clinic + Women's Health Collective + Haight-Ashbury Free Clinic + UC Berkeley Black Health Science Caucus + Community Services United — and her own canonical 1972-73 cervical-cancer-survival via the clinic's self-pap-smear training program — is canonical first-person testimony on the Bay Area BPP-allied free-clinic ecosystem.
- **BPP Minister of Finance 1974-77 canonical first-person**: Mtume's first-person account of taking over BPP National HQ finances under Elaine Brown in 1974 — including the canonical 10% income-tithe system that funded the canonical BPP school + breakfast + clothing + housing programs — is canonical first-person testimony on the institutional financial sustainability mechanisms of the post-Huey-exile 1974-77 BPP.
- **Huey Newton Cuba-return + 1977 exit canonical first-person**: Mtume's first-person account of being expelled from the BPP after refusing to take the fall for a Newton-ordered killing-the-prosecution-witness operation (which led to her own arrest, conviction, and 12-13 months in California Institution for Women Frontera) — is canonical first-person testimony on a tragic late-BPP episode that is rarely told in the canonical literature.
- **SHIELDS for Families canonical first-person**: Mtume's account of building SHIELDS for Families from a half-million-dollar-trailer-on-Drew-Medical-School-land 1990 startup to a 350-employee, $25M-budget canonical 2014 LA substance-abuse / mental-health / housing services nonprofit — explicitly modeled on the BPP survival programs — is canonical first-person testimony on the post-BPP healthcare-nonprofit life trajectory that several BPP cadre members followed.
- **Cross-corpus US Organization vs. BPP Jan 17, 1969 UCLA murder canonical first-person**: Mtume's account of being attacked while pregnant by US Organization members during paper-selling — and the canonical Bunchy Carter + John Huggins Jan 17, 1969 UCLA murder by US Organization members (the speaker says Ron Karenga gave the order in collaboration with the police) — is canonical first-person testimony on the canonical US-BPP feud that cross-corpus references in Cleaver #73 and McCarty #89.
