**Subject**: Mildred Pitts Walter (b. Sept 1922, Louisiana; age 90 at interview = 6 months past 90) — canonical Black children's book author + civil rights activist; one of the very few canonical Coretta Scott King Award-winning Black children's book authors of the 1980s-90s. Raised in De Ridder, Louisiana (Beauregard Parish, SW LA); attended Southern University Scotlandville LA (graduated 1944); WWII shipyard work in Vancouver WA (Kaiser Shipyards); moved to Los Angeles 1944. LA City Schools teacher 1955 onward. Married Earl Lloyd Walter (CORE member from 1942; California civil rights leader). LA CORE 1958 onward — bank/grocery-store fair-employment campaigns, hotel/motel test cases enforcing the 1959 CA Unruh Civil Rights Act. Published 14+ children's books beginning *Lillie of Watts* (1969); canonical first-author of the first *Reading Rainbow* book *Ty's One-Man Band* (1980); won the canonical Coretta Scott King Award for *Justin and the Best Biscuits in the World* (1986); won Jane Addams Children's Book Award for various titles. Walked the canonical 1986 American Peace Walk from Moscow.

#### Notes for Pass 2

- **Children's literature canonical first-person**: Walter's first-person account of her trajectory from teacher-who-noticed-Black-children-had-no-books-with-Black-protagonists in mid-1960s LA elementary schools, to author of *Lillie of Watts* (1969) → *Ty's One-Man Band* (1980, first *Reading Rainbow* book) → *Justin and the Best Biscuits in the World* (1986 Coretta Scott King Award) — is canonical first-person testimony on the under-documented Black-children's-literature-as-civil-rights-work trajectory that paralleled the canonical Black-Studies-curriculum work in higher education.
- **De Ridder LA + Carter G. Woodson canonical first-person**: Walter's first-person account that the only new book she ever had in her segregated 1930s De Ridder LA high school was Carter G. Woodson's *The History of the Negro* — and her teachers' canonical use of Woodson's history to instill "we were not inferior" in their students — is canonical first-person testimony on the canonical Carter-G.-Woodson-as-curriculum-substrate role in pre-*Brown* Black-school pedagogy.
- **Elmina Castle + "I am not African and I am not American" canonical first-person**: Walter's account of being mistaken for white at the Ghanaian Elmina Castle slave-trade fortress (where the Ghanaian guide responded "she's a white woman") — and her resulting epiphany "I am not African and I am not American... I have a heritage of Africa and I'm born in America... I am unique" — is canonical first-person testimony on the post-diaspora identity-reconciliation moment that many Black Americans report after Ghana pilgrimages but is rarely articulated this clearly.
- **LA CORE hotel/motel test cases canonical first-person**: Walter's first-person account of LA CORE 1959 test-case enforcement of Jesse Unruh's Civil Rights Act 1959 — including the canonical "Walter family with suitcase + child applies first, gets denied; white couple applies after, gets a room" sting operation — is canonical first-person testimony on the under-documented LA West-Coast direct-action test-case infrastructure.
- **Carson CA 1960s housing-tract picket canonical first-person**: Walter's account of the canonical Carson CA housing-tract picket with the Holocaust-survivor co-picketer "Herbert" — and the canonical "ovens too good for niggers" KKK-Nazi-uniformed counter-protesters who showed up — and the Black lone-voice "Oh Freedom" song that grounded the marchers — is canonical first-person testimony on the West-Coast convergence of Holocaust survivors and Black civil rights activists in 1960s housing-rights organizing.
- **"People of Color" nursing terminology canonical first-person**: Walter's account of helping coin the canonical "People of Color" terminology (replacing "minorities") in the 1970s Western Interstate Commission for Higher Education (WICHE) nursing-education-improvement project led by Dr. Marie Branch — is canonical first-person testimony on the 1970s coinage of a key Movement-language term that has since become broadly accepted.
- **Soviet peace walk + Kofi Awoonor + Coretta Scott King Award trade-off canonical first-person**: Walter's account of being awarded the Coretta Scott King Award in 1986 *while* on the canonical Soviet-American Peace Walk through the USSR — and her decision to send a letter of acceptance rather than return early to attend the ceremony — is canonical first-person testimony on Black writers' international peace organizing during the late-Cold War period.
