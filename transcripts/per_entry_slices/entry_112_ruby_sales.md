**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier — Monday April 25, 2011, Atlanta, GA (probably the SpiritHouse Project office)
**Cameraman**: John Bishop
**Subject**: Ruby Nell Sales (b. 1948 Columbus GA), canonical Lowndes County AL SNCC field organizer summer 1965, present at the Aug 20, 1965 Cash Store killing of Jonathan Daniels in Hayneville AL (Daniels physically pulled Sales backwards to save her life from Tom Coleman's shotgun blast at point-blank range; Coleman then killed Daniels instead). Family background: father (Bonnard Sales) born to a woman who learned to read in slavery or just after + had been a teacher; father moved from LaGrange GA → Birmingham AL as an itinerant Baptist preacher with 13 children; father served in CCC + WWII + Korean War as an Army chaplain (post-Truman's integration order family lived on Fort Benning GA, where they experienced canonical Army-base-integrated facilities at odds with the surrounding off-base segregated Columbus GA society); mother (a Fletcher by maiden name) deeply confrontational with white retail clerks who disrespected her. Educated through Carver Heights Elementary School / Mother Mary Mission Catholic → Carver High School Columbus GA, where principal S.P. Charleston + home teacher Marian Armstrong cultivated a canonical "counterculture of education" that Sales credits as the Smithsonian-grade backbone of post-Reconstruction Black Southern leadership. Entered Tuskegee Institute (now University) Fall 1963 at age 16 via the canonical Southern accelerated-college admission program; arrived as a self-described "beatnik wannabe" intending to skip the Civil Rights Movement → drinking wine at the local pub + quoting Allen Ginsberg + planning Greenwich-Village future → galvanized by Stokely Carmichael's canonical 1965 Tuskegee speech + Lowndes County resident-organizers' visit + the canonical March 1965 Edmund Pettus Bridge mass-march aftermath where Sales (and other Tuskegee students) were trapped in the Capitol-Selma church under police siege without food, no chariot of God came, and Sales lost her Southern Baptist religion + began contextualizing the world in social and economic terms. Joined SNCC Lowndes County project under canonical project director Silas Norman + canonical organizer Stokely Carmichael; lived in the canonical Hubbard / Hulett / Jackson / Maul Lowndes-County freedom houses; canonical first-day-of-voter-registration episode where Stokely told the gun-drawing sheriff who threatened to "blow his brains out" that "hell will be an integrated hell tonight". Present at the Aug 14, 1965 Fort Deposit AL demonstration that resulted in the canonical Hayneville jail-confinement; released Aug 20, 1965 in canonical "they want us to leave" warning-sign-of-trap manner; walked with Jonathan Daniels + Father Richard Morrisroe + Joyce Bailey to the Cash Store; survived Coleman's shotgun by Daniels' physical intervention; testified at Coleman's all-white-jury murder trial against the canonical Alabama hostile-cross-examination tactic of casting her as a sexual "whore" (Coleman acquitted). Post-trial: graduated Princeton + Manhattanville; founded canonical SpiritHouse Project; canonical lifelong articulator of the "Southern Freedom Movement" framing (vs. the "civil rights movement" framing) + canonical critic of the "dismembering" of Movement-historiography.
