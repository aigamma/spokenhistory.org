#### Notes for Pass 2

- **Full read**: This transcript was fully read end-to-end (not partial); the file is comparatively short.
- **Hattiesburg / Vernon Dahmer canonical first-person**: This transcript is the most-direct first-person source in the corpus on the January 10, 1966 KKK firebombing of the Dahmer home, the years-long voter-registration struggle that preceded it, and the *U.S. v. Lynd* litigation that established the 18-plaintiff registration relief order. Pair with Holloway #33 for the broader Hattiesburg context.
- **U.S. v. Lynd canonical first-person**: Ellie Dahmer's account of going to the Lynd registration window, being denied, and later testifying via affidavit in the Justice Department's case, is the most direct first-person source in the corpus on this foundational pre-Voting-Rights-Act federal voting-rights case. Pass 2 should treat as canonical.
- **The blackout-curtains and sleeping-in-shifts sequence**: Ellie Dahmer's description of how the Dahmer family lived under nightly KKK threat for years before the attack — sleeping in shifts, taking down see-through curtains, keeping the porch light off — is one of the most-vivid first-person accounts of domestic-life-under-terror in the entire SNCC oral history corpus. Pass 2 should preserve this thematic unit intact.
- **Ladner sisters cross-reference**: Both Dorie Ann and Joyce Ladner are confirmed by Ellie Dahmer as having grown up in the Kelly Settlement / Palmer's Crossing area and worked with the Dahmer NAACP branch as teenagers. This grounds the later joint "Ladners" interview (#~75–80 in the alphabet) in the Forrest County movement family-network context.
