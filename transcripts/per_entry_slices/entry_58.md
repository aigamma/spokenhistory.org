**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Jamila Jones_interview_20250704_213219/`
**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier (SOHP at UNC Chapel Hill); videographer John Bishop — 27 April 2011, at the Art More Hotel in Midtown Atlanta

#### Notes for Pass 2

- **Full read**: This transcript was fully read end-to-end.
- **The "We Are Not Afraid" verse canonical first-person**: Jones's account of being at Highlander Folk School at age 14 when Tennessee state police turned off the city lights during a movie night — and her adding the "We Are Not Afraid" verse to "We Shall Overcome" in the dark (while one of the policemen came up to her shaking and asked "could you not sing so loud?") — is one of the most-direct canonical first-person sources in the corpus on the foundational *We Shall Overcome* anthem-building process. Pass 2 should treat this as canonical for any *We Shall Overcome* citation.
- **Montgomery Gospel Trio canonical first-person**: Jones's account of the foundational late-1950s student-led "Montgomery Gospel Trio" at Alabama State Laboratory High School — singing freedom songs at the MIA mass-meeting cycle when adult feuds had fractured Black-adult organizing — is canonical first-person material on the often-overlooked youth-led component of the post-1957 Montgomery Movement.
- **The Antoinette Carson "stole the car" Freedom Rider pickup canonical first-person**: Jones's account of teenage Antoinette Carson stealing her grandfather's car (with chalk-marks on the garage floor to put it back exactly) to drive the Montgomery youth to Atlanta to pick up the Freedom Riders May 1961 is canonical first-person material on the under-documented teen-improvisation aspect of the Montgomery Freedom Rider rescue.
- **Rosa Parks as NAACP Youth Council mentor canonical first-person**: Jones's account of Mrs. Parks's home being the foundational NAACP Youth Council meeting site — with Kool-Aid and Kool-Aid cookies and readings from the National NAACP papers — is canonical first-person material on Parks-as-organizer (rather than Parks-as-tired-seamstress) framing.
- **Harambee Singers canonical first-person**: Jones's account of co-founding the Harambee Singers with Bernice Johnson Reagon and Matti Casey Pierce post-SNCC-Freedom-Singers is canonical first-person material on the foundational Pan-African / Black Power-era music-organizing transition.
- **Doodle Bird canonical first-person**: Jones's recollection of Reverend Solomon S. Seay Sr.'s "doodle bird" stand-your-ground analogy — "Your house is on fire and your children's burning; back, back, doodle, back, back" — during the May 1961 First Baptist Church mass-meeting overnight standoff with the surrounding mob is canonical first-person material on the Reverend Seay leadership style.
