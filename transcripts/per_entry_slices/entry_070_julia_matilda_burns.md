**Cameraman**: John Bishop, Media Generation
**Subject**: Julia Matilda Burns (born to Margaret Griffin and Trevenu Griffin) — leading Holmes County, Mississippi Movement activist. Family moved at age 3 from Greenwood (Leflore County) to Humphreys County to take advantage of the WWII-era Farm Security Administration government-purchased-land program ("Farm and Home Administration") that yielded her family 123 acres. Lost her mother at age 13; became part-farmer, part-housekeeper as the eldest daughter. Attended Womack and Hodge consolidated school, then Humphreys County Training School in Louise, MS, graduating 1954. Attended Mississippi Valley State (founded 1950 in Itta Bena under President Dr. James Herbert White); graduated 1959 in English major. Lost her teaching job under pressure for husband's SNCC organizing in Tchula, MS (Holmes County); came back to Marshall High School Chula MS teaching 1967. Was a Movement-affiliated school board member 1971–76 with Tchula colleague Dr. Arenia C. Mallory (the canonical Saints Industrial School president, cross-ref Amos C. Brown #2!). Sons among first Black children to integrate Tchula Consolidated School 1965 (which was set on fire by segregationists; rebuilt; set on fire again — fully resegregated Black after whites pulled out to form the Cruger Tchula Academy). Worked with University of Illinois anthropologist Dr. Demitri Shimkin on the Holmes County Health Research Program that produced *The Extended Family in Black Societies* (1978).

#### Notes for Pass 2

- **Mileston FSA / Mileston Community Center canonical first-person**: Burns's first-person account of her father's 1940s purchase of 123 acres of FSA-purchased Mileston-adjacent land — and Abe Osheroff's 1965 building of the Mileston Community Center where Henry and Sue Lorenzi (later Sojourner) headquartered the Holmes County SNCC project — is canonical first-person testimony on the *land-precedes-Movement* substrate of Holmes County's unique organizing power. The same FSA-purchased Black land pattern is documented in Henderson #68 same iteration (Section 7 / Olive Ray in Panola County).
- **Tchula school integration + arson canonical first-person**: Burns's testimony that the Tchula Consolidated School was set on fire after Black students first entered fall 1965, rebuilt, set on fire a second time, and then completely abandoned by white families who formed the Cruger-Tchula Academy — is canonical first-person testimony on one of the most extreme white-flight resistance patterns of post-Brown Mississippi school integration. Her son was in the first integrated 1st grade class with Christopher Epps (canonical later MS DOC commissioner).
- **Movement-affiliated school board canonical first-person**: Burns's election to the Holmes County School Board (1971–76 approximately) as a Black Movement-affiliated candidate against a white incumbent who died the night before the election — alongside Dr. Arenia C. Mallory — is canonical first-person testimony on the post-1965 Voting Rights Act Black officeholding wave that the Mississippi Movement directly produced.
- **Demitri Shimkin Holmes County Health Research canonical first-person**: Burns's role as secretary for the University of Illinois Holmes County Health Research Program that produced *The Extended Family in Black Societies* (Shimkin, Shimkin, Frate, eds., Mouton 1978) is canonical first-person testimony on an under-known academic-Movement collaboration that documented 6-year-old children with hypertension and the FSA-land-cooperative-extended-family economic-resilience pattern.
- **Arenia C. Mallory cross-corpus**: Mallory appears in both this transcript (as Burns's school-board colleague who told her "you're sitting up here trying to get everything black folks") and Amos C. Brown #2 (as the first Saints Industrial School principal who descended from MS Governor Edmund Noel). This is the first cross-corpus appearance of Mallory's name and confirms the canonical alias.
- **Henry Lorenzi "you understand people" canonical first-person**: Burns's recall of Henry Lorenzi telling her "Ms. Burns, you understand. People are not understanding people. Be patient" is canonical first-person testimony preserving Lorenzi's organizing style — a unique Whisper transcript record of an utterance not preserved elsewhere.
