### 1. Aaron Dixon

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Aaron Dixon_interview_20250704_170306/`
**Interviewer / Date**: David Klein (Virginia Tech) and the Southern Oral History Program at UNC Chapel Hill — 11 May 2013, Seattle, WA
**Cameraman**: John Bishop, Media Generation / UCLA
**Subject**: Aaron Dixon, founding captain of the Seattle chapter of the Black Panther Party (April 1968 onward), the first Black Panther Party office outside the state of California. Memoir context: family migration narrative, mid-1960s political awakening, time in jail during the King assassination, baptism into the Black Panther Party under Bobby Seale, Seattle chapter organizing through 1969, the "Portchop Hill" stand-off with Seattle Police, internal purges, breakfast-for-children program.

- **Compound merge errors**: "Eldritch John Huggins" is a Whisper sentence-merge artefact. Pass 2 should look for similar mergers, especially around comma- or conjunction-joined names.
- **Family-names class**: Whisper-renders of family proper nouns from speaker's oral history (Miljert, Roy Sledge, Josephine Sledge, Brooksville) cannot be ground-truthed against `civil_rights_facts.json`. Flag once, do not re-investigate; reviewer can ask the family or check census records out of band.
