### 32. Dr. Doris Derby

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Dr. Doris Derby_interview_20250704_194657/`
**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier, Southern Oral History Program at UNC Chapel Hill — interviewed at Georgia State University, Atlanta, GA (inaugural year of the Civil Rights History Project, ~2010–2011 era)
**Subject**: Doris Derby — Bronx-raised cultural anthropologist (Hunter College, undergraduate; later Ph.D.), NAACP youth member from age 16, 1960 NAACP-sponsored summer in Nigeria (the John F. Kennedy "Airlift Africa" generation), summer 1962 SNCC work in Albany, GA on the recommendation of Charles Sherrod, 1963 Mississippi adult literacy project under Bob Moses, co-founder of the Free Southern Theatre with John O'Neal and Gilbert Moses (October 1963), Mississippi Head Start lead-teacher and trainer 1965–67, Poor People's Corporation / Liberty House handicraft co-op organizer through the late 1960s, Southern Media documentary filmmaker, lifelong photographer and visual artist documenting the Mississippi movement.
**Cross-references**: Derby↔Cotton (#29; both in the Mississippi adult-education / SCLC Citizenship School orbit, though through different organizational entry points — Cotton via SCLC Citizenship School and Highlander, Derby via SNCC adult literacy project); Derby↔Sherrod (#18; Sherrod is the SNCC field secretary who routed her from the Atlanta SNCC office to Albany in summer 1962, becoming her entry point into the Movement); Derby↔Zellner (#30; both SNCC field secretaries at the $10/week stipend); Derby↔Forman (James Forman as fellow Atlanta SNCC office figure during her 1962 arrival).
