**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier (Whisper-rendered "Joe Maniere" — recurring) — Tuesday, September 22, 2011, Jefferson Building of the Library of Congress, Washington DC
**Cameraman**: John Bishop; with project manager Khaki Wright (?) of the Smithsonian museum
**Subjects**: Dorie Ann Ladner (b. ~1942, Palmer's Crossing, Hattiesburg MS) and Joyce Ann Ladner (b. ~1944, Hattiesburg MS), sisters from Palmer's Crossing community on the Camp Shelby outskirts. Activated by Emmett Till's murder (Aug 1955 — both sisters scrapbooked the Hattiesburg American coverage). Hattiesburg NAACP Youth Council under Clyde Kennard (jailed for trying to integrate Mississippi Southern College, falsely charged with bootlegging), C.E. Clack, Vernon Dahmer. Met Medgar Evers as teenagers; he became their mentor. Attended Jackson State (expelled with Walter Williams over the Tougaloo Nine demonstration support — prayed-in-defiance moment); transferred to Tougaloo. Lived at 714 Rose Street Jackson Freedom House (the Freedom Riders' canonical address) with Bob Moses, James Bevel, Diane Nash, Lawrence Guyot, Catherine Burks Brooks, Lester McKinney. Dorie was working at Jackson SNCC office during Medgar Evers's June 12, 1963 murder and Lawrence Guyot's Winona beating; Joyce worked NYC SNCC fundraising / March on Washington office (Bayard Rustin's direction, sharing ILGWU apartment with Rachelle Horowitz, later Eleanor Holmes Norton). Joyce later PhD sociology Washington University St. Louis; faculty at Howard University, Hunter College; first woman President of Howard University 1994–95.

#### Notes for Pass 2

- **Hattiesburg / Palmer's Crossing canonical first-person**: The Ladners' first-person account of Palmer's Crossing as a Camp Shelby recreational outlet for Black soldiers (with juke joints, prostitution, bootleg whiskey) — paired with the canonical local NAACP infrastructure under Clyde Kennard, C.E. Clack, and Vernon Dahmer — is canonical first-person testimony on Hattiesburg's distinctive Movement substrate as a moderately liberal Mississippi Black middle-class community (with two HBCUs nearby + military-base economic activity).
- **NYC SNCC office summer 1963 canonical first-person**: Joyce's account of working at the Harlem SNCC office summer 1963 under Bayard Rustin, sharing the ILGWU apartment at 8th Ave and 24th St with Rachelle Horowitz and (later) Eleanor Holmes Norton, and Bob Dylan strumming guitar on their sofa-bed at midnight — is canonical first-person testimony on the March on Washington's logistics infrastructure.
- **Lawrence Guyot Winona June 1963 cross-reference**: Dorie's first-person account of being in the Jackson SNCC office during Guyot's Winona beating June 9, 1963 (with Hamer, Ponder, Johnson, Simpson) — cross-corpus with Guyot #76 same iteration where Guyot describes the same event from inside Winona jail.
- **Medgar Evers June 12, 1963 canonical first-person**: Joyce's account of receiving Ruby Doris Smith Robinson's phone call at the Lake Forest IL student conference informing her of Medgar's murder — and being told "you need to go to Washington to work on the March on Washington... this is what Meg would have expected of you" — is canonical first-person testimony on SNCC's response logistics after Evers's murder.
- **Tougaloo Nine + Jackson State expulsion canonical first-person**: Dorie's first-person account of being expelled from Jackson State (with Joyce and Walter Williams) for praying in solidarity with the Tougaloo Nine — a prayer interpreted as a political statement by Jackson State's matron + Dean Oscar Rogers + President Jacob L. Reddix — is canonical first-person testimony on Jackson State's collaborationist administration's role in suppressing Black college student activism.
- **Mound Bayou Curfew 1962 canonical first-person**: Dorie's account of the post-COFO-founding Aug 1962 caravan from Clarksdale to Jackson — including the late-night Mound Bayou search for Amzie Moore's house, then driving through Ruleville knowing one of Emmett Till's killers was the canonical night watchman — is canonical first-person testimony on the Delta's 1962 organizing infrastructure.
- **Emmett Till scrapbook canonical first-person**: Joyce's account of clipping every Hattiesburg American article on the Emmett Till case in 1955 and keeping a scrapbook (now lost) — and showing the scrapbook to her "play mama" Modell Stewart while crying — is canonical first-person testimony on how the Till lynching crystallized political consciousness in pre-Movement Hattiesburg Black teenagers.
