**Subject**: Roberta "Bird" Alexander (b. December 1946 Berkeley CA), canonical Black Panther Party Black Panther Newspaper editorial staff under Elbert "Big Man" Howard in 1969 + canonical participant in the UC Berkeley Free Speech Movement Fall 1964 + canonical victim of US-State-Department-arranged-via-Spanish-state Vietnam-protest deportation from Madrid April 1967. Family background: daughter of Franklin Alexander (b. 1914 Omaha NE / Sioux City IA / Winnebago Reservation, grandson of an enslaved-1842-born grandfather who later worked the Pony Express + spoke Winnebago / Omaha / Sioux + had 10 children with a Scott Irish woman; Franklin's mother died of TB when he was 15; Franklin himself joined the IWW at age 12 in Minnesota after Swedish-Norwegian striking grain shockers stood up for him on a wage-equity strike; later canonical CIO + ILWU + Civil Rights Congress director in Oakland CA, fighting police brutality + lynching-as-genocide / housing rights; attacked by William Knowland's Oakland Tribune; called before HUAC; his shoe repair shop on the corner across from LA City Hall was shot into one night after his name appeared in the Los Angeles Times) and Frances "Ette" Alexander (a Lithuanian Jewish revolutionary's daughter from Vilna; her father Russian Jewish shoe-repair-owner in St. Louis who disowned Frances when she common-law-married Franklin; her mother left him to stay with Frances; both very progressive culturally-Jewish-but-not-religious garment-worker-class). Roberta was the youngest of three; the family moved around frequently (Perris CA → Venice Beach LA → various LA addresses). Roberta as a teen attended the Anytown / National Conference of Christians and Jews canonical interracial youth camp; her older sister was elected "Mayor of Anytown" before being kicked out when the program director learned her father was HUAC-called. Spanish-major at UC Berkeley fall 1964 → arrested in Sproul Hall during canonical FSM Dec 2-3, 1964 → year abroad Madrid spring 1967 → arrested + deported by Spanish state under direct US State Department orders for organizing the canonical April 28, 1967 anti-Vietnam-War demonstration at the Facultad de Filosofía y Letras (where Roberta gave the keynote speech to 5+ floor of "Yankis fuera de Vietnam" banner-draped students, then was smuggled by Spanish revolutionary students out of the country only to be arrested + jailed + sent to Hendaye on the French border with police machine-guns at each station + the canonical interview at the Hotel George V Paris with CBS the morning after deportation, hosted by canonical UCSB Spanish professor + their study-abroad-program director Carlos Blanco Aguinaga). Joined Black Panther Party 1969 → assigned to the Black Panther newspaper editorial staff under Big Man Howard → typed in/laid out the paper on the Adler Justowriter machine at the South Berkeley national office → traveled to Japan summer 1969 with Big Man on an unsponsored / one-way-ticket grassroots-diplomacy trip funded entirely by paid interviews + facilitated by canonical Ogawa Productions documentary-film company (avoiding entanglement with the canonical Japanese Red Army misinterpretation crowd) → gave canonical speeches at the canonical anti-Tokyo-International-Airport farmer-led demonstrations of hundreds of thousands. Quit BPP late 1969 after canonical David Hilliard sexual-pressure-on-female-cadres episode that protected her only via her relationship with Bobby Bowen.
