### 102. Reverend Harry Blake

**Interviewer / Date**: David Cline (Virginia Tech History Department / Southern Oral History Program at UNC Chapel Hill) — October 3, 2013, Mount Canaan Baptist Church, Shreveport LA
**Cameraman**: John Bishop (Media Generation / UCLA)
**Subject**: Reverend Dr. Harry Blake (b. 1934, Arkansas), Shreveport LA SCLC field staff hired by Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. on March 1, 1960 — arrested on day one of his SCLC employment. Family background: sharecropper plantation childhood across two LA plantations (Ashley Plantation, Tallulah, Madison Parish, northeast LA; then Wood Spur Plantation, Caddo Parish, north of Shreveport). Educated through Florida Memorial College's "one-room church school" plantation school + Bishop College in Marshall TX, where Dr. King was his commencement speaker. Hired to SCLC after Bishop College commencement via Dr. C.O. Simkins (Shreveport SCLC board member, who asked King to interview Blake). Major career incidents: Sept 22, 1963 (Little Union Baptist Church 16th-Street-Baptist-memorial-march planning meeting), Commissioner George D'Artois personally manhandled and beat Blake — along with two unnamed officers — inside the church vestibule with nightsticks, dragging him to the church lawn where they "left him lay [on the] sidewalk" believing him dead. Blake recovered in Dallas TX at Bishop College's relocated campus (under the school president's care) before returning to Shreveport. Forty years later (2003), the city of Shreveport issued a public proclamation of apology to Blake for the assault. Other career milestones: October 12, 1960 assassination attempt (bullet shattered Blake's car radio and made four bullet holes in his coat as he drove home from his rural church); first assignment under SCLC was voter education (teaching the Louisiana voter literacy test: interpret the US Constitution to the registrar's satisfaction + calculate one's age in years/months/days); served as pastor of Mount Canaan Baptist Church Shreveport since 1966 (47 years at the time of the interview); built a 120-unit low-income housing development + a senior citizens tower + a daycare center on city-allocated land via Mayor Calhoun (acquired land for $35,000 reserved for housing/recreation/education/religious uses); founded the Mount Canaan Summer Boys Academy (preventing the "fourth-grade-male-prison-cell" pipeline through homeschool-style intervention).

**Cross-references**: Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.; Dr. Wyatt Tee Walker (SCLC executive director; the canonical "stay here Harry Blake with me" arrest moment); Dr. C.O. Simkins (canonical Shreveport SCLC board member; Blake's mentor / hiring contact); Reverend T.J. Jemison (canonical Baton Rouge 1953 bus boycott leader who modeled the boycott approach for King); Bishop College (Marshall TX / Dallas TX); Meharry Medical College (referenced as "Mahare"); H.L.
