### 115. Samuel Berry McKinney

**Subject**: Rev. Dr. Samuel Berry McKinney (b. December 28, 1926, Flint MI — d. April 7, 2018, Seattle WA), canonical Seattle Mount Zion Baptist Church pastor 1958-1998 + canonical 1968 Liberty Bank co-founder (first Black-owned commercial bank west of the Mississippi). Father Wade Hampton McKinney was the canonical Antioch Baptist Church Cleveland Ohio pastor 1928-Jan 1963 (34½-year pastorate; 3,000+-member peak); paternal grandfather of same name was a Georgia sharecropper from Gainesville GA who later moved to South Carolina + back to Georgia; the canonical "encouraged-to-finish-his-education-at-18-in-the-5th-grade" story is the family pivot. Mother Annie Ruth Berry (b. Birmingham AL; one of 6 siblings; 17-year-old older sister Anita raised her after their mother died when Annie Ruth was 2 + brought her up in the canonical Black-Puritan female-moral-purity tradition); attended Spelman Seminary (became Spelman College 1929) and was one of 13 or 17 women who technically graduated from Morehouse College pre-1929 Spelman-incorporation; later studied at Columbia Teachers College + taught at Jackson State + Roger Williams College Nashville. McKinney himself: educated at Morehouse College Class of 1948 (entered fall 1944 at age 17; canonical Martin Luther King Jr. was his 15-year-old freshman classmate via the Georgia accelerated-college program for WWII enrollment shortfall; both graduated 1948); drafted into the Army Air Corps 1945; trained at Sheppard Field Wichita Falls TX → Yuma AZ → Big Field CA → Tampa FL; canonical 1945 train-route experience of being shipped via the Western route to Yuma + seeing German POWs being served on tablecloths with silverware in a "white dining room" while McKinney + other Black US soldiers in uniform ate from cardboard plates with wooden forks in the "colored dining room" — McKinney's canonical "if I'm 1800 miles from Cleveland I'm going AWOL" moment. Released from Army 1946; back to Morehouse → Colgate Rochester Crozer Divinity School 1948-1951 → first pastorate Olivet Baptist Cleveland → Providence RI Pond Street Baptist 1955-1958 → call to Mount Zion Baptist Seattle 1957-58. November 1961: canonical organizer of MLK Seattle visit (the canonical "we can't host you at First Presbyterian Church Seattle" debacle where the segregationist church board reneged 2 weeks before the event + the canonical 38-39-years-later 1998 public apology by their new minister + the canonical 13th-and-Madison alternative venue rented for $X dollars within hours); MLK's last canonical solo travel anywhere — McKinney quotes King saying that "after his trip here was his last trip anywhere by himself" because of the assassination-attempt risks. Founded the canonical 1968 Liberty Bank Seattle (Black-owned commercial bank in the Central District; subsequently merged with Emerald City Bank; the canonical 24th + Union building was later threatened with closure; McKinney was working on protesting that closure the day after the interview). Co-organizer of the Central Area Civil Rights Committee (CACRC) with then-Bishop John Adams (AME); canonical Frederick & Nelson Seattle department-store integration march. Founder of the Mount Zion Manor low-income-elderly housing development + the Seattle Vocational Institute (the former Seattle OIC — Opportunities Industrialization Center, after Leon Sullivan-style Philadelphia model — that was salvaged via state takeover by US Rep Jim McDermott after Reagan defunded community OICs).
