### 51. Gwendolyn Annette Perkins Duncan

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Gwendolyn Annette Duncan_interview_20250704_210411/`
**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier (Southern Oral History Program at UNC Chapel Hill); videographer John Bishop — 13 September 2011, at the Bayfront Hilton in St. Augustine, Florida
**Subject**: Gwendolyn Annette Perkins Duncan — fifth-generation Lincolnville (St. Augustine FL Black neighborhood) native, born June 28, 1956; daughter of Mrs. Perkins (mother of eight girls, no boys); great-great-grand-daughter of Thomas A. Finley (Carolinas-to-Saint-Augustine migration story); her great-grandmother Abby Finley was a co-founder of St. Cyprian's Episcopal Church Lincolnville; first-hand child witness (age 7-12) to the 1963-65 St. Augustine SCLC campaign led by local NAACP youth-council leader Dr. Robert B. Hayling and the Ku Klux Klan night-marches through Lincolnville; co-founder and chair of the 40th ACCORD organization (commemorating the 40th and 50th anniversaries of the St. Augustine Movement and the Civil Rights Act); secured Northrop Grumman funding for 30 historic Civil Rights Trail markers throughout St. Augustine 2003-2014; co-organizer of the 50th-anniversary commemoration events (2014) with Ambassador Andrew Young and historian Taylor Branch as featured speakers.
**Cross-references**: Duncan↔Dr. Robert B. Hayling (canonical St. Augustine NAACP youth-council leader, dentist, foundational pre-King St. Augustine movement 1962-63); Duncan↔JT Johnson (canonical SCLC organizer); Duncan↔Audrey Nell Hamilton and JoeAnn Anderson Ulmer (transcript #4 in this corpus — the St. Augustine "Foot Soldiers"); Duncan↔Dr. Dorothy Cotton (canonical SCLC Citizenship School director; same as transcript #29); Duncan↔Hank Thomas (canonical Freedom Rider; same as Thelwell #34.12; Thomas's "paternalistic" St. Augustine framing Duncan quotes); Duncan↔Taylor Branch (canonical MLK biographer; *Parting the Waters* / *Pillar of Fire* / *At Canaan's Edge* trilogy).

- **Full read**: This transcript was fully read end-to-end.
- **St. Augustine 1964 movement legacy canonical first-person**: Duncan's transcript is one of the most-detailed first-person sources in the corpus on the canonical 1963-65 St. Augustine SCLC campaign — the foundational pre-King work of Dr. Robert Hayling, the KKK night marches through Lincolnville, the Klan firebombing of Black homes, and the post-Civil-Rights-Act era reflections of the founding 40th ACCORD organization.
- **40th ACCORD canonical institutional first-person**: Duncan's account of founding the 40th ACCORD organization with Cora Tyson Johnson, JT Johnson, Dwight Hines, Sandra Parks, David Nolan, and Jane Billings — and of securing Northrop Grumman funding for the 30 historic Civil Rights Trail markers around St. Augustine 2003-14 — is canonical first-person material on the post-Movement institutional-legacy work.
- **Fort Mose canonical first-person**: Duncan references Fort Mose (1738), the first legally sanctioned free Black settlement in what would become the US, as the canonical reason her Finley ancestors migrated from the Carolinas to St. Augustine. Pass 2 should add Fort Mose to the ground-truth corpus as a foundational pre-Movement Black American institution.
