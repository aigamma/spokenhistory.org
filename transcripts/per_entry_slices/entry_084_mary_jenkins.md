#### Notes for Pass 2

- **Albany Movement MLK office canonical first-person**: Jenkins's first-person account of working in MLK's Albany office (in Dr. William G. Anderson's house, Dec 1961-1962) — answering Dr. King's phones, including being handed a number for Harry Belafonte — is canonical first-person testimony from the office-side of the Albany Movement that Sherrod + the SNCC field workers usually don't describe.
- ***Open Themselves* pictorial history canonical first-person**: Jenkins's first-person account of writing the canonical Albany Movement pictorial history *Open Themselves* (1980s, self-published) — including her note-taking on fans when she ran out of paper at mass meetings — is canonical first-person testimony on Movement documentation work that has been less foregrounded in Movement history.
- **Baker County 1958-59 white power canonical first-person**: Jenkins's first-person account of her first teaching job at Baker County (where the white school supervisor — wife of the white school superintendent — fired a young Black male teacher on the spot for an unspecified weekend incident; and later assembled all Black teachers to say "they may make us go to school with you, but I would never invite one of you to my home") — is canonical first-person testimony on the canonical 1958-59 Black-teachers-as-vulnerable-workers pattern that the Brown decision did not protect.
- **Mass meeting near-arrest canonical first-person**: Jenkins's account of standing up to march at Mount Olive Baptist Church when Rev. James Grant stepped out of the pulpit saying "I'm going to do this for myself" — but her husband pulling her out of line at the door because she was carrying her 4-year-old son — and Pritchett deciding not to arrest anyone that day anyway — is canonical first-person testimony on the moment-of-decision dynamics of mass-meeting → demonstration transitions.
