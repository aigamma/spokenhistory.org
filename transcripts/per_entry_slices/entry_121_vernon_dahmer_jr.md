**Subject**: Vernon F. Dahmer Jr. (b. August 27, 1929, Kelly Settlement, Forrest County MS; ~5 miles north of Hattiesburg), canonical son of the canonical Jan 10, 1966 Klan-firebombing-martyr Vernon F. Dahmer Sr. + canonical 1945-onward Forrest County branch NAACP co-founder. Family background: paternal grandfather George Dahmer was a canonical white-passing mixed-race man (his German father Peter Dahmer from Darmstadt + his mixed-race mother Laura Barnes who had been raised by a Mulatto family the Barnes; George Dahmer himself raised on Domstedt family land; later married Ellen Kelly, daughter of canonical Kelly Settlement founder Warren Kelly who was a descendant of canonical antebellum slave-owner Green Kelly + his African slave-mistress Sarah); canonical 12-Dahmer-children family (6 boys + 6 girls), 5 girls + 5 boys surviving + (per the canonical decision by George Dahmer to protect his daughters from canonical white-male-racial-predation) all the daughters + all-but-one of the sons left Mississippi for Chicago (where they passed as white in canonical post-WWI Great Migration assimilation). Vernon Dahmer Sr. was the only son who stayed in Mississippi. Family business: father Vernon Sr. owned ~50+ acres + a 1940-onward school-bus-leasing-to-Forrest-County operation + a canonical 1942 onward country grocery store + canonical 1945-onward small saw mill + canonical 1942 ice house adjacent to the grocery (a chip-from-300-lb-block ice operation that Vernon Jr. ran as a young teen). Speaker is the eldest of 8 children from Vernon Sr.'s first marriage (his mother Lily was Vernon Sr.'s first wife, divorced ~1935 — Vernon Jr. is from the 1928 marriage; Vernon Sr. then married Ellie / Ellen Dahmer in 1952 — the canonical 1966 firebombing widow). Educated through Bay Springs Black School Kelly Settlement (the one-room then multi-room canonical Black school on land donated by his great-grandfather Warren Kelly 1879). Tougaloo College fall 1948 → fell ill, withdrew → Alcorn State College 1949-50 → drafted September 1951 → re-enlisted, made staff sergeant by end of first enlistment → 20-year Air Force career (1951-1971) including Tyndall AFB Florida + France + Cheyenne Wyoming + retired Senior Master Sergeant. Canonical witnessing as a teenager of his father's denied-voter-registration at the Forrest County courthouse where Theron Lynd's predecessor handed Vernon Sr. an index card to "read and interpret" and then claimed Vernon Sr. could not interpret correctly (Vernon Jr. was 16-17 years old at the time and old enough to know his father was being lied about). Canonical 1945-onward Vernon Sr. was one of 9 Black men who founded the Forrest County NAACP branch (canonical mid-1940s secret-meeting-at-night origin). Canonical mid-1950s Vernon Sr. became Forrest County NAACP president (cross-corpus #100 Branch + Smith + #36 Ellie Dahmer; cross-corpus with virtually every MS-Movement transcript that mentions Hattiesburg + Forrest County voter-registration history). Canonical Jan 10, 1966 Klan firebombing of the Dahmer family's then-new home killed Vernon Sr. — Vernon Jr. was in the Air Force on assignment at the time.
