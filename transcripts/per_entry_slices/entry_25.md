### 25. Cynthia and Fletcher Anderson

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Cynthia Baker Anderson and Fletcher Anderson_interview_20250704_191008/`
**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier (with John Bishop videographer and Elaine Nichols, NMAAHC) — date not explicit; Bogalusa, LA, in the Anderson home.
**Subjects**: Fletcher Anderson (b. 1941, Bogalusa) and Cynthia Baker Anderson — Fletcher was a **founding member of the Bogalusa chapter of the Deacons for Defense and Justice** (chartered February 1965 after the model meeting with Ernest Thomas and Fred Kirkpatrick from the Jonesboro chapter). Crown Zellerbach (Bogalusa paper mill) employee; one of the first Black workers to file Title VII employment-discrimination lawsuits. Helped recruit the **first Black Bogalusa city policemen** (his Class of 1959 classmates). Cynthia was a Coventon teacher who survived the period in which her husband was a Deacon and faced multiple police-harassment arrests.
