### 132. Wyatt Tee Walker

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Wyatt Tee Walker_interview_20250705_030007/`
**Interviewer / Date**: David Cline (Smithsonian National Museum of African American History and Culture) — June 20, 2014, Chester VA
**Cameraman**: John Bishop
**Subject**: Rev. Dr. Wyatt Tee Walker (b. August 16, 1928, Brockton MA — corrected from his prior-believed 1929 birth year discovered late in life via his mother's papers; d. January 23, 2018). Canonical SCLC Executive Director 1960-64 + chief architect of Project C (the canonical Confrontation blueprint for the 1963 Birmingham campaign) + canonical typist + co-editor of the canonical *Letter from Birmingham Jail* + canonical March on Washington Southern Regional Coordinator + canonical Anti-Apartheid Movement organizer 1970s-90s + canonical pastor of Canaan Baptist Church Harlem 1967-2004 (37 years) + canonical 1,800-member 80%-tithing-membership Harlem congregation that received over a million dollars in tithes Walker's last two years + canonical 55-book lifetime author. Family: father a Baptist preacher pastoring small South Jersey congregation of ~100 + canonical scholarly Greek-and-Hebrew-daily-reading + canonical "Frederick Douglass portrait on the wall" home environment + canonical "race man" father whose pistol-on-the-trigger incident in a near-Norfolk VA train-station Jim Crow waiting room became Walker's model. Childhood South Jersey segregation experience (his first girlfriend Nancy at age ~5-6 told him she couldn't play with him "because you're colored"; Klan cross-burning at Father Divine headquarters in the same town; canonical "Little Georgia" nickname for the South Jersey segregation pattern). Education: Virginia Union undergraduate (physics + chemistry major, intended pre-med) + Virginia Union Graduate School of Religion MDiv. Canonical 1953-58 Petersburg VA Gillfield Baptist Church (200-year-old Black Baptist congregation) pastor at age 22-30; canonical CORE + NAACP + Petersburg Improvement Association president; canonical Ministerial Alliance integration with Rev. R.G. Williams + Virginia State College chaplain; canonical February 27 1960 Petersburg Public Library Douglas Southall Freeman *R.E. Lee* biography sit-in arrest — Walker + Rev. R.G. Williams chose to stay in jail two days; canonical first-Southern-library-integration-attempt; canonical "I always thought Lee was guilty of treason — I wanted to see the Southern view of why they made him such a hero" library-book-choice. Canonical 1958 SCLC Clarksdale MS organizational meeting joining + canonical 1960-64 SCLC Atlanta Executive Director under Dr. King — built SCLC from a 5-person staff and $57,000 budget into a 100-staff $1M-budget organization in 4 years. Canonical Birmingham 1963 Project C confrontation blueprint architect — spent a year in advance walking storefronts measuring eating-facility seat counts + young-vs-middle-aged-vs-elderly walking times to model the campaign + canonical Bull Connor reactivity prediction. Canonical Children's Crusade Birmingham co-decision-maker with Dr. King + James Bevel. Canonical *Letter from Birmingham Jail* typist + chief editor — was canonical "only one in Birmingham who could make out Dr. King's chicken scratch writing" + canonical "from the edges of newspapers + toilet paper" source-material transcription + canonical "tears of love" → "Letter from Birmingham Jail" title-rename. Canonical 1964 March on Washington Southern Regional Coordinator. Canonical 1964 departure from SCLC after the Board froze his salary at $10,000 while administering a $1M budget with four children — canonical "Daddy King came to my house in Atlanta to beg me to stay" rejection of the Daddy-King-personally-asked-Walker-to-stay-at-$10K offer in favor of a $25,000 NYC publishing-firm marketing-VP offer. Canonical 1967-2004 Canaan Baptist Church Harlem pastor + canonical New York State largest-developer-of-affordable-housing + canonical NY charter-school-movement co-architect with Floyd Flake + Rubén Díaz Sr. (Bronx PR pastor) + Steve Klinsky NYC Charter School #1 co-founder. Canonical 1970s-90s anti-apartheid organizer — 300-member Black-church federation + canonical 22-ministers-to-South-Africa fact-finding visit + canonical Nelson Mandela election worker. Canonical April 3 1968 phone call from MLK the day before assassination (call that Walker missed; canonical "I have always imagined he wanted me to come to Memphis to help with the march that had gone wrong the day before"). Canonical Theresa "Theresa Walker" Walker (Wyatt's wife, present at this interview + canonical Freedom Rider + canonical 1963 Mother's Day Birmingham National Guardsman head-strike attack + canonical East Point GA jail with all 4 children). Canonical 30+ published books + 25 more unpublished + canonical 55-book lifetime count. Canonical post-1968 Maya Angelou friendship + canonical "she ran her fundraising office out of New York 25th Street" Canaan connection.
