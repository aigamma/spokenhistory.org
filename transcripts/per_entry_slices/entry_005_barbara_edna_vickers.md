**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier (Southern Oral History Program / UNC) — Tuesday 13 September 2011, St. Augustine, FL (Bayfront Health Inn)
**Cameraman**: John Bishop. Dr. Robert B. Hayling present in the room.
**Subject**: Barbara Henry Vickers — born St. Augustine, graduated Excelsior High School ~1942; spent WWII as a tack-welder at Boeing in Seattle; returned to St. Augustine after marrying. Beautician with a home salon. Lived directly across Scott Street from Dr. Hayling's house during the 1964 St. Augustine campaign; participated in the kneel-ins at white churches; cooked food for the West Side Boys who guarded Hayling's home from Klan night-riders. Later (post-2004) led the fund-raising drive that erected the Foot Soldiers Monument in the St. Augustine Plaza de la Constitución (2011) — the first public civil rights memorial in the city.
