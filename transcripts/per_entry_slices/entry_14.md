### 14. Cecil J. Williams (PARTIAL)

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Cecil J. Williams_interview_20250704_182131/`
**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier (Southern Oral History Program / UNC) — Thursday 9 June 2011, Orangeburg, SC (the Williams home/studio). Cameraman John Bishop.
**Subject**: Cecil J. Williams (b. 1937) — Orangeburg, SC native; began photographing the South Carolina civil rights movement at age 13 as an apprentice to Sumter photographer E.C. Jones. At 15, became *Jet* magazine's official Orangeburg correspondent. Photographed the Briggs petitioners' meetings in Clarendon County (the case that became *Brown v. Board*), the Elloree Black school teachers' protest resignation in 1956, the Old All-Star Bowling Alley protests that culminated in the 1968 Orangeburg Massacre. Author of multiple books on the South Carolina movement; uniquely positioned as the in-community photographer for events that Black national press could not otherwise reach. Photographed Thurgood Marshall, John F. Kennedy (twice — at JFK's Roosevelt Hotel candidacy announcement and on the *Caroline* plane), and Harvey Gantt's 1963 entrance to Clemson. Graduated Claflin University 1960 in art.
