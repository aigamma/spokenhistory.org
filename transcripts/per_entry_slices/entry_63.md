#### Notes for Pass 2

- **Partial read**: ~58% of the 117 KB transcript was read (cap-truncated). Pass 2 must read the remaining 42% covering Churchville's post-1966 Freedom Library Day School expansion, his ordained ministry, and his contemporary educational work.
- **"Black power earlier than Carmichael" canonical first-person**: Churchville's 1963 SW Georgia SNCC formulation that "the rationale for voting is not to integrate... the rationale for voting is to build a power base so you can elect people who look like you, who will do for you, what people who don't look like you will do" — and his negotiation with Charles Sherrod for a Black SNCC partner so the Black community would not look to the white partner for power — is canonical first-person material that predates Carmichael's June 1966 "Black Power" speech by 3 years. Pair with the canonical Bob Mants / Lowndes County / Sherrod 1965-66 articulation.
- **Buttermilk Bottom NOI Atlanta project canonical first-person**: Churchville's account of organizing a small economic-development cell in Buttermilk Bottom Atlanta — with three NOI professionals (a mathematician, an economist, and a third) — and submitting the proposal to NOI Chicago HQ, only to have it shot down — is canonical first-person material on the under-documented NOI-Atlanta-1964 grassroots-vs-Chicago-HQ tension that paralleled the larger Malcolm-X-vs-Elijah-Muhammad split.
- **Minister Jeremiah X "don't touch him" canonical first-person**: Churchville's account of Minister Jeremiah X pacing my mother's floor pleading "talk to John, don't let him continue with this stuff, he could get hurt" — and protecting Churchville from the Fruit of Islam after Churchville placed Malcolm's picture in the NSM Freedom Library window 1965 — is canonical first-person material on the post-Malcolm-X-leaves-NOI period's intra-NOI tensions and individual-protection-orders.
- **The 1964 North Philadelphia riot canonical first-person**: Churchville's account of the NSM Freedom Library being the only Ridge Avenue storefront not looted during the 1964 North Philadelphia riot — because of the "NSM Freedom Library" signs in the window with the white-gloved hands — is canonical first-person material on the under-documented 1964 Philadelphia riot.
