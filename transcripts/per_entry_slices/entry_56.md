### 56. Jack Greenberg

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Jack Greenberg_interview_20250704_212505/`
**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier (SOHP at UNC Chapel Hill); videographer John Bishop — 18 July 2011, in Midtown Manhattan
**Subject**: Professor Jack Greenberg (1924–2016) — Columbia Law School 1948; NAACP Legal Defense and Educational Fund (LDF) attorney from March 1949 (the 5th of only 6 LDF attorneys); co-counsel with Bob Carter and Thurgood Marshall in the Topeka KS *Brown v. Board* trial 1951-52; LDF Director-Counsel 1961-84 (succeeded Marshall when Marshall went to the Second Circuit); founding author of the first modern-era civil rights legal-survey text *Race Relations and American Law* (1959); orchestrator of the foundational ~3,500 civil-rights-movement-arrestee defense-acquittal record 1960-66; co-counsel in foundational US v. Stennis-cert-blocked desegregation case via the ancient common-law writ of certiorari; co-counsel and SCOTUS argument *Swann v. Charlotte-Mecklenburg Board of Education* 1971 (the first Supreme Court substantive school-busing decision); Columbia Law professor 1984-2017.
**Cross-references**: Greenberg↔Thurgood Marshall (the foundational LDF leadership Greenberg succeeded as Director-Counsel 1961); Greenberg↔Bob Carter (co-counsel in *Brown* Topeka KS trial 1951; later federal judge); Greenberg↔A.P. Tureaud (the canonical first Black New Orleans attorney post-Reconstruction; LDF cooperating attorney); Greenberg↔Julius Chambers (the foundational NC Black civil rights attorney; LDF cooperating attorney); Greenberg↔Marian Wright Edelman (the foundational Children's Defense Fund founder; LDF attorney 1963-68); Greenberg↔Herbert Wechsler (canonical Columbia Law professor; "Toward Neutral Principles of Constitutional Law" 1959 — the canonical academic challenge to *Brown*'s reasoning); Greenberg↔Walter Gellhorn (canonical Columbia Law professor; Legal Survey seminar that drew Greenberg into civil rights law 1947).

- **NAACP LDF Director-Counsel canonical first-person**: Greenberg is one of the foundational first-person sources in the corpus on the foundational 1949-84 LDF strategic-litigation operation. Pair with any other LDF/Marian Wright Edelman/Bob Carter materials.
- **Walter Gellhorn / Legal Survey seminar canonical first-person**: Greenberg's account of how Columbia Law professor Walter Gellhorn called his civil rights / civil liberties seminar "Legal Survey" to avoid the McCarthy-era McCarthy taint — and was summoned twice to Congress himself to swear he was not and never had been a Communist Party member — is canonical first-person material on the pre-McCarthy / McCarthy-era civil-rights-as-controversial-subject academic landscape.

Pair with Geiger #54 for cross-corpus connection.
- **Stennis-cert-blocked / Common-Law-Certiorari canonical first-person**: Greenberg's account of how President Nixon, under Senator John C. Stennis's pressure during the armed-services-appropriations-bill blockade, asked the Court of Appeals to send school-desegregation cases back to the district court without deciding them — and how LDF responded by invoking the ancient common-law writ of certiorari (not the statutory certiorari) — is canonical first-person legal-strategy material.
- **Swann canonical first-person**: Greenberg's account that Chief Justice Burger initially voted to uphold the lower-court non-busing order, but found himself outvoted in conference, switched his vote so he could keep the opinion assignment to himself, then assigned the opinion to himself — and wrote the canonical 1971 substantive school-busing opinion — is canonical first-person SCOTUS-procedural material confirmed via Chief Justice Earl Warren's later confidence to Greenberg.
