**Subject**: Peggy Jean Connor — born and raised Hattiesburg, MS (Palmer's Crossing); beauty-shop owner on Mobile Street from age 11 (apprenticed under aunt; graduated Franklin Beauty School Houston TX 1946). Took the canonical Mrs. Tatum (Hattiesburg HS) citizenship class that started her decade-long poll-tax-paying campaign (tried at 21; succeeded only in January 1964 after a court order — 12 days before the canonical Freedom Day Jan 22, 1964 in Hattiesburg). Took **18 people** to the all-white Library Precinct meeting in Hattiesburg in 1964, where she was the only registered voter — and was elected by acclamation to go to the Forrest County meeting. Trained at Dorchester (SCLC Citizenship Education Center) under Dorothy Cotton + Andrew Young 1963-64; taught the Hattiesburg citizenship classes that prepared the canonical 1964 Forrest County voter cohort. **Lead plaintiff in *Connor v. Johnson*** (canonical 1960s-70s Mississippi voting-rights districting case that established the canonical MS-2 congressional district — which elected Bennie Thompson as canonical first Black MS Representative since Reconstruction in 1993). Worked for CDGM (Child Development Group of Mississippi) Head Start program. Co-defendant on Mississippi Freedom Democratic Party financial-secretary lawsuits as executive secretary of the MFDP.

#### Notes for Pass 2

- ***Connor v. Johnson* canonical first-person**: Connor's role as lead plaintiff in the canonical *Connor v. Johnson* MS voting-rights districting litigation — which created the canonical MS-2 congressional district that elected Bennie Thompson as the first Black MS US Representative since Reconstruction in 1993 — is canonical first-person testimony from the Connor whose name is on the SCOTUS case caption.
- **1964 Library Precinct meeting Hattiesburg canonical first-person**: Connor's first-person account of taking 18 Black Hattiesburg residents to the all-white Library Precinct meeting January 1964 (where she was the only registered voter, ruled the only one eligible to vote, and elected by the white Democrats to go to Forrest County) — and the canonical phone call where the white committee chair called Theron Lynd to verify her single registration — is canonical first-person testimony on a pivotal pre-MFDP-formation moment.
- **Hattiesburg citizenship class canonical first-person**: Connor's first-person account of training at SCLC's Dorchester Center under Dorothy Cotton + Andrew Young 1963-64, returning to Hattiesburg, and teaching the canonical citizenship classes at Rev. Carter Walker's True Light Baptist Church (Wednesday nights after prayer meeting) — including the canonical Helen Anderson + Ruth Campbell + J.W. Brown students who were ordered registered by court order — is canonical first-person testimony on the SCLC Citizenship Education program's Hattiesburg implementation.
- **Fannie Lou Hamer "go tell it on the mountain" canonical first-person**: Connor's account of meeting Fannie Lou Hamer for the first time at the canonical Hattiesburg mass meeting (probably 1963) where Hamer wore "a starch and iron dress" and sang "Go Tell It on the Mountain" — is canonical first-person testimony on Hamer's emergence as a canonical Movement singer-orator from the Hattiesburg side of the canonical Movement geography.
- **Atlantic City 1964 + Chicago 1968 canonical first-person**: Connor was elected MFDP delegate to Atlantic City 1964 + Regular Democrat delegate to Chicago 1968 — the canonical *only* MS organizer to traverse both delegations as voting member. Her testimony on the canonical August 1964 "the men wanted to take the two-seat compromise; it was the women who saved us" gendered-split dynamic — including her observation that the men were listening to Adam Clayton Powell while Fannie Lou Hamer + Annie Devine + Victoria Gray refused — is canonical first-person testimony on a key gender-dynamics moment in MFDP history.
- **Vernon Dahmer firebomb murder Jan 10, 1966 canonical first-person**: Connor's first-person account of being among the first people at the Dahmer house the morning after the firebomb attack (Jan 10, 1966) — and visiting Dahmer in the hospital where "he was talking when I left... I guess I might have been home about 30 minutes when... [he was] dead" — is canonical first-person testimony on Dahmer's death from a Hattiesburg-neighbor perspective.
- **CDGM Head Start displacement canonical first-person**: Connor's first-person account of being displaced from her CDGM Head Start position (likely 1966-67) "because they didn't want you to do political work" — and her observation that "somebody would have talked to me" if she had been formally fired — is canonical first-person testimony on the well-documented 1966-68 federal-government pressure that defunded CDGM and replaced it with the canonical Mississippi Action for Progress (MAP).
