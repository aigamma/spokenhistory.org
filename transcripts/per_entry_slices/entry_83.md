### 83. Martha Prescod Norman Noonan

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Martha Prescod Norman Noonan_interview_20250704_234600/`
**Interviewer / Date**: John Dittmer — Monday March 18, 2013, Baltimore, MD
**Cameraman**: John Bishop
**Subject**: Martha Prescod Norman Noonan (b. ~1944 Providence, RI) — daughter of West Indian-descent optometrist (from St. Vincent, naturalized US, threatened with deportation after Progressive Party state-chair role) and an Ann Arbor MI-born mother whose family migrated to Buxton, Ontario (canonical Underground Railroad Elgin Settlement) in the 1850s. Attended Lincoln School for Girls (Quaker prep, Providence; first Black student). Attended University of Michigan 1961-65, joined SDS, attended Apr 1962 SDS-SNCC Chapel Hill meeting, Wayne State summer 1962 (CLR James / George Rawick classes, Bernard Lafayette + Detroit Friends of SNCC support work + bringing Diane Nash to Detroit 9 months pregnant). Summer 1963 worked Charles Sherrod's Albany GA project, then Greenwood MS (Sam Block + Willie Peacock). Spring 1965 - Selma + Tuskegee SNCC project (Pat Gurin Univ. Michigan Black college student aspirations study with Doris Derby + John Bracey). Married Silas Norman (canonical SNCC Augusta GA + Selma project director — cross-ref Varela #81) Fall 1967. Married Jimmy Noonan later. Co-editor of *Hands on the Freedom Plow: Personal Accounts by Women in SNCC* (2010, U. Illinois Press) — the canonical 52-essay SNCC women's anthology.

#### Notes for Pass 2

- ***Hands on the Freedom Plow* anthology canonical first-person**: Noonan's role co-editing *Hands on the Freedom Plow* (2010) — the canonical 52-essay anthology of SNCC women — places her at the center of the late-2000s SNCC women's documentation effort that included Faith S. Holsaert + Judy Richardson + Dorothy Zellner + Betty Garman Robinson + others (all cross-corpus in this audit).
- **Detroit Friends of SNCC + DRUM pipeline canonical first-person**: Noonan's first-person account of working with John Watson + Luke Tripp + Mike Hamlin (the canonical Wayne State Black radical core that became DRUM/League of Revolutionary Black Workers 1968) — in the Detroit Friends of SNCC fundraising effort summer 1962 onwards — is canonical first-person testimony on the rarely-documented pipeline from SNCC Friends chapters to the post-1968 Black Power labor organizing.
- **Diane Nash 9 months pregnant Detroit fundraising summer 1962 canonical first-person**: Noonan's first-person account of Diane Nash speaking in Detroit while 9 months pregnant + going into labor as she left Michigan — "yes my baby might be born in jail, but anywhere a black child is in Mississippi they're in jail already" — is canonical first-person testimony.
- **Mother's RFK phone call canonical first-person**: Noonan's account of her mother (estranged) calling Neil Staebler → Burke Marshall → Robert Kennedy in 1963 to ensure no harm came to her daughter in Albany GA — and Noonan never being arrested again after that call — is canonical first-person testimony on family-back-channel federal intervention.
- **Wayne State summer 1962 / C.L.R. James + George Rawick / facing reality canonical first-person**: Noonan's first-person account of taking C.L.R. James-affiliated classes (Rawick, Faber) at Wayne State summer 1962 while also doing SNCC Friends fundraising work with Bernard Lafayette — is canonical first-person testimony on the under-documented intellectual lineage from C.L.R. James's Trotskyist Facing Reality group to SNCC organizing strategy.
- **Stokely Black Star vs Black Panther canonical first-person**: Noonan's account of preferring a Black Star (Marcus Garvey + Nkrumah Pan-African symbol) over the Black Panther for the Lowndes County Freedom Organization at the canonical 1965 organizing meeting — but not having the nerve to say so publicly — is canonical first-person testimony on a small dissenting voice from the canonical LCFO branding decision.
