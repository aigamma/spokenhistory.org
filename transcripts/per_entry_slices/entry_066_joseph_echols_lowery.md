### 66. Joseph Echols Lowery

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Joseph Echols Lowery_interview_20250704_221334/`
**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier (Whisper-rendered "Joe Mayae" — the canonical recurring SOHP interviewer Whisper-error pattern) and the Southern Oral History Program at UNC Chapel Hill — Monday, 6 June 2011, Cascade United Methodist Church, Atlanta, GA
**Cameraman**: John Bishop, Media Generation
**Subject**: Reverend Joseph Echols Lowery (1921–2020) — co-founder of the Southern Christian Leadership Conference (SCLC, founded 1957 in New Orleans following Atlanta meeting interrupted by the bombing of Ralph Abernathy's church). Pastor of Warren Street Methodist Church in Mobile, AL 1952 onward. President of Interdenominational Ministerial Alliance of Mobile during the Montgomery Boycott. Original SCLC vice president (later president, succeeding Rev. Joseph E. Lowery served as SCLC president 1977–97). Co-defendant in *New York Times v. Sullivan* (Sullivan ad case, decided 1964). Delivered benediction at Barack Obama's first inauguration, January 20, 2009. Friend and contemporary of Andrew Young, John Lewis, and Coretta Scott King.

**Cross-references**:
- Fred Shuttlesworth (Birmingham SCLC co-founder) — recurring across the corpus.
- C.K. Steele (Tallahassee bus boycott leader) — SCLC co-founder.
- Kelly Miller Smith (Nashville SCLC co-founder).
- T.J. Jemison (Baton Rouge SCLC co-founder).
- Ralph Abernathy — recurring across the corpus; co-defendant in *NYT v. Sullivan*.
- Bayard Rustin — recurring; helped draft SCLC founding documents.
- Ella Baker — recurring; SCLC's first effective full-time executive director.
- Bull Connor, George Wallace — recurring antagonists.
- Sammy Davis Jr. (and his wife May Britt) — Lowery testifies how Davis's name on the *NYT v. Sullivan* ad list provoked Alabama jury prejudice because Davis had married a Swedish-blonde white actress.
- Harry Belafonte, Reinhold Niebuhr — co-signers of the *NYT v. Sullivan* civil rights ad.
- The Selma to Montgomery March (March 1965) — Lowery chaired the committee that delivered the petition to Governor George Wallace at the Alabama Capitol.
- Jimmie Lee Jackson (killed Marion, AL February 1965, his death triggered Selma → Montgomery march).
- James Reeb (Selma martyr, March 1965).
- LBJ / 1965 Voting Rights Act, 1964 Civil Rights Act.

#### Notes for Pass 2

- **SCLC founding canonical first-person**: Lowery's first-person account of the SCLC organizing meeting in Atlanta being interrupted by the bombing of Ralph Abernathy's church, and the meeting being adjourned and reconvened in New Orleans (which Lowery says is "the official birthplace of SCLC") is canonical SCLC-founding-narrative material that the larger Civil Rights Movement history sometimes glosses over (most histories cite the Atlanta meeting; Lowery's testimony points to New Orleans).
- **Mobile bus desegregation canonical first-person**: Lowery's account of the Prichard bus route ride with Rev. S.M. McCree (or Seay) — the "by God, if you don't make a move, I'll make a move" white passenger confrontation — is canonical first-person testimony on the Mobile bus campaign, which is much less-documented than the Montgomery or Tallahassee bus boycotts.
- ***NYT v. Sullivan* canonical first-person**: Lowery's account of the Alabama lawyer (Roland) Nachman naming Sammy Davis Jr. in summation to inflame the Alabama jury — because Davis had married a Swedish blonde (May Britt) — is canonical first-person testimony on jury-bias mechanics in segregation-era Alabama courts that shaped the constitutional libel-law landscape (*NYT v. Sullivan*, 1964).
- **Property auction canonical first-person**: Lowery's account of his Mobile YMCA secretary (R.B. DeWitt) bidding $8900 on his car at the *Sullivan* auction and gifting it back to his wife is canonical first-person testimony on the economic-retaliation infrastructure that the Sullivan-era libel suits weaponized against civil rights leaders.
- **Selma march "blue sea" canonical first-person**: Lowery's account of leading the post-Selma delegation up the Capitol steps and the National Guard parting the line of blue-uniformed state troopers ("the blue sea, just like the red sea, they parted") is canonical first-person testimony on the climactic Selma-to-Montgomery moment.
- **Obama 2009 inauguration canonical first-person**: Lowery's account of delivering the benediction at Obama's first inauguration is canonical first-person testimony on the Joshua-generation-to-Moses-generation handoff (his words during the Bronner's Chapel Selma 2007 commemoration with Obama framing this contrast).
- **Whisper-error of note**: "Star Spank Obama" → "Star-Spangled Banner" is one of the most surreal Whisper errors observed in the corpus. The phrase is now an artifact-of-record.
