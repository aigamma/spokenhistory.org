### 103. Robert Bagner Hayling

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Robert Bagner Hayling_interview_20250705_010615/`
**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier, Southern Oral History Program at UNC Chapel Hill — Wednesday September 14, 2011, Bayfront Hilton Hotel, St. Augustine FL
**Cameraman**: John Bishop
**Subject**: Dr. Robert Bagner Hayling (b. November 20, 1929, Tallahassee FL — d. December 20, 2015 Cocoa Beach FL), the canonical leader of the St. Augustine civil rights movement 1963-65. Father Charles Hayling Sr. was a Grenadian immigrant who taught Industrial Education (tailoring) at Florida A&M for 32-34 years; mother of New Orleans Creole extraction. Children Charles Hayling Jr. (older brother), Francis Hayling (sister, deceased), Yvonne Hayling Clark (younger sister, Sarasota). Educated through Florida A&M (BA); served in US Air Force 1951-55 — basic training at Lackland AFB / TX, medical-technician training at Sheppard AFB Wichita Falls / TX, Officer Candidate School Class 52D (Dec 1952, commissioned 2nd Lt), assigned to Wright-Patterson AFB Dayton OH Biomedical Laboratory as Aeromedical Researcher specializing in toxicology. Declined promotion to Captain in exchange for early release to attend Meharry Medical College dental school (Nashville 1955-60). At Meharry his Jurisprudence/Dental-Law instructor was Z. Alexander Looby (canonical Nashville civil rights attorney whose home was dynamited April 1960; Hayling's dorm wing felt the blast) — Hayling marched with John Lewis, Diane Nash, C.T. Vivian, James Bevel in the Nashville student march on City Hall after the Looby bombing. Began dental practice in St. Augustine FL Dec 1960 (taking over from late Dr. Rudolph Gordon's practice, where Gordon's widow Mrs. Gordon helped him build a 50-60% white clientele built on Gordon's Cuban-cigar-smoking goodwill). Became NAACP Youth Council advisor 1963 (after Rev. Thomas Wright was forced to leave St. Augustine for sponsoring the youth movement); developed strategy of pressuring the 400th-anniversary federal $400,000 grant by demanding Black appointments to the celebration committees; was outfoxed by LBJ-press-aide George Reedy in the canonical "we'll be seated in the Grand Ballroom" / actually-seated-in-the-alcove-above-the-Grand-Ballroom Vice Presidential dinner debacle of spring 1963. Suffered the canonical Sept 1963 Klan-rally beating (with James Jackson, James Hauser, Clyde Jenkins) by Connie Lynch / J.B. Stoner / Hoss Manucy's klansmen — beaten with axe handles and baseball bats, then piled like cordwood on the rally stage where the announcer said they would soon "smell a nigger burn" via gasoline ignition. Rescued by Florida Highway Patrol auxiliary forces summoned by Episcopal priest "Channing" of Daytona Beach (likely Rev. William Sloane Coffin-affiliated Daytona Episcopal priest who infiltrated the rally with a hidden recorder). Treated initially at Flagler Hospital ER, then mortician's hearse to Brewster Hospital Jacksonville for Black-doctor care; lost 11 teeth, sustained right-arm injury threatening his dental-practice career. Broke from NAACP late 1963 after Roy Wilkins threatened to revoke the St. Augustine charter — Hayling preemptively mailed the charter back to Wilkins via Mrs. Elizabeth Hawthorne secretary. Pivoted to SCLC via CK Steele bridge, recruiting Yale Divinity School students (via Rev. William Sloane Coffin Jr.) for spring break 1964 tutoring; recruited Mrs. Mary Peabody (mother of MA Governor Endicott Peabody) for the Mar 1964 arrest at the Monson Motor Lodge; brought Dr. King to St. Augustine May-June 1964 where the canonical James Brock "acid in the swimming pool" photograph drove the final push for Senate passage of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 on July 2, 1964. Moved to Cocoa Beach FL after July 1965 (five-year LA-state tuition obligation completed); built a NASA-Cape-Kennedy-supported practice; received the St. Augustine Award of Valor July 2, 2011 (3 months before this interview); Dr. Hayling Place street naming June 20, 2003.
