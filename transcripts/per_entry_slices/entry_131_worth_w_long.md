**Subject**: Worth Westmoreland Long Jr. (b. January 15, 1936, Durham NC at Duke Hospital; d. November 13, 2021). Canonical SNCC field secretary 1962-67 + canonical Alabama state-coordinator 1965 + canonical SNCC ethnomusicologist who recorded the canonical Bernice Johnson Reagon + Hollis Watkins + Sam Block + canonical Mississippi Freedom Schools folk-singing tradition + canonical Mississippi Freedom Information Service archivist + post-1972 Atlanta-based historian-ethnomusicologist + canonical 2009 Reece Museum East Tennessee State University Selma 45-year archive curator. Family: Rev. William Worth Long Sr. (AME Zion presiding elder of the Durham District for 30+ years, b. Mecklenburg County NC; managed 10 churches via quarterly-conference circuit-rider model); Gertrude Watson Long (mother, b. Chatham County NC; widowed first husband in a mining accident; bought Durham duplex; worked Liggett & Myers tobacco factory + then Duke-family domestic). Father's first marriage was to Minnie Banger (Free African family of Chatham County NC; canonical family deeply researched by Long via Carter G. Woodson's *Free Negro Heads of Families* — "free Negroes" census research; Hood Theological Seminary at Livingstone College Salisbury NC training year for father); after Minnie died father married Gertrude (Long's mother). Childhood community: Formosa Avenue Durham NC (canonical Black-elite community adjacent to North Carolina Central University development; neighbors included Mr. Henry McKinley Michaux insurance, Mr. C.C. Spaulding founder of North Carolina Mutual Life Insurance Company, the Sharon piano-studio family, Dr. Holmes German-and-French-professor neighbor who became Long's classical-music mentor); canonical Durham Black community Hayti (H-A-Y-T-I) self-sustaining district with the Bull City Drug Store, Rialto Theater, Booker T Theater, Service Printing Company. Canonical neighborhood mentor: Dr. Holmes who gave Long admission to North Carolina College for the Marian Anderson + Paul Robeson + Philippa Schuyler classical-concert series. Canonical childhood neighbor + later family lawyer + later national CORE chairman: Floyd McKissick — Long's civics teacher at Hillside High School Durham who applied to UNC Law School and forced NC to build a separate Black law school at NCC rather than integrate UNC. Canonical Hillside High School (Durham NC HBCU-feeder where most teachers had Master's degrees from University of Chicago + Columbia University, paid for by NC's segregation-era subsidies-rather-than-integrate policy). Canonical multi-family-of-North-Carolina-Central-girls-dormitory-overflow boarding house — Long grew up with up to 8 college-women boarders simultaneously. Education: Hillside HS Durham → Morehouse 1956-58 → 1957-59 USAF Korean DMZ deployment → Atlanta University master's + doctoral coursework. Canonical SNCC entry: Worth Long joined SNCC in summer 1962 (Mississippi); central early-1960s figure across the canonical Mississippi-Alabama-Arkansas Movement; canonical 1963-65 Forrest City Arkansas project + Phillips County Arkansas voter registration + canonical 1964 Mississippi Summer Project + canonical 1965 Alabama state-coordinator (Selma + Lowndes County + Black Belt). Canonical post-1972 Atlanta-based historian-ethnomusicologist + 1976 *In Search of a Common Ground: Conversations with Erik H. Erikson and Huey P. Newton* co-editor + canonical *Voices of Freedom: An Oral History of the Civil Rights Movement from the 1950s through the 1980s* (1990, with Henry Hampton + Steve Fayer) collaborator + canonical 2010 *Hands on the Freedom Plow: Personal Accounts by Women in SNCC* contributor. Canonical Joe Stadium NCCU football + last-exam-program admittance pathway. Canonical 2015-onward Black Lives Matter elder + Mississippi Movement-veteran community archivist.
