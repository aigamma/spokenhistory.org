#### Notes for Pass 2

- **Full read**: This transcript was fully read end-to-end.
- **Richmond 34 February 22, 1960 sit-in canonical first-person**: Grinnell is one of the Richmond 34 — the foundational Virginia Union and Virginia Commonwealth students who sat in at Thalhimer's lunch counter on February 22, 1960, three weeks after Greensboro. Pair with any Charles Sherrod transcript (#18 in this corpus) for the most complete Richmond 34 picture.
- **Charles Sherrod boyfriend canonical first-person**: Grinnell's account of dating Charles Sherrod at Virginia Union (where he was in the School of Religion 1958-61 before he went to Albany GA as a SNCC field secretary) is one of the most-personal first-person sources on Sherrod in the corpus.
- **"Integration closed Black businesses" thesis canonical first-person**: Grinnell's articulation of the loss of Peg Leg Bates' resort, Black hotels, and *Green Book*-era Black establishments after integration is canonical first-person material on the often-uncomfortable economic-collateral-damage thesis. Pair with any other Black-business / Black-institution-loss-of-integration first-person source.
- **LAUSD expulsion-counselor race-disparity first-person**: Grinnell's account of writing a 3-page letter protesting the differential treatment of a white drug-involved student who was let off because "he's going to college" while inner-city Black students with comparable or lesser drug involvement were expelled, is canonical first-person material on the post-1960s educational-discipline race-disparity that became the "school-to-prison pipeline" framework.
