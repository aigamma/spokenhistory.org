### 67. Joseph Howell and Embry Howell

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Joseph Howell and Embry Howell_interview_20250704_221508/`
**Interviewer / Date**: David Cline (Whisper-rendered "David Klein" — recurring Whisper Cline→Klein error; Virginia Tech history dept and SOHP at UNC Chapel Hill) — Sunday, 13 December 2015
**Cameraman**: John Bishop, Media Generation; with Guha Shankar from the Library of Congress.
**Subjects**: Joseph "Joe" T. Howell III (b. 1942 Nashville, TN) and Embry Howell (b. 1945 Bethesda, MD; raised Davidson, NC). Married couple, both white Southerners, who participated in the SNCC SW Georgia Project Summer 1966 under Charles Sherrod's recruitment from Union Theological Seminary. Joe's father was president of Nashville City Bank, Belle Meade elite; Joe attended Montgomery Bell Academy then Davidson College, where as a senior in spring 1964 he led a "March on Charlotte" of 50+ HBCU students collecting Civil Rights Bill signatures for LBJ. Embry's father D. Grier Martin was president of Davidson College (oversaw early integration of Davidson with African students 1963). Joe attended Union Theological Seminary (Bill Webber's MUST program), then Chapel Hill urban planning; Embry attended Barnard then Chapel Hill public health. Recruited summer 1966 by Charles Sherrod to work in SW Georgia (Albany / Terrell County area) at the moment of the Stokely Carmichael vs. John Lewis SNCC chair fight (Carmichael elected May 1966) and the June 1966 Black Power articulation in the Meredith March.

**Cross-references**:
- Charles Sherrod (recurring; transcript #18; cross-ref McCullar #65; Churchville #63).
- Stokely Carmichael, Willie Ricks, John Lewis (SNCC June 1966 Black Power moment).
- Allard Lowenstein (NSA / Dump Johnson architect, recruited Joe from Davidson days).
- Paul Tillich, Reinhold Niebuhr, William Webber (Union Theological Seminary faculty).
- D. Grier Martin (Davidson College president, integrated Davidson 1963).
- Johnson C. Smith University, Barber-Scotia College, Haverford, Bryn Mawr (the schools that joined the Charlotte March).
- Lowndes County, Alabama (where the "Black Panther" emblem originated — referenced in the SW Georgia SNCC office welcome).
- Joe Mosnier — NOT this interviewer; David Cline is the canonical Howell interviewer.
