**Interviewer / Date**: David Klein (Virginia Tech History Department) with the Southern Oral History Program at UNC Chapel Hill; videographer John Bishop; also present Guha Shankar (Library of Congress) — 26 June 2016, at Arellanes's home in El Monte, California
**Subject**: Gloria Arellanes — born 1946 in El Monte CA; Tongva (Gabrielino) elder of the Houtgna/Houtnga village (the original indigenous name for the San Gabriel/El Monte area); daughter of a Mexican-American father (wrought-iron shop owner, WWII GI bill home-buyer; "fair-skinned, hazel-eyed" who passed as Greek to El Monte realtors until Arellanes's "very native looking" Tongva mother arrived to sign for the house — at which point realtors attempted to rescind the sale on race-restrictive grounds, the foundational anti-Mexican/Asian housing-covenant practice across mid-20th-century LA County); the foundational woman leader of the Chicano-movement Brown Berets at the East LA La Piranya Coffee House (the Young Citizens for Community Action group); co-founded with David Sanchez (the Brown Berets Prime Minister) the East LA Free Clinic with the Physicians for Community Action and Psychologists for Community Action coalition (mostly Anglo and Jewish progressive professionals); maintained the "non-aggression coalition pact" with the LA Black Panthers during the 1965 Watts rebellion (the Black Panthers wouldn't touch the Chicanos and vice versa); attended weekly Sunday cultural-celebration sessions with Maulana Karenga's US Organization during the founding-Kwanzaa era 1965-69.
**Cross-references**: Arellanes↔David Sanchez (Brown Berets Prime Minister and East LA Free Clinic co-founder); Arellanes↔LA Black Panthers / US Organization (the 1960s LA-area inter-racial / inter-organizational coalitions Arellanes describes); Arellanes↔Lucha / La Junta / United Mexican American Students (UMAS) (the broader 1960s Chicano-movement organizational network); Arellanes↔Aaron Dixon #1 / Elmer Dixon #37 / Big Man Howard #35 (the BPP cohort whose LA chapter overlapped with the Brown Berets era).
