### 24. Courtland Cox

**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier — Friday 8 July 2011, Jefferson Building of the Library of Congress, Washington, DC.
**Subject**: Courtland Cox (b. 1941, NYC; raised partly in Trinidad) — Howard University 1959+, founding member of **NAG (Non-Violent Action Group)** alongside Stokely Carmichael, Ed Brown, Mike Thelwell, Tom Kahn, Butch Kahn. Project Awareness organizer (Bayard Rustin / Malcolm X debate at Howard, 1961). SNCC steering committee. Logistics coordinator for the **1963 March on Washington** (under Bayard Rustin). The SNCC representative who passed John Lewis's draft speech to the press before it was changed under Archbishop Patrick O'Boyle's pressure. SNCC organizer at Lowndes County 1965. **Secretary-General of the Sixth Pan-African Congress** (Dar es Salaam, June 1974), the largest Pan-African gathering since the 1945 Manchester Congress; worked with C.L.R. James to convene heads of state Nyerere, Sékou Touré, Burnham. Co-founder of **Drum and Spear Bookstore** and Drum and Spear Press (Washington DC, 1968–78). Director of the Office of Minority Business Enterprise in the Clinton administration.

**Net-new ground-truth corpus candidates:**

- **Sixth Pan-African Congress (Dar es Salaam, June 19–27, 1974)**: largest Pan-African gathering since the 1945 Manchester Fifth Pan-African Congress; convened by C.L.R. James and Courtland Cox; hosted by Tanzanian president Julius Nyerere; attended by Sékou Touré (Guinea), Forbes Burnham (Guyana); the Caribbean insurgent/state-actor exclusion question and the Eritrean liberation movement attendance dispute defined the political tensions of the conference. Cox served as Secretary-General. Major Pan-African historical event not yet in corpus — high priority.
- **Project Awareness (Howard University NAG lecture/debate series 1961–65)**: organized by NAG; produced the canonical October 1961 Bayard Rustin–Malcolm X debate at Cramton Auditorium (capacity 1500, oversubscribed); the Norman Thomas–Herman Kahn debate on thermonuclear warfare; the James Baldwin–Ossie Davis–John Killens symposium moderated by Sterling A. Brown with Sidney Poitier present. Foundational venue for the SNCC intellectual cohort. Not yet in corpus.
- **C.L.R. James (Cyril Lionel Robert James, 1901–1989)**: Trinidadian Marxist intellectual; author of *The Black Jacobins* (1938) and *Beyond a Boundary* (1963); 6PAC intellectual godfather (wrote the manifesto and the call); foundational pre-Movement Pan-African theorist. Major omission from the pre-Movement corpus given his foundational role.
- **Ivanhoe Donaldson (1941–2016)**: SNCC executive committee member; foundational organizer of Mississippi and Lowndes work; later chief strategist for Mayor Marion Barry's 1978 DC mayoral campaign; convicted 1985 for embezzlement from Barry's office. Cox names him repeatedly as travel companion through the late-1960s and MLK-assassination weekend. Major SNCC figure not yet in corpus.
- **Jack Minnis (1932–2005)**: SNCC research director in Atlanta 1962–67; foundational researcher of the LCFO black-panther emblem and the Alabama county-party-formation law that enabled the LCFO; edited SNCC's research-and-analysis output. Cox calls him "absolutely brilliant." Critical SNCC figure not yet in corpus.
- **Julius Lester (1939–2018)**: SNCC photographer and writer; went to North Vietnam with Charlie Cobb late 1966; author of *Look Out, Whitey! Black Power's Gon' Get Your Mama!* (1968); later UMass Amherst Africana Studies + Jewish Studies professor (controversial mid-career conversion to Judaism). SNCC figure not yet in corpus.
- **Theodore Bikel (1924–2015)**: Austrian-Jewish-American folk singer, actor, longtime Northern white liberal supporter of SNCC and SCLC; his 1966–67 public criticism of SNCC's expel-whites and Black Power direction was named by Cox as a key inflection point in the Northern white funding collapse. Cross-corpus figure (folk-circuit benefit performers).
- **Mary Frances Berry (b. 1938)**: civil-rights historian, then-Howard Law professor, attendee of the Sixth Pan-African Congress 1974, later U.S. Commission on Civil Rights chair under Clinton (deposed by George W. Bush). Resolution candidate for the "Mary Barack" Pass-3 flagged span. Major Black-Studies/civil-rights scholar.
- **Emmett Dorsey (Howard University government professor, mid-1950s through 1970s)**: moderated the 1961 Rustin–Malcolm X Cramton Auditorium debate; named in Cox's testimony as the professor who taught NAG that "racism is architectonic to the Constitution of the United States" and pointed to the three-fifths clause. Local-but-foundational Howard NAG-era figure.
- **John Doar (1921–2014)**: Assistant Attorney General for Civil Rights 1965–67 (succeeded Burke Marshall); foundational federal civil-rights enforcement attorney; supervised Selma-Voting Rights Act enforcement and the Mississippi Burning prosecutions. Already named in the existing Robert F. Kennedy and Burke Marshall corpus entries but does not yet have his own. High priority.
- **Russell Tribunal (Stockholm + Roskilde, May–December 1967)**: international tribunal organized by Bertrand Russell investigating U.S. war crimes in Vietnam, with Sartre, Beauvoir, Deutscher, Colonel Hà Văn Lâu of North Vietnam, and Cox representing SNCC. Foundational anti-Vietnam internationalist body.
- **Cramton Auditorium (Howard University)**: 1,500-seat auditorium, principal venue for Howard's major lectures and debates from its 1961 opening; site of the canonical Rustin–Malcolm X Project Awareness debate of October 1961.
