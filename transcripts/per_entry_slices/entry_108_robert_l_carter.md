**Subject**: Hon. Robert Lee Carter (b. March 11, 1917, Caryville, FL — d. January 3, 2012, NYC), canonical NAACP General Counsel under Thurgood Marshall (1944-56, then full General Counsel 1956-68 after the LDF spinoff); chief Brown v. Board legal architect on the canonical social-science / Kenneth Clark doll-test theory; first counsel to argue McLaurin v. Oklahoma State Regents at the Supreme Court (1950 — Carter's first SCOTUS argument); later federal judge SDNY (1972-2012, 40 years on the bench). Family background: birthed in Caryville FL just before his mother joined his Newark NJ-bound father (a factory worker post-WWI Great Migrant); raised in poverty in Newark by his washerwoman mother after his father died when Carter was 1 year old; his older sister Frieda's 1929 marriage to Newark bar-owner Frank lifted the family into a 3-bedroom + indoor-toilet middle-class life. Educated through Newark + East Orange NJ public schools (where he integrated the East Orange HS swimming pool by force, prompting the school to close the pool entirely rather than admit Black students alongside white) → Lincoln University Pennsylvania (canonical Black-college, where Carter as part of his student generation agitated for Black faculty, joining the debate team that competed against Haverford and the small-college circuit) → Howard University Law School 1937-40 (full scholarship under Dean William Hastie + Vice Dean Charles Hamilton Houston; witnessed Houston's December 1938 Missouri ex rel. Gaines v. Canada Supreme Court argument, including the canonical Justice James Clark McReynolds turning his back on Houston throughout the argument) → Columbia Law School master's-program Rosenwald fellowship 1940-41 (working on First Amendment / right-to-strike thesis). Drafted into the Army Air Corps 1941; spent the war years experiencing the canonical military-segregation pattern that Carter credits as the transformative experience radicalizing him from "if you work hard you can beat the system" to militant lifelong civil-rights commitment. Hired as Thurgood Marshall's NAACP staff attorney 1944 by Hastie's recommendation; rose to be Marshall's first deputy + drafted virtually every Marshall Supreme Court brief through 1956 (without credit, until Carter began insisting on it). Argued + won McLaurin v. Oklahoma State Regents (June 5, 1950) at the Supreme Court — his first SCOTUS argument, attacking the canonical "separate-but-equal" doctrine via the canonical roped-off-special-seat-and-special-lunch-table indignities George McLaurin endured at the University of Oklahoma graduate school. Conceived + implemented the canonical social-science / Kenneth + Mamie Clark doll-test theory in Briggs v. Elliott (Clarendon County SC) — the only one of the Brown component cases where the canonical doll test was actually administered + the testimony entered into evidence. Sole architect of the canonical post-Brown 1954-58 NAACP defense against southern-state attempts to outlaw the NAACP — NAACP v. Alabama (1958, Supreme Court ruled membership lists protected by First Amendment associational rights) + NAACP v. Button (1963, Supreme Court ruled NAACP litigation tactics protected). Resigned the NAACP general-counselship 1968 over the canonical firing of NAACP attorney Lewis Steel for his New York Times Magazine article criticizing the Supreme Court's gradualism on Brown enforcement.
