### 15. Cecilia Suyat Marshall

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Cecilia Suyat Marshall_interview_20250704_182251/`
**Interviewer / Date**: Date and interviewer not stated explicitly; recorded in the Marshall home at Lake Barcroft, VA. Approximately 2013–14 based on Supreme Court Voting Rights Act ruling references.
**Subject**: Cecilia Suyat Marshall (1928–2022) — widow of US Supreme Court Justice Thurgood Marshall. Born and raised in Hawaii; moved to New York City in the late 1940s; placed by the employment office at the NAACP National Office; worked as secretary to Gloucester Current (Director of Branches) from ~1948 onward; married Thurgood Marshall in 1955 after his first wife's death. First-hand witness to the NAACP National Office's strategic work on *Brown v. Board* and the Marshall family's residential integration of Morningside Gardens (NYC) and later Lake Barcroft (Virginia).

- Robert L. Carter (1917-2012): canonical NAACP General Counsel under Thurgood Marshall; chief architect of the Brown v. Not in current corpus despite foundational role.
- Franklin H. Williams (1917-1990): canonical NAACP Legal Defense Fund attorney 1945-58 (worked on Groveland Four 1949-51 with Thurgood Marshall); later US Ambassador to Ghana 1965-68 under LBJ; first director of Peace Corps Africa programs. Not in current corpus.
- Edward R. Dudley (1911-2005): canonical first Black US Ambassador (Liberia 1948-53) under Truman; later first Black NY Surrogate's Court Judge; NAACP staff before ambassadorship. Not in current corpus.
- Groveland Four (1949 FL case): canonical NAACP rape-prosecution defense case; Charles Greenlee, Ernest Thomas, Samuel Shepherd, Walter Irvin; Marshall and Franklin Williams handled appeals; Thomas killed before trial, Shepherd killed by Lake County Sheriff Willis McCall en route to retrial (Irvin survived the shooting). Posthumously pardoned 2019 by FL Governor DeSantis. Foundational pre-Brown legal-defense case; documented in Gilbert King's *Devil in the Grove* (2012 Pulitzer Prize for nonfiction). Not in current corpus.
- *Henderson v. United States* (1950): canonical Supreme Court case ending segregated dining cars on interstate trains; argued by Belford V. Lawson Jr. + Thurgood Marshall amicus. Sets the legal stage for Brown v. Board four years later. Already referenced in entries #14, #15; should be added as canonical-events entry.
- *Shelby County v. Holder* (2013): canonical Supreme Court decision (570 U.S. 529) invalidating Section 4(b) coverage formula of the Voting Rights Act of 1965 — effectively gutting Section 5 preclearance. Major Movement-legacy reversal; referenced as dating-marker in this transcript. Catalog candidate.
- Ramsey Clark (1927-2021): canonical US Attorney General 1967-69 under LBJ; Lake Barcroft VA neighbor who recruited the Marshalls to integrate the neighborhood; later international human-rights attorney. Not in current corpus.
- Trenton Six (1948 NJ case): six Black men convicted of murdering a Trenton NJ shopkeeper; defended by NAACP + ACLU on appeal; conviction overturned 1949; the case that drew the NAACP into post-WWII Northern criminal-justice work. Mentioned in P1 #15.8 as possible alternative to Groveland Four. Catalog candidate.
- 409 Edgecombe Avenue: canonical Sugar Hill Harlem apartment building; W.E.B. Du Bois, Thurgood Marshall, Walter White, Robert L. Catalog candidate as canonical Movement-history venue.
- Morningside Gardens: canonical 1957-built NYC middle-income housing cooperative; six high-rise buildings on LaSalle Street / 123rd Street; integration-pilot site that the Marshalls helped pioneer 1958-67. Catalog candidate.
