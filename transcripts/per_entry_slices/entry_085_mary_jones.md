#### Notes for Pass 2

- **Albany Movement Police Committee canonical first-person**: Jones's first-person account of being one of seven members of the Albany Movement Police Committee (the only living member as of 2013) that met with Chief Pritchett — and McCree Harris's canonical "we've been here three times and we tried to tell you" speech that finally got Pritchett to agree to hire Black police officers — is canonical first-person testimony on the under-documented post-1961 institutional change pressure phase of the Albany Movement.
- **First Black Albany officers 1962 canonical first-person**: Jones's account that the first Black Albany officers (Etheridge Smith, David Hyde, Robert Benning Jr., and 2 others) walked a beat in uniform but were initially told they couldn't arrest whites — and that the canonical reason the Movement pushed for Black officers was the pattern of "every week or two some black man got killed" by white officers — is canonical first-person testimony on the immediate institutional impact of the Albany Movement.
- **Dec 1961 700-person mass arrest canonical first-person**: Jones's account of being one of the 700 people arrested Monday-Thursday in Dec 1961 — held in a tiny 8-10 person jail cell with 25 people, one bed and one toilet — and being released by her father putting up his property — is canonical first-person testimony from a mass-arrestee perspective.
- **Black Christmas Boycott 1961 canonical first-person**: Jones's account of taking Albany Black shoppers to Cordele or Tifton for Christmas shopping to enforce the canonical 1961 Albany boycott of downtown white merchants is canonical first-person testimony.
- **Washington Carver Public Housing 12 o'clock mass-meeting canonical first-person**: Jones's account of holding daily 12 o'clock mass meetings at her house in the Washington Carver project — so that older residents who couldn't walk to the church mass meetings could participate — is canonical first-person testimony on the under-documented project-housing micro-organizing layer of the Albany Movement.
- **Teachers-as-financial-support canonical first-person**: Jones's account of Albany Black teachers (forbidden to march on pain of losing their jobs) instead organizing financial support for jailed marchers' families — paying rent, light bills, buying food — and one teacher who finally said "well I can go to jail because the folks ain't buying" and went 3-4 times — is canonical first-person testimony on the role-stratification of Black-community Movement support.
