**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier (Southern Oral History Program at UNC Chapel Hill); videographer John Bishop — 8 June 2011, at Justice Finney's home in Columbia, South Carolina
**Subject**: Justice Ernest Adolphus Finney Jr. (1931–2017) — first African-American Chief Justice of the South Carolina Supreme Court (1994–2000); raised in Smithfield, Virginia and Washington, DC, with multiple moves following his father's teaching positions (the father was Dean of Claflin College, Orangeburg SC); Claflin College undergraduate, South Carolina State University Law School JD 1954 (entered just months before *Brown v. State* decision (the foundational SC school-equity ruling).

**Net-new ground-truth corpus candidates:**

- South Carolina Corridor of Shame: 2005 Bud and Ferrell Ferillo documentary on SC's chronically-underfunded rural school districts along the I-95 corridor (Allendale, Bamberg, Clarendon, Dillon, Hampton, Jasper, Lee, Marion, Marlboro, Williamsburg counties); directly tied to Finney's 1999 *Abbeville County v. State* school-equity ruling and the continuing SC school-equity battle through the 2010s.
- *Wrighten v. Board of Trustees of the University of South Carolina* (1947): The lawsuit by SC State graduate John H. Wrighten III that triggered the establishment of the SC State Law School (1947-66) as a "separate but equal" delay tactic before USC Law's eventual desegregation; foundational SC NAACP legal-education precedent.
- *Abbeville County School District No. 60 v. State* (1999): The 1999 SC Supreme Court decision authored by Chief Justice Finney holding that the SC state constitution required the legislature to provide students a "minimally adequate education"; the foundational SC school-equity ruling, though Finney himself describes the standard as "almost as bad as separate but equal".
- *Brown v. South Carolina Forestry Commission* (early 1960s): SC State Parks desegregation lawsuit brought by Matthew J. Perry / I. DeQuincey Newman that resulted in then-Governor Donald Russell closing rather than integrating the parks in 1963; canonical SC desegregation-era closure-rather-than-integrate response.
- *Henderson v. United States* (339 U.S. 816, 1950): SCOTUS decision overturning segregation in interstate-train dining cars; argued by Belford V. Lawson Jr.; one of the pre-*Brown* "separate but equal" erosion cases.
