### 18. Rev. Charles Melvin Sherrod

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Charles Melvin Sherrod_interview_20250704_183651/`  
*Layer 5 removed 4 low-impact phantom rendering rows (whisper renderings not present in raw).*
**Interviewer / Date**: Joe Mosnier (Southern Oral History Program / UNC) — Saturday 4 June 2011, Albany, GA (Albany State University, during the same 53rd Reunion as the McDew interview). Cameraman John Bishop.
**Subject**: Rev. Charles Sherrod (1937–2022) — Petersburg, VA native; Virginia Union seminary graduate; one of the first SNCC field secretaries (the very first as he describes); arrived in **Rock Hill, SC in early 1961** for the Friendship Nine jail-no-bail, then to **southwest Georgia in October 1961** to organize what became the **Albany Movement**. Together with Charles Jones, Cordell Reagon, and (later) Bernice Johnson Reagon, recruited the city's Black teenagers and gang members into nonviolent direct action. Survived a near-fatal beating in Baker County. Co-founded New Communities Inc. (1969, with his wife Shirley Sherrod and others) — a 5,735-acre Black-owned cooperative farm in southwest Georgia.
