### 2. Amos C. Brown

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Amos C. Brown_interview_20250704_172044/`
**Interviewer / Date**: David Klein (Smithsonian NMAAHC / Library of Congress Civil Rights History Project) — Saturday 2 March 2013, San Francisco, CA
**Subject**: Rev. Dr. Amos C. Brown — born Jackson, MS 1941. Mentored by Medgar Evers from age ~15; organized the first NAACP Youth Council in Jackson 1955; attended Morehouse 1959–64 (one of the eight students in Martin Luther King Jr.'s social-philosophy seminar); Crozer Theological Seminary; pastor of Third Baptist Church San Francisco since 1976. Member of the NAACP National Board. Has personal documentation of his Mississippi Sovereignty Commission surveillance file.

  1. African-American historical figures with unusual phonetics (Medgar Evers → multiple variants; Mordecai Johnson; Samuel D. Proctor; James Nabrit Jr.)
  2. African / Indian / non-Western names (Rajmohan Gandhi; Abidjan; Châtelain pronunciations)
  3. Place names in the South (Farish Street; Baltimore as "Vautamor")
- **Hymn / sermon quotations**: speaker quotes from "Come, Thou Almighty King" and from various biblical and constitutional sources; Whisper struggles with these. Pass 2 should sweep for known hymn lyrics + canonical biblical/legal quotations.
