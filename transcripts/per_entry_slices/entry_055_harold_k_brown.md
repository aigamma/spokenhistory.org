### 55. Harold K. Brown

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Harold K. Brown_interview_20250704_212356/`
**Interviewer / Date**: David Klein (Virginia Tech History Department / SOHP); videographer John Bishop — 28 June 2016, in San Diego, California
**Subject**: Harold K. "Hal" Brown — York, Pennsylvania-born (1934); migrated to San Diego via San Diego State University 1953; US Army 1954-56 (Fort Monmouth NJ; sent to Operation Sagebrush Louisiana 1955); San Diego State 1956+; founding chairman of San Diego CORE 1961+; later Chairman of the Western Region of CORE (CA/OR/WA/CO); foundational San Diego anti-discrimination campaigns against Bank of America (force-hire Black bank-tellers) and San Diego Gas & Electric (force-hire Black workers); coin-in / mail-in / sit-in tactics innovator 1962-66; Afro-American Association Black-history-study group member; Peace Corps Deputy Director for Lesotho 1967-68 (resigned after MLK assassination); Floyd McKissick Enterprises NYC + Marine Midland Bank loan officer 1968-71; San Diego State Africana Studies founding administrator and faculty hire 1971-73 (first Black administrator in SDSU history); SDSU College of Business Administration Associate Dean 1980-2004; Black Economic Development Task Force founder 1987; CLUB (Community Leaders Undoing Biases) founder; real estate developer 2004 onward.
**Cross-references**: Brown↔James Farmer / Floyd McKissick (canonical CORE Founders and National leadership); Brown↔Jack Kimbrough (canonical San Diego Black dentist and pre-CORE Woolworth's-sit-in activist); Brown↔Lerone Bennett Jr.'s *Before the Mayflower* (the foundational 1962 Afro-American Association reading-group text); Brown↔Tony Brown / *Tony Brown's Journal* PBS / *White Girl* film (the canonical post-Movement Black-economic-development voice).
