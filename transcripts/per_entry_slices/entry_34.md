### 34. Ekwueme Michael Thelwell

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Ekwueme Michael Thelwell_interview_20250704_200252/`
**Interviewer / Date**: Emily Crosby (Southern Oral History Program at UNC Chapel Hill); videographer John Bishop — 23 August 2013, at Thelwell's home in Pelham, Massachusetts (transcript reads "Column, Massachusetts" — likely "Pelham")
**Subject**: Ekwueme Michael Thelwell — Jamaican-born novelist and Howard University NAG / SNCC activist; came to the US in September 1959 to enroll at Howard, just months before the February 1960 Greensboro sit-ins; member of the foundational Howard cohort of the Nonviolent Action Group (NAG) alongside Stokely Carmichael, Courtland Cox, Charlie Cobb, Cleveland Sellers, Hank Thomas, Mary Lovelace O'Neal, Bill Mahoney, Muriel Tillinghast, and Tim Jenkins; observed Malcolm X repeatedly at Howard via the Bayard Rustin–Tom Kahn–led Project Awareness debate series; later co-authored *Ready for Revolution* with Stokely Carmichael (Kwame Ture), authored the novel *The Harder They Come*, became professor and chair of the W.E.B. Du Bois Department of Afro-American Studies at UMass Amherst; partial transcript covers only the first ~36% of the source file (cut off mid-Mississippi narrative around the Hartman Turnbow self-defense discussion).
**Cross-references**: Thelwell↔Cox (#24; Courtland Cox; Howard NAG co-organizer); Thelwell↔Sellers (#22; Cleveland Sellers; Howard NAG co-organizer); Thelwell↔Carmichael (Stokely Carmichael; co-author of *Ready for Revolution*; Howard NAG co-organizer; not yet in numbered transcript list); Thelwell↔Tillinghast (Muriel Tillinghast; Howard NAG co-organizer; not yet numbered); Thelwell↔Cobb (Charlie Cobb; Howard NAG co-organizer; not yet numbered).

#### Notes for Pass 2

- **Partial read**: The transcript was truncated at offset ~68 KB of 190 KB — Pass 2 must read the remaining 122 KB, which covers (a) the second-half Mississippi narrative beyond the Hartman Turnbow self-defense discussion, (b) the post-SNCC academic career including UMass W.E.B. Du Bois Department of Afro-American Studies, (c) the relationships with Carmichael / Ture in Guinea, (d) the Achebe / Nsukka friendship and Thelwell's three Nigerian visits, (e) the *Harder They Come* novelization project, (f) the *Ready for Revolution* co-authorship process, and (g) the contemporary movement-history work.
- **Foundational Howard NAG canonical first-person**: Thelwell's transcript is the canonical first-person source on the foundational Howard NAG cohort — the Carmichael/Cox/Cobb/Sellers/Mahoney/Tillinghast/Lovelace/Thomas/Mboya-connection bloc. Pair with Sellers #22, Cox #24, and any future Carmichael / Cobb / Tillinghast / Mahoney transcripts for the most complete account in the corpus of how the Howard cadre formed and dispatched into SNCC's southern projects.
- **Cross-cultural-pan-African perspective**: Thelwell's Jamaican origin and his close personal relationships with Mboya, Kaunda, Mboya's brother, and later Achebe make this transcript uniquely positioned to ground the SNCC oral histories in the broader pan-African independence movement. The accuracy of the African names is therefore particularly load-bearing — Pass 2 should treat the African political-figure corrections (#34.28, #34.29, #34.30, #34.47, #34.48) as high-priority for verification.
- **The "Slave Sellers" error**: One of the most damaging single-name Whisper errors in the corpus. The Stage-2 fuzzy matcher should be calibrated to never confuse "Cleve" or "Cleveland" with "Slave" — this is a worst-case loss of dignity in the source.
