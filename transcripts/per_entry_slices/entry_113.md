### 113. Sam Mahone

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Sam Mahone_interview_20250705_013850/`
**Interviewer / Date**: Dr. Hasan Kwame Jeffries (Ohio State / SOHP UNC Chapel Hill) — Saturday March 9, 2013, Albany State University, Albany GA
**Cameraman**: Pete Daniel (rendered as "Petna Delico" in Whisper)
**Subject**: Sam Mahone (b. 1945 Americus GA, Sumter County, Southwest Georgia), canonical Americus Movement SNCC field worker; arrived in late 1962 from Charles Sherrod's Albany / Southwest Georgia Project; co-organized the canonical 1963 Americus voter registration + freedom-school + public-accommodations-testing operation. Family background: father Burgin Mahone (basket factory worker producing fruit-shipment baskets); mother Lula Bell Walker (domestic worker); 6 siblings, mostly absent during Sam's high-school years. Educated through Sumter County High School Americus (graduated 1963). Pre-SNCC: joined the Americus Voters League (ministerial voter-registration project that pre-dated SNCC's arrival in Americus); recruited as a 10th/11th-grader. Late 1962: SNCC field secretaries Don Harris + John Perdew + Ralph Allen arrived in Americus (dispatched from Albany by Charles Sherrod, who picked Americus for its demographics — 50%+ Black population, $1,200 median Black income vs. ~$4,800 white). Spring/Summer 1963: 11 students from the canonical SNCC house decided to test the canonical Americus movie theater (segregated rear-of-building entrance via dark alley + 150-foot staircase to 3rd-floor balcony); all 11 arrested on disorderly conduct + fighting-an-officer charges + given 90-day probation; one week later all re-arrested at a second demonstration and sentenced to 90 days at the Sumter County prison farm (where Mahone + one other male prisoner were placed in a 4x6-ft concrete "hole" for 7 days hunger strike on bread + water → passed out, hospitalized, returned to chain-gang work). Canonical sewage-treatment-plant cleanup work assigned specifically to the SNCC prisoners. Released → 4 SNCC affiliates (Don Harris + John Perdew + Ralph Allen + CORE worker Zev Aelony) arrested + charged under the Reconstruction-era Georgia anti-sedition law (carrying the death penalty); held 84+ days; the federal 3-judge panel released them after the canonical CB King + Donald Hollowell + Constance Baker Motley + (Macon attorney) Larry Thompson legal team won the case. July 1964: Mahone + John Perdew + Bob Mants drove to the canonical Hasty House restaurant in Americus the night the Civil Rights Act was signed → were attacked by tire-iron-and-baseball-bat-armed white men from across the street → escaped only after one SNCC member (who had gone to the restroom outside) was beaten + ran away never to return for 2 days. Canonical 1965 mass arrest of the 4 Black women (Mary Kate Bell Fishbel + Mamie Campbell + Gloria Wise + Lena Turner) for attempting to vote in the white line. Late 1964-65 canonical Maids Union organizing. Late 1965-66 canonical Americus public library 20,000-book national-book-donation campaign that established the U-Center (the formerly canonical Colored Hospital, a 1940s training facility that produced the highest number of Black doctors in the country at the time; now on the National Historic Register). 1966 → SNCC scholarship to Tougaloo College Mississippi (joined Tougaloo's anti-Vietnam-War protest, the only Black institution protesting); subsequently worked in Greenville MS. 1969 drafted into the Army → assigned to Squadron F at Fort Benning GA holding company (digging archery-course trails for officers) → shipped to a 5-hour-from-Paris-France material command in Europe where he was assigned essentially no work → bought a $100 used car + drove every weekend through France, picking up the camera that became the foundation of his postwar career as a photographer and gallery owner (Ancestral Art Gallery, Atlanta) + 15-year apprenticeship in paper conservation at the Georgia Department of Archives.

**Cross-references**: Charles Sherrod (canonical Albany Movement / Southwest Georgia Project director; cross-corpus #18); Don Harris (canonical SNCC field secretary); John Perdew (canonical SNCC field secretary; cross-corpus); Ralph Allen (canonical SNCC field secretary); Zev Aelony (canonical CORE worker arrested under the anti-sedition law); Sheriff Fred Chappell (canonical Americus "meanest man in the world" Dr. King reference, the canonical 1961 King-jailed-here sheriff); Bob Mants (canonical Atlanta SNCC / Morehouse student); the canonical "Stolen Girls" / Leesburg Stockade incident (the canonical post-Americus-march abandoned-prison-camp confinement of juvenile Black girls under 16 in a single 40-girl-toilet area with one rattlesnake-into-cell-area incident); the canonical Sumter County jail "hole" 4x6-ft concrete confinement; Constance Baker Motley (cross-corpus #108); Donald Hollowell (canonical Atlanta civil rights attorney); CB King (canonical Albany attorney, cross-corpus); Mary Kate Bell Fishbel + Mamie Campbell + Gloria Wise + Lena Turner (canonical 1965 Americus voter-line arrest 4); Warren Fortson (canonical white Americus lawyer who fled after defending Roberta Freeman + her boyfriend on trumped-up morals charges + the canonical Americus library desegregation fight); Ben Fortson (canonical Georgia Secretary of State; Warren's brother); Roberta Freeman (canonical first Black student at Americus High); Jimmy Carter (canonical Plains GA / Americus son; canonical hometown); Bottoms Funeral Home (canonical Americus Black funeral home + 1960s Americus-movement bail-bondsman + meeting site).
