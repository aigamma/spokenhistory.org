### 114. Sam Young, Jr.

**Source**: `transcripts/raw/Sam Young, Jr.,_interview_20250705_014056/`
**Interviewer / Date**: Willie Griffin (SOHP UNC Chapel Hill research associate) — Saturday March 9, 2013, Albany State University, Albany GA
**Cameraman**: John Bishop
**Subject**: Sam Young Jr. (b. March 13, 1950, Worth County GA, Sylvester GA; 10 sisters + 7 brothers, 17 siblings total), canonical Southwest Georgia Project participant 1968-1983 + canonical New Communities Inc. cooperative-farm participant 1968-late-1970s. Family background: father Sam Young Sr. (politically engaged Worth County farmer who insisted on Sam Jr. attending integrated school under "Freedom of Choice"); experienced canonical 1968-era racial intimidation including church-painted-as-"devil's-den" + cousin-arrested-from-school-bus-and-disappeared-3-days episodes. Worked at Firestone Tire (Albany GA factory) first job out of high school → fired/let-go in canonical racial-supervisor incident → joined the Southwest Georgia Project under Charles Sherrod via the canonical SWGP-scholarship-to-community-college program → studied graphic arts at Albany Technical College (for the Movement newspaper) → became one of the canonical New Communities Inc. on-site farmers managing the canonical 300+-acre peanut allotment + corn + vegetable operation on the 5,000-acre Lee County land that Charles Sherrod had bought via the canonical 1968 land-trust gift mechanism that became the foundation for the entire US Black-cooperative-farm movement. Lost the New Communities farm in the canonical Pigford v. Glickman + Pigford II (US Department of Agriculture / ASCS systematic discriminatory-foreclosure) lawsuit which finally settled with the December 2009 Claims Resolution Act payouts (~$1.25 billion to roughly 16,000 Black farmers). Young recovered post-settlement and is now beginning to reconnect with the Southwest Georgia Project + the new Charles + Shirley Sherrod-led land-purchase initiative.
