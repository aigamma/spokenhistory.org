**Cameraman**: John Bishop; with Randy Gaines and Henry Louis Gates Jr. (Whisper-rendered "Henry Gattes") as auditing observers
**Subject**: Kathleen Neal Cleaver (b. 1945) — daughter of Ernest Cooper Neal (Tuskegee Institute faculty, then US State Department Point Four Program officer in India, Philippines, Sierra Leone, Liberia) and Juette Johnson Neal (Tuskegee staff, later teacher abroad). Lived abroad as a child during the formative anti-colonial 1950s in India (under Nehru), Philippines, Sierra Leone (under newly-formed independence). Attended George School (Quaker boarding HS, Bucks County PA) — same as Julian Bond (a few years ahead). Briefly Oberlin College 1963–64; dropped out to work at the Community Relations Service (CRS) in Washington DC 1964–66 (under Roger Wilkins). Transferred to Barnard College 1966; dropped out again to work as SNCC New York office staff summer 1966 (after black-power transition at SNCC, June 1966 Greenwood MS, Carmichael + Willie Ricks); moved to SNCC Atlanta National Office January 1967 as George Ware's assistant in the campus program. Met Eldridge Cleaver at Vanderbilt Impact Symposium / Fisk "Liberation Will Come from a Black Thing" SNCC conference Easter weekend March 1967; married Eldridge June 1968. Communications Secretary of the Black Panther Party. Exile to Algeria + Cuba 1969–75 with Eldridge.

#### Notes for Pass 2

- **Sammy Younge Jr. Jan 1966 canonical first-person**: Cleaver's first-person account of Sammy Younge Jr.'s murder — and George Ware's resulting "I was the one who inspired and led and encouraged Sammy" guilt that led Ware to quit Hoffman-LaRoche to return to the Movement and take over SNCC's campus program in Atlanta — is canonical first-person testimony on the chain of events that brought Cleaver into SNCC's National Office January 1967. Cleaver's narrative is one of the best preserved accounts of Younge's death's catalytic role in SNCC's anti-Vietnam-War 1966 statement (which then triggered Julian Bond's denied Georgia state legislature seating Jan 1966 — *Bond v. Floyd* 1966 SCOTUS).
- **Carmichael "What We Want" canonical reception**: Cleaver's first-person account of reading Carmichael's "What We Want" essay (NY Review of Books, Sept 22, 1966) as articulating that "integration is disintegration" — and recognizing it as a precursor framework to her own revolutionary turn — is canonical first-person testimony on how the Black Power thesis was articulated to college-educated radicals.
- **SNCC 1967 newsletter Israel-Palestine controversy canonical first-person**: Cleaver's account of the SNCC 1967 newsletter that critiqued Israeli paramilitary practices in the 1967 Six-Day War — and Harry Belafonte's "I can smash the organization" reaction (which she heard but cannot confirm) — is canonical first-person testimony on the under-documented SNCC-Jewish-donor split that helped financially deplete SNCC in 1967.
- **Fisk "Liberation Will Come From a Black Thing" April 1967 canonical first-person**: Cleaver's account of the SNCC conference at Fisk Easter weekend 1967 — Fisk withdrawing room rights two weeks before due to anti-Stokely-Carmichael political pressure from the Tennessee state legislature, Fr. Woodruff's nearby Episcopal church providing sanctuary venue, an East Coast blizzard preventing speakers from arriving, and Eldridge Cleaver being the only speaker who made it (and falling in love with Kathleen at first sight while she retyped the agenda) — is canonical first-person testimony on the under-documented SNCC-vs-Black-college-administration tensions of spring 1967.
- **Becoming-a-revolutionary canonical first-person**: Cleaver's articulation that "by the time Martin Luther King was murdered in 68, that attitude had crystallized already among the black revolutionaries" — and her positioning of SNCC as "a debating society or like an ideological institute as well as an activist group, as well as a family, as well as civil rights organizations" — is canonical first-person testimony on SNCC's nature as a committee rather than a hierarchical organization.
- **Black Panther Party for Self-Defense founding date discrepancy**: Cleaver says BPP was founded "October 21st because that was Bobby Seal's birthday." Bobby Seale's actual birthday is October 22, 1936, and the BPP founding date is canonically October 15, 1966 (per Newton's *Revolutionary Suicide*). Cleaver's recall may conflate the founding-day choice with Seale's birthday. Pass 2 should verify against external sources.
