## Section 1 — Subject Paragraph Audit

**Subject paragraph under review** (from entry slice):

> Reginald "Reggie" Robinson (b. October 2, 1939, Baltimore MD), one of the earliest SNCC field secretaries — joined as Baltimore CIG (Civic Interest Group) representative at the June 1961 Louisville KY SNCC meeting (where Ella Baker recognized him by his McKinley family resemblance), then volunteered to go to McComb MS to work with Bob Moses on voter registration. Family background: Baltimore east-side; father a 39-year Bethlehem Steel worker who died one month after retirement; mother Leovester ("Leo Vester") Jenkins Robinson; siblings Leroy (older brother) and Yula Jean (sister). Education: Dunbar High School (left mid-10th grade), then Cortez Peters Business College (Black-owned business school founded by world-record typing speedster Cortez Peters, financed by Royal/Underwood, with branches in DC, Baltimore, Chicago). Joined Baltimore Civic Interest Group spring 1960 under the encouragement of CPBC Dean Walter Dixon (also Baltimore City Councilman who had just introduced the city's public-accommodations bill). Rose through CIG to treasurer; ran the Clarence Mitchell Jr.-area voter-registration drive (registered ~30,000 voters in a short period via sound trucks + school buses + hot dogs / community parties). June 1961: traveled to Louisville KY SNCC coordinating committee meeting (where Ella Baker recognized him); shifted from CIG to SNCC; went to McComb MS in August 1961 under Bob Moses; worked under "Super Cool Daddy" (Mr. Webb) at the local infrastructure level (CC Bryant's lodging, Berglin/Burglund's supermarket where they rented mimeograph and set up the Masonic Temple voter-registration school). Witnessed the canonical Sept 1961 Herbert Lee killing by State Representative E.H. Hurst at a Liberty MS cotton gin; covered the Curtis Hayes / Hollis Watkins / Brenda Travis post-Lee-killing bus-station-arrest jail visits at Magnolia MS. Survived (with Diane Nash, who had an outstanding warrant) the post-Burgland-walkout police assault on Bob Zellner outside the Pike County Masonic Temple by escaping through the Masons' black-cloth-curtained back-roof. Post-McComb: helped found the Cambridge MD SNCC project with Bill Hansen (white SNCC organizer; Robinson highly critical of Hansen's grandstanding); negotiated with Maryland National Guard General George Gelston during the 1963-64 Cambridge crisis; clashed with Gloria Richardson over her position that Black Cambridge residents should boycott the public-accommodations-referendum vote. Continued SNCC field work through 1967 across Orangeburg SC + Selma AL (where he reported on the 12 ministers Bernard Lafayette would later organize) + Northern Student Movement support work in Raleigh NC with Dottie Burlage.
