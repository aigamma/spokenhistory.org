> Kay Tillow (b. 1942 Paducah, KY; raised Metropolis, IL) — daughter of a Southern Illinois Teachers College alumna teacher and a furniture-store-owning farmer. Worked Appalachian Committee for Full Employment Eastern Kentucky 1964; met Walter Tillow at Highlander; joined March 5, 1964 March on Frankfort for Jobs and Freedom. After 1966, UE (United Electrical Workers) organizer (Northern PA); 1969 onward Local 1199 hospital workers union organizer (Pittsburgh, PA): won Jewish Home and Hospital for the Aged Pittsburgh Christmas Eve 1969 sit-in (with Henry Nicholas). Later Pennsylvania state collective-bargaining-rights expansion to hospital workers (1970), pre-dating the 1975 NLRB amendments. Carried on into single-payer healthcare organizing (HR 676, Rep. John Conyers's Expanded and Improved Medicare for All Act). Currently working on the All Unions Committee for Single Payer Healthcare HR 676 (605 union endorsements as of 2013, 43 state AFL-CIO federations, 146 Central Labor Councils).
