Robert J. Brown (b. February 1935, High Point NC), founder of B&C Associates (1960) — one of the first Black-owned public-relations consulting firms in the United States, with F.W. Woolworth as its first major client. Family background: raised by maternal grandmother Nelly Marshall Brown (née Kendall, born Anson County NC), whose father Joseph Kendall Sr. was a slave — a fact Brown only fully grasped when his grandmother brought him and his brother by bus through Fayetteville NC c. 1943 specifically to see the old Fayetteville slave market where Joseph Kendall had been last sold. Educated through **Leonard Street School** → William Penn High School (Quaker-founded High Point Black high school, where Mary McLeod Bethune spent an afternoon counseling Brown as her student escort, and where Langston Hughes + a Morehouse faculty visitor spoke). Took the High Point police examination and joined HPPD as an integrating-arrests Black officer (arrested whites despite the unwritten "Black officers arrest Black suspects" rule) → recruited into federal civil-rights / mafia-investigation work in NYC (testified in the Vito Genovese case + other major mafia cases through ~1958-60). Quit December 1960 and founded B&C Associates in High Point NC; first major client was F.W. Woolworth (which engaged him in early 1961 after the Greensboro sit-ins to advise on integrating their stores and corporate structure). Through F.W. Woolworth's board (John J. McCloy + Fred Kirby of Allegheny Corp), Brown became the lead PR consultant on race-relations crisis management for major American corporations through the 1960s. Simultaneously, Brown funded and brokered SCLC operations from behind the scenes — including personally testing hotel integration in Memphis (The Peabody Hotel) on behalf of SCLC. After Dr. King's April 4, 1968 assassination, Brown traveled to Memphis to help recover the body; hosted Robert Kennedy + Ethel + Sidney Poitier + others in a Hyatt Regency Atlanta suite the night before King's funeral. Then joined Nixon's 1968 campaign as Bob Finch's personal recruit; rode the canonical Ohio "Bring Us Together" whistle-stop train (the last whistle-stop tour in American presidential history). Became one of Nixon's principal White House assistants with full cabinet-meeting access (first Black principal advisor at that level), serving through the Nixon first term. Post-White House: Coretta Scott King's 1986 South Africa trip (Brown personally arranged Mrs. King's visas through SA Ambassador Piet Koornhof; personally arranged + funded the Mandela grandchildren's Boston University education after Winnie Mandela's request; met Nelson Mandela inside Pollsmoor Prison via a special grant from PW Botha — the first outside visitor to see Mandela in approximately 20+ years, per Brown's own account).
