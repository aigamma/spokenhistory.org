**Proposal 2: Sargent Shriver**

**Proposal 3: Albany Black Christmas Boycott 1961 (organizational entry)**  
- Role: The December 1961 boycott of downtown Albany white merchants organized as part of the Albany Movement; layaway-clearing date "first Monday in December" was the threshold the Black community used; enforced by transporting Albany Black shoppers to neighboring towns (Cordele, Tifton, Moultrie).  
- Why belongs: The Albany Movement corpus entry describes the broad movement but does not isolate the Christmas Boycott as a distinct named event with its institutional mechanics. Jones's first-person account of the boycott's operational structure (layaway clearing, shopping in Moultrie/Cordele, the "black Christmas" outcome) is the most detailed first-person account in the corpus and merits a standalone ground-truth entry.  
- Transcript evidence: "I believe it was the first Monday in December. Get out of your layaways... I was taking people to to motry [Moultrie]... Folks was going to come Miller [Camilla]... they didn't shop here... it was a black Christmas."
