## Section 3 — Residual Ground-Truth Corpus Proposals

The following 3 candidates are not in the current 140-entry corpus and have the strongest case for addition based on transcript evidence and cross-corpus relevance:

**1. National Council of La Raza (NCLR, now UnidosUS)**

- **Role**: Premier US Hispanic civil-rights coalition; founded 1968 as Southwest Council of La Raza (Ford Foundation-funded); renamed NCLR 1972; renamed UnidosUS 2017.
- **Why it belongs**: Camarillo served as a senior board member for multiple terms; the NCLR was the primary organizational vehicle for negotiating the 1986 IRCA with Congress and the Reagan White House. The corpus covers UFW and SNCC but has no entry for the leading Latino civil-rights coalition. Cross-corpus relevance: also appears in the Varela (#81) interview context.
- **Transcript evidence**: "one of the first national organizations I was asked invited to join was the National Council of La Raza, which is the most prominent Latino national organization. And I served the maximum terms. And during that time... we worked with Sen. Orrin Hatch... and Sen. Edward 'Ted' Kennedy... to develop immigration reform."

**2. 1986 Immigration Reform and Control Act (IRCA)**

- **Role**: Reagan-signed federal legislation (November 6, 1986); provided amnesty to approximately 3 million undocumented immigrants resident since before 1982; established employer sanctions; co-sponsored by Sen. Alan Simpson (R-WY) and Rep. Romano Mazzoli (D-KY); shaped by NCLR advocacy with Senators Kennedy and Hatch.
- **Why it belongs**: The IRCA is a foundational immigration-policy landmark of the Chicano Movement's policy-influence era, directly comparable in scope to the Civil Rights Act of 1964 (already in corpus) and the Voting Rights Act (already in corpus). Multiple interviewees reference it. No corpus entry currently exists.
- **Transcript evidence**: "And that passed, signed by Ronald Reagan. [It] was called immigration reform. Some people call it amnesty. It was actually a way to pardon and legalize people already here fully employed and contributing to the building of America."

**3. José Vasconcelos and *La Raza Cósmica* (1925)**

- **Role**: José Vasconcelos (1882–1959), Mexican philosopher and education minister under President Álvaro Obregón (1921–24); author of *La raza cósmica* (1925) — the foundational philosophical-political treatise of pan-Latin-American Mestizo identity that undergirds the "La Raza" concept in US Chicano Movement ideology.
- **Why it belongs**: Camarillo directly explains the philosophical lineage of "La Raza" through Vasconcelos; the conceptual framework shapes the naming of the National Council of La Raza and the Chicano Movement's self-identification. No Vasconcelos entry exists in the corpus despite its foundational role. Cross-corpus relevance: any Chicano Movement interview.
- **Transcript evidence**: "During the Mexican Revolution, one of the members of the Revolution was an educator José Vasconcelos. José Vasconcelos wrote a book right after the war, the Mexican Revolution called La Raza Cósmica, the Cosmic Race. And that's where the concept La Raza comes from."
