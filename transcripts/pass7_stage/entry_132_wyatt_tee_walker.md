Rev. Dr. Wyatt Tee Walker (b. August 16, 1928, Brockton MA — birth year corrected from a lifelong belief of 1929 via his mother's papers discovered late in life; d. January 23, 2018) served as SCLC Executive Director under Martin Luther King Jr. from 1960 to 1964, building the organization from a 5-person staff and $57,000 budget to 100 employees and $1 million in four years. He was the chief architect of Project C (Confrontation), the strategic blueprint for the 1963 Birmingham campaign, developed through a year of advance reconnaissance measuring storefront seating capacities and modeling march-pace timing to predict and provoke Bull Connor's response. He was the primary typist and editor of the *Letter from Birmingham Jail*, transcribing King's handwritten drafts from newspaper margins and toilet paper, and renamed the Quakers' first printing (titled "Tears of Love") to the canonical published title. He served as Southern Regional Coordinator for the 1963 March on Washington. After his 1964 departure from SCLC over a $10,000 salary freeze, he pastored Canaan Baptist Church of Christ in Harlem from 1967 to 2004 (37 years), building a 1,800-member congregation that received over $1 million in annual tithes in his final two years, co-architecting New York's charter school legislation with Floyd Flake and Rubén Díaz Sr., and becoming the state's largest developer of affordable housing. He organized a 300-member Black-church anti-apartheid federation, took 22 ministers to South Africa, and worked on the 1994 Mandela election. Walker's wife Theresa Walker (also interviewed here) was a 1961 Freedom Rider, a Mother's Day 1963 Birmingham National Guardsman assault survivor, and was jailed in East Point GA with all four children after Mother's Day 1963. Walker reported approximately 55 books written, 30 published and about 30 more to be published.
