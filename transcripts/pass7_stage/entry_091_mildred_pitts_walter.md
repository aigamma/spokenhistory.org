**Proposal B — Coretta Scott King Award (1969–present)**
- **Role:** Annual award given by the American Library Association (ALA) to African-American authors and illustrators whose books promote understanding and appreciation of the culture and contributions of all people. **Proposal C — Carter G. Woodson (1875-1950)**
- **Role:** African-American historian, author of *The Negro in Our History* (1922; 11 editions through 1966), founder of the Association for the Study of Negro Life and History (ASNLH, 1915), founder of Negro History Week (1926 — became Black History Month 1976), founder of the *Journal of Negro History* (1916). One of the earliest and most influential systematizers of Black history as an academic and pedagogical discipline.
- **Why corpus-relevant:** Carter G. Woodson's *The Negro in Our History* was the most widely distributed Black history textbook in segregated Black schools from the 1920s through the 1940s. He recurs across the corpus wherever Black-education and Black-history-pedagogy intersect.
