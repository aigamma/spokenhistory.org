## Section 5 — Publication-Readiness Verdict

Entry 73 (Kathleen Cleaver) documents a September 16, 2011 oral history interview conducted in Atlanta, Georgia by Joe Mosnier (UNC Southern Oral History Program), with Henry Louis Gates Jr. and Randy Gaines as auditing observers. Cleaver provides canonical first-person testimony on her formative anti-colonial childhood (Tuskegee → India → Philippines → Sierra Leone), her path through George School, Oberlin, the Community Relations Service (under Roger Wilkins), Barnard, and SNCC (under George Ware), through to her role in the Black Panther Party's Communications leadership and the Algeria + Cuba exile period 1969–75. The interview is one of the richest first-person accounts in the CRHP corpus, covering SNCC's internal organizational culture, the SNCC–BPP transition, COINTELPRO, the SNCC 1967 Israel-Palestine newsletter controversy, and the under-documented Vanderbilt Impact Symposium / Fisk "Liberation Will Come from a Black Thing" conference (Easter 1967).
