### Proposal 1: Charleston Hospital Workers Strike (1969)

**Name:** Charleston Hospital Workers Strike (1969)  
**Role:** Canonical labor-civil-rights strike; March 20 – June 27, 1969; Charleston SC  
**Why they belong:** This is one of the most significant post-MLK SCLC engagements — a 100-day strike by predominantly Black women hospital workers at Medical University of South Carolina (MUSC) and Charleston County Hospital. Saunders's first-person testimony is the richest available account of the back-channel resolution mechanism. Not in current corpus despite its canonical Movement significance.  
**Transcript evidence:** Saunders provides extensive first-person detail across multiple transcript segments; the strike is the central event of the interview. "I think the hospital strike changed South Carolina. It really did…No blacks were elected to public office before that hospital strike."
