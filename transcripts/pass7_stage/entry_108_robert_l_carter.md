### Corrected Subject paragraph

Hon. Robert Lee Carter (b. March 11, 1917, Caryville, FL — d. January 3, 2012, NYC), canonical NAACP General Counsel under Thurgood Marshall (1944-56, then full General Counsel 1956-68 after the LDF spinoff); chief Brown v. Board legal architect of the canonical social-science / Kenneth Clark doll-test theory; first counsel to argue McLaurin v. Oklahoma State Regents at the Supreme Court (1950 — Carter's first SCOTUS argument); later federal judge SDNY (1972-2012, 40 years on the bench). Family background: birthed in Caryville FL just before his mother joined his Newark NJ-bound father (a factory worker in the post-WWI Great Migration); raised in poverty in Newark by his washerwoman mother after his father died when Carter was 1 year old; his older sister's marriage to a Linden, New Jersey bar-owner lifted the family into middle-class life. Educated through Newark + East Orange NJ public schools (where he integrated the East Orange HS swimming pool by force, prompting the school to close the pool entirely rather than admit Black students alongside white) → Lincoln University Pennsylvania (canonical HBCU, where Carter joined the debate team that competed against Haverford and the small-college circuit) → Howard University School of Law 1937-40 (full scholarship; Dean William Hastie was his model; Carter witnessed Houston's December 1938 Missouri ex rel. Gaines v. Canada Supreme Court argument, including Justice James Clark McReynolds turning his back on Houston throughout the argument) → Columbia Law School Rosenwald Fellowship 1940-41 (First Amendment / right-to-strike master's thesis under Noel Dowling). Drafted into the Army Air Corps 1941; the military-segregation experience radicalized him from accommodationism to lifelong civil-rights commitment. Hired as Thurgood Marshall's NAACP staff attorney 1944 by Hastie's recommendation; drafted virtually every Marshall Supreme Court brief through 1956. Argued + won McLaurin v. Oklahoma State Regents (June 5, 1950) — his first SCOTUS argument. Conceived + implemented the social-science / Kenneth + Mamie Clark doll-test theory in Briggs v. Elliott (Clarendon County SC) — the only Brown component case where the doll test was actually administered and the testimony entered into evidence. Architect of NAACP v. Alabama (1958) + NAACP v. Button (1963) defense against southern-state attempts to outlaw the NAACP. Resigned the NAACP general-counselship 1968 over the firing of NAACP attorney Lewis Steel for his New York Times Magazine article criticizing the Supreme Court's gradualism on Brown enforcement.
