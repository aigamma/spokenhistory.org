#### Notes for Pass 2 supervisor

Pass 1 was substantively complete for this transcript (95 items in the original table). Pass 2 surfaced several **new canonical figures and locations** not in Pass 1:
1. **Dorothy Cotton** at the July 2, 2011 Hayling Award of Valor luncheon — canonical SCLC Citizenship Education Program director, the only woman on SCLC executive staff under MLK
2. **ACCORD** (the St. Augustine post-Movement preservation organization) recovered via the "40 the court" Whisper rendering — important canonical organization for the entire St. Augustine cross-corpus cluster
3. **Lundy Brothers Restaurant** in Sheepshead Bay Brooklyn as Hayling's 1955-60 summer-employment hub — canonical 1950s NYC HBCU-student-summer-work pattern
4. **Judge Charles Mathis** as the canonical FL state circuit judge who heard the St. Augustine movement cases (NOT to be confused with the modern TV judge of the same name) — important for accurate biographical context
5. **Bishop College President Dr. Milton K. Curry Jr.** — the unnamed "president of my school that moved to Dallas, Texas" — useful for biographical accuracy when summarizing Bishop College's relocation history (which serves as background for both Hayling's commencement-speaker MLK and Blake's post-beating recovery facility)
6. **Mass observation of 4+ Whisper renderings of "Hayling"**: Haling, Halein, Halen, Haley, Helen — closer to 5+ unique renderings within the same transcript, reinforcing the cross-corpus pattern

Pass 2 also surfaced several **common-noun Whisper errors** that Pass 1 missed (gougarious → gregarious, aridite → erudite, she-grand/shagran → chagrin, unrequited → unrepentant, leopard/lepathy → leper/leprosy, Orcis Canada → Officer Candidate School recurring, Linda Johnson → Lyndon Johnson). These are useful as canonical Whisper-error-pattern documentation across multiple transcripts.

One important **Pass 1 attribution clarification**: The "President of my school had moved to Dallas, Texas, Bishop College" line is from Hayling's transcript (since Hayling went to Bishop and Bishop moved to Dallas during his lifetime), but the same line appears in Blake's transcript at a different context — the Pass 1 Notes for #102 (Blake) accidentally cross-contaminated with Hayling's recovery context. Hayling's actual recovery was at Brewster Hospital in Jacksonville (correctly per Pass 1 Notes for #103); Blake's actual recovery was at the Dallas Bishop College faculty housing (which Pass 1 Notes for #102 erroneously described as Brewster Hospital). See #102 Pass 2 notes for the corresponding correction request.

The "Channing" Episcopal priest from Daytona reference in Pass 1 Notes for #103 deserves further canonical research — the actual transcript text uses "a piscopal priest out of Daytona" without naming him, suggesting the Pass 1 Notes have additional canonical context the transcript itself does not provide (could be from secondary research, in which case it's fine to keep).
